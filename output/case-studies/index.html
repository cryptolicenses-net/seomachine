License application fees in Switzerland typically range from CHF 5,000 to CHF 25,000 depending on the service type and complexity, with additional costs for legal representation, compliance setup, and ongoing regulatory fees. Our advisory package starts at CHF 15,000 for a complete application with documentation preparation. Annual maintenance and compliance reporting costs generally fall between CHF 8,000 and CHF 20,000 once licensed. Keep in mind that banking relationships and capital requirements may add significant expenses depending on your business model.
You will typically need articles of association, shareholder identification documents, proof of address for all beneficial owners, detailed business plans, risk management policies, anti-money laundering procedures, and corporate governance documentation. The Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority (FINMA) requires demonstration of adequate capitalization and proof of qualified personnel managing compliance. We provide a complete documentation checklist tailored to your specific license type during the initial consultation.
Switzerland remains one of the most crypto-friendly jurisdictions globally, with clear FINMA guidelines established since 2019 and the 2024 updates providing additional clarity on digital asset activities. Compared to jurisdictions like Malta, Liechtenstein, or Singapore, Switzerland offers stronger reputation and banking relationships, though with potentially longer processing timelines of 6-12 months. The cost structure in Switzerland is generally mid-range compared to major financial centers, making it attractive for serious projects seeking regulatory legitimacy in 2026.
Minimum capital requirements in Switzerland are typically CHF 300,000 for smaller operations, though larger exchanges often maintain CHF 1 million to CHF 5 million in liquid assets to demonstrate financial stability. FINMA evaluates capital requirements based on your business volume, customer base size, and market risk exposure. Your bank and FINMA will both review capitalization during the licensing process to ensure you can handle operational losses and customer protection obligations.
If rejected, FINMA provides specific reasons for denial, typically related to inadequate controls, capitalization, or AML procedures. You can remediate issues and reapply, though this extends your timeline by 4-8 months and incurs additional costs. We assess rejection likelihood during pre-application review and help structure applications to minimize this risk, with many clients successfully reapplying within 6 months if initial issues are corrected.
Licensed entities must file quarterly financial reports with FINMA, conduct annual audits by approved auditors, and maintain detailed transaction records for regulatory oversight. You are required to implement ongoing AML/KYC procedures, report suspicious transactions, and maintain current documentation on all beneficial owners and management. Non-compliance can result in fines up to 5% of annual turnover or license revocation, making continuous compliance management essential in 2026.
As a licensed cryptocurrency business, you are subject to standard corporate tax (approximately 12-18% depending on canton), VAT exemption for cryptocurrency exchanges (a significant advantage), and specific reporting of digital assets for beneficial owners. Capital gains on cryptocurrency holdings may be taxable depending on holding periods and business classification. We recommend engaging a Swiss tax advisor specializing in crypto, as cantonal variations and 2026 tax law updates significantly impact your overall tax burden.
