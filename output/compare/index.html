How to read it

Picking the right route

For EU market access, a MiCA CASP authorisation is the single most useful licence: one approval in Estonia, Lithuania, Poland, the Czech Republic or Malta passports across all 27 member states. Lithuania and Poland are the quickest entry points; Malta carries the strongest brand but the longest review.

For speed and tax neutrality, the offshore routes win. Seychelles (2–3 months), St Vincent & the Grenadines (around 90 days) and the British Virgin Islands (2–4 months) combine fast approval with 0% corporate tax, at the cost of EU passporting and easy banking.

For credibility with banks and institutions, Switzerland, the UAE (VARA), Hong Kong and Singapore are the premium tier. Approval takes longer and capital is higher, but these licences open doors that offshore registrations do not.

FAQ

Common questions

Among the jurisdictions compared here, Poland is the quickest (4–8 weeks via MiCA grandfathering), followed by Lithuania (8–12 weeks) and offshore routes such as Seychelles (2–3 months) and St Vincent & the Grenadines (around 90 days). Speed depends on how complete your application file is at submission.
Any MiCA CASP authorisation passports across all 27 EU member states. In this table that means Estonia, Lithuania, Poland, the Czech Republic and Malta. One licence then lets you serve clients EU-wide without separate national approvals.
Several offshore jurisdictions levy 0% corporate tax on crypto activity — Cayman Islands, the British Virgin Islands and Seychelles (offshore income). Panama and El Salvador apply 0% to foreign-source income and crypto capital gains respectively. Labuan and Mauritius sit at roughly 3% effective.
Choose onshore (EU MiCA, Switzerland, UAE, Hong Kong, Singapore) when you need banking access, institutional clients and market credibility. Choose offshore (Seychelles, SVG, BVI, Cayman) when speed, lower cost and tax neutrality matter more than passporting. Many groups run a hybrid: an offshore operating entity plus an onshore licence for regulated markets.
