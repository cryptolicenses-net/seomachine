Overview

Bermuda: The Institutional Crypto Offshore Standard

Bermuda's DABA framework was ground-breaking when enacted in 2018 — it preceded most other jurisdictions' crypto laws and was designed with institutional clients in mind. The BMA, with decades of experience supervising offshore insurance and reinsurance giants, applied the same rigorous approach to digital assets.

The result is a license that is genuinely respected by institutional counterparties, US and European banks, and compliance teams. BMA-licensed entities can demonstrate regulatory substance that lighter-touch jurisdictions cannot match. This makes Bermuda particularly attractive for crypto businesses targeting institutional clients, prime brokerage relationships, or partnerships with traditional financial institutions.

Bermuda has zero corporate tax, no capital gains tax, and strong USD banking through HSBC Bermuda, Clarien Bank, and the Bank of Butterfield. The legal system is English common law with sophisticated local lawyers specializing in both financial services and digital assets.

ICO/Token Framework: Bermuda also has a dedicated Initial Coin Offering framework under the Companies Act (Digital Asset Offering rules). Bermuda was one of the first jurisdictions to provide a legal pathway for regulated token issuances.

Process

DABA Licensing Process

1
Entity Incorporation
Incorporate a Bermuda Exempted Company with a licensed registered agent. Establish the board of directors, appoint a local director (recommended), and set up the corporate governance structure. The BMA requires genuine Bermuda nexus — not a mere letterbox entity.
Weeks 1–3
2
Pre-Application Consultation
Engage the BMA in a pre-application meeting to discuss the business model, proposed activities, and compliance approach. The BMA is responsive to these consultations and provides valuable guidance before a formal application is submitted. Engage local Bermuda counsel experienced with DABA applications.
Weeks 3–6
3
Application Preparation & Filing
Prepare and file the complete DABA application: business plan, financial projections, AML/CFT program, technology documentation, management fit and proper questionnaires, organizational structure, and source of funds. Pay the application fee and submit to the BMA's FinTech division.
Months 2–5
4
BMA Review & Due Diligence
The BMA conducts background checks on all principals, reviews the business plan and risk assessment, assesses capital adequacy, and may request management interviews or supplementary information. Class M applications are reviewed more quickly (3–4 months); Class F takes longer.
Months 4–9
5
License Grant & Operations
Upon BMA approval, receive the DABA license certificate. Inject required capital, complete any conditions precedent, open Bermuda banking accounts, and commence operations. File quarterly and annual regulatory returns; prepare for annual BMA examination cycle.
Months 9–12

FAQ

Bermuda DABA FAQ

The Digital Asset Business Act (DABA) 2018 is Bermuda's comprehensive crypto licensing framework. Administered by the BMA, it establishes a tiered licensing structure for entities conducting digital asset business activities including exchanges, custodians, issuers, and payment processors.
A Class F (Full) license is for established digital asset businesses with full regulatory oversight. A Class M (Modified) license is for businesses seeking a provisional license while developing their full compliance framework. Class M typically applies to startups transitioning to full operations.
Capital requirements vary by license class: Class M minimum is $100,000; Class F ranges from $100,000 to $1,000,000+ depending on activities. Exchanges typically require $500,000+. The BMA assesses capital adequacy against the specific risk profile of the business.
Bermuda has no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax, and no personal income tax. It is one of the world's most favorable tax jurisdictions for financial services businesses. There are no restrictions on repatriation of profits or capital.
Yes. The BMA is widely considered one of the most sophisticated financial regulators in the offshore world. Its crypto regulation (DABA) is substantive and well-respected by institutional counterparties, making a BMA license more credible than many competitor offshore licenses.
The standard application process takes 8-12 weeks from submission to approval, assuming all documentation is complete and no additional information requests from the BMA. Complex applications involving multiple jurisdictions or novel business models may extend to 16-20 weeks. Initial assessment typically occurs within 2-3 weeks of submission.
Major Bermuda banks including Butterfield Bank and Bank of Bermuda maintain crypto-friendly banking relationships with licensed entities, though account minimums typically start at USD 250,000. Many licensed firms also utilize international banking corridors through the BMA's recognized correspondent networks in Singapore, Hong Kong, and the UAE. Some firms opt for blockchain-native banking solutions as secondary accounts to complement traditional banking.
Annual compliance costs typically range from USD 50,000 to USD 150,000 depending on business size and complexity, including audit fees, legal counsel, and regulatory reporting. The annual BMA license fee is approximately USD 3,000-5,000, with additional transaction reporting and AML monitoring costs varying by volume. Larger operations handling over USD 500 million annually should budget USD 200,000+ annually for full compliance infrastructure.
Core documents include a detailed business plan, ownership structure documentation, director and beneficial owner background checks, financial projections for three years, and AML/KYC policies. The BMA also requires proof of fit-and-proper persons status for all officers, details of the proposed technology infrastructure, and evidence of initial capital funding. All documents must be certified by a local Bermuda attorney or auditor.
Bermuda offers faster approval timelines (8-12 weeks vs Malta's 12-16 weeks) and lower initial licensing fees, though Malta provides broader EU market access. Singapore's MAS framework is more prescriptive on tokenization rules but offers larger institutional banking partnerships; Bermuda maintains a lighter regulatory touch with focus on fintech innovation. Bermuda licenses are increasingly preferred for custody and DeFi infrastructure businesses, while Malta and Singapore dominate trading and asset issuance.
Initial failures typically result in a remediation notice with 30-90 days to correct deficiencies, with ongoing supervisory engagement during the cure period. Serious breaches can trigger enhanced supervision status, suspension of new business activities, or license revocation in extreme cases such as AML violations. The BMA publishes enforcement actions for systemic violations, which can significantly impact market reputation and banking relationships.
Bermuda DABA licenses generally cover digital asset custody, exchange services, and broker-dealer activities without specific asset restrictions, allowing operations across major cryptocurrencies and tokens. However, securities tokens fall under additional regulatory frameworks and may require separate permissions from the Bermuda Monetary Authority's securities division. Stablecoin issuance requires explicit authorization and enhanced capital requirements depending on collateral type and redemption mechanisms.
