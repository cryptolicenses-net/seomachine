Overview

Belize: Fast, Low-Cost Offshore Crypto License

Belize has been an offshore financial services center since the 1990s, and the IFSC has extended its regulatory framework to digital assets through the Digital Asset Dealer license. The IFSC's streamlined approach — with 4–8 week approval timelines — makes it one of the fastest offshore crypto licenses available globally.

The jurisdiction uses the Belize Dollar (BZD) pegged 2:1 to the USD, providing monetary stability. Belize operates an English common law legal system (a legacy of British colonization), making it familiar to international businesses. Corporate formalities are straightforward, with no requirement for local directors or shareholders for IBCs.

The main consideration for Belize is banking access: the jurisdiction's historical FATF compliance issues have made correspondent banking cautious. IFSC-licensed entities should plan to use EMI accounts, crypto-friendly payment processors, or banks in other jurisdictions for fiat operations. The license is primarily credible for crypto-to-crypto businesses with minimal fiat banking needs.

Banking Note: Belize-licensed crypto businesses may face challenges opening bank accounts with major correspondent banks. Consider supplementing with EMI accounts or USD accounts in other jurisdictions. Our team can assist with banking strategy as part of the licensing process.

FAQ

Belize Crypto License FAQ

The International Financial Services Commission (IFSC) of Belize is the regulator for international financial services businesses including digital asset dealers. The IFSC issues Digital Asset Dealer licenses under the IFSC Act and accompanying Digital Asset Regulations.
The IFSC typically processes Digital Asset Dealer license applications within 4–8 weeks for complete applications. This is one of the fastest regulatory approvals in the Caribbean, making Belize ideal for businesses needing to get operational quickly.
Belize IBCs conducting international business pay zero Belize corporate tax on foreign-source income. There are no withholding taxes, no capital gains taxes, and no inheritance taxes. The only taxes applicable are minimal annual fees to the IFSC and company registry.
The IFSC requires a minimum paid-up capital of USD $100,000 for a Digital Asset Dealer license. This must be maintained during the license period. Overall licensing costs are among the lowest in the region.
Belize has historically had FATF compliance challenges. Businesses should verify current FATF status. Banking access can be more challenging for Belize-licensed entities versus more FATF-compliant jurisdictions like Barbados or Bermuda. The license is most suitable for crypto-to-crypto businesses with minimal fiat banking requirements.
The application fee for a Belize IFSC Digital Asset License ranges from USD 5,000 to USD 15,000 depending on the license class, with additional costs for legal documentation, incorporation, and compliance setup typically totaling USD 8,000 to USD 20,000. Annual renewal fees are approximately USD 2,000 to USD 5,000. Our advisory firm can provide a detailed cost breakdown based on your specific business model during the initial consultation.
A Belize IFSC Digital Asset License provides access to correspondent banking relationships with institutions in jurisdictions including the UAE, Singapore, and Eastern Europe, though direct banking partnerships have become more restrictive since 2024. Most licensees utilize payment processors and stablecoin settlement networks rather than traditional banking channels. We recommend establishing banking relationships prior to license issuance, as post-licensing options are limited.
Required documents include a detailed business plan, source of funds declaration, proof of identity and address for all beneficial owners, AML/KYC policies, technical infrastructure specifications, and audited financial statements if applicable. The IFSC also requires organizational documents, proof of office address in Belmopan, and evidence of compliance with local anti-money laundering regulations. Complete documentation submission typically accelerates approval by 4-6 weeks.
Belize offers faster approval timelines (90-180 days versus 6-12 months) and lower compliance costs, but Malta and Cyprus provide stronger international recognition and access to EU banking infrastructure. Belize is ideal for businesses prioritizing cost-efficiency and speed, while Malta and Cyprus suit firms requiring European regulatory credibility and banking relationships. Each jurisdiction has distinct advantages depending on your target market and operational model.
License holders must maintain quarterly compliance reporting, annual financial audits, ongoing AML/KYC procedures for all clients, and regular updates to the IFSC on changes to beneficial ownership or business operations. The Belize IFSC conducts periodic compliance reviews and may request additional documentation within 30-60 days notice. Failure to maintain compliance standards can result in license suspension or revocation.
Licenses are automatically renewable annually provided you remain compliant with IFSC regulations and pay the renewal fee by the specified deadline. However, material changes to your business structure, beneficial ownership, or operations may trigger a review process before renewal is granted. We recommend submitting renewal documentation 60 days before expiration to avoid service interruption.
The main risks include potential international pressure on Belize regarding crypto regulation, increased FATF scrutiny on license holder activities, and the possibility of banking relationship restrictions if your jurisdiction of operation changes. Additionally, Belize has limited regulatory history compared to established crypto hubs, which may affect client perception and institutional partnerships. Diversifying your jurisdictional licensing strategy can mitigate these risks effectively.
