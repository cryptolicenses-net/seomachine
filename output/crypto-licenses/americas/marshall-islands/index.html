Overview

World's First DAO LLC Jurisdiction

In 2022, the Republic of the Marshall Islands (RMI) made history by becoming the first sovereign nation to legally recognize DAOs through the Non-Profit Entities (Amendment) Act 2022. This legislation allows DAOs to register as Limited Liability Companies with the RMI Registrar, gaining legal personality, limited liability for members, and the ability to function as recognized legal entities globally.

The DAO LLC structure is particularly relevant for DeFi protocols, Web3 projects, and decentralized exchanges that cannot easily fit into traditional corporate structures. Members can be identified by blockchain addresses rather than legal names, governance can be embedded in smart contracts, and the LLC agreement can incorporate on-chain voting mechanisms.

For traditional VASPs, the MIDAX framework provides a streamlined registration path with zero tax, USD currency, and minimal bureaucratic requirements. The jurisdiction is particularly popular with US-connected founders who want offshore structure without OECD complexity but with USD as native currency.

US Person Warning: US citizens and residents using Marshall Islands entities must still comply with US tax obligations (FBAR, FATCA, Subpart F, PFIC, CFC rules). The RMI's USD usage and Compact of Free Association with the US means US founders face specific tax reporting requirements. Always obtain US tax counsel.

Process

Marshall Islands Registration Process

1
Select Entity Type & Structure
Determine whether a DAO LLC or traditional VASP structure best fits your project. For DeFi/Web3 projects, the DAO LLC is typically preferred. For traditional exchanges, a standard LLC or Corporation with VASP registration may be more appropriate. Engage counsel with RMI and crypto expertise.
Days 1–5
2
Prepare LLC Agreement / Corporate Documents
For DAO LLC: draft an LLC Agreement that incorporates smart contract governance provisions, defines membership rights by blockchain address, and sets governance rules. For VASP: prepare corporate articles, operating agreement, and AML program documentation.
Weeks 1–2
3
RMI Registrar Filing
File the entity registration documents with the RMI Registrar through the licensed registered agent. Pay government fees. For DAO LLCs, the unique DAO provisions must be properly included in the filing. Registration is typically processed within 5–10 business days.
Weeks 2–3
4
MIDAX VASP Registration (if applicable)
For entities conducting regulated VASP activities, register with MIDAX. Submit AML/CFT program documentation, ownership information, business description, and proposed activities. MIDAX reviews and approves VASP registrations — typically 1–3 weeks for complete applications.
Weeks 3–5
5
Launch & Ongoing Compliance
Deploy smart contracts, begin operations, or launch the exchange. Maintain the annual filing with the RMI Registrar, comply with MIDAX reporting requirements for VASPs, and ensure ongoing AML/CFT compliance. Update the Registrar of material membership or governance changes.
Week 5–6 onward

Marshall Islands Crypto License FAQ

The Marshall Islands DAO LLC is the world's first legally recognized Decentralized Autonomous Organization structure. It allows DAOs to register as legal entities with limited liability for members, smart contract governance, and the ability to hold assets, enter contracts, and sue or be sued.

This means there is no exchange rate risk and USD transactions are natively supported. However, the Marshall Islands does NOT have access to US regulatory systems or the Federal Reserve.
MIDAX refers to the Marshall Islands' digital asset regulatory framework. It accommodates traditional VASPs and innovative Web3 structures including DAOs. The RMI Registrar administers entity formations; MIDAX handles regulatory compliance for digital asset businesses conducting client-facing operations.
There is no specified minimum capital requirement for DAO LLC registration or basic VASP registration. The low barrier makes the Marshall Islands attractive for early-stage Web3 projects and DAOs. Adequate working capital for operations is still needed practically.
The Marshall Islands has no corporate income tax, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax, and no inheritance tax. International business income is not taxed. Combined with USD as official currency, this makes it uniquely positioned for USD-denominated crypto businesses. US persons must still comply with US tax reporting requirements.
The initial application fee ranges from $5,000 to $15,000 USD depending on the license type and complexity of your business model. Annual renewal fees typically cost $2,000 to $5,000 USD, with additional compliance and legal fees potentially adding $3,000 to $10,000 during the first year. Total first-year costs generally fall between $10,000 and $30,000 USD when accounting for professional advisory services.
The standard processing timeline is 8 to 12 weeks from submission of a complete application to the Marshall Islands Financial Services Authority. Applications with missing documentation or requiring additional clarification can extend this timeline to 16 to 20 weeks. Expedited review options are available in some cases for an additional fee, potentially reducing approval time to 4 to 6 weeks.
Required documents include business plans detailing your crypto operations, proof of ownership structure, AML/KYC policies, financial projections for at least three years, and compliance officer credentials. You must also provide background checks for all directors and beneficial owners, proof of registered office in the Marshall Islands, and detailed risk management procedures. Banking references and proof of initial capitalization are typically required to demonstrate financial stability.
The Marshall Islands regulatory framework permits multiple business models including cryptocurrency exchanges, custody providers, payment processors, and blockchain infrastructure services. However, your license type and permitted activities are clearly defined in your authorization document, and operating outside these parameters constitutes a breach of your license. You must apply for a separate license or amendment if you wish to expand into additional service categories.
Many traditional banks have de-risked relationships with crypto companies, making it difficult to open business accounts even with a valid Marshall Islands license. However, several jurisdictions including Singapore, the UAE, and Malta have banking partnerships specifically designed for Marshall Islands-licensed crypto firms. It is advisable to establish banking relationships before applying for your license or work with a corporate services provider that offers banking facilitation as part of their package.
The Marshall Islands license is significantly faster and less expensive to obtain than Swiss or Maltese licenses, with lower ongoing compliance costs and simpler capital requirements. However, Swiss and Malta licenses typically provide stronger international recognition and easier access to banking relationships with major financial institutions. The Marshall Islands license is ideal for startups prioritizing speed to market, while Switzerland and Malta suit established firms seeking maximum regulatory prestige and banking convenience.
Licenses must be renewed annually, typically 30 days before expiration, or you risk operating without valid authorization. If you miss the renewal deadline, the Marshall Islands Financial Services Authority may impose penalties ranging from $1,000 to $5,000 USD and temporarily suspend your license pending compliance. Operating during a suspension period could result in fines up to $50,000 USD and potential license revocation, making timely renewal critical for business continuity.
