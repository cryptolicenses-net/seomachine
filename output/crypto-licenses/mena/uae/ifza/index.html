Overview

What Does IFZA Stand For? Full Guide to IFZA Crypto Licensing

IFZA stands for International Free Zone Authority — a UAE federal free zone authority headquartered in Dubai Silicon Oasis (DSO), one of Dubai's technology-focused districts. IFZA was established in partnership with the Dubai Silicon Oasis Authority and has grown rapidly into one of the UAE's most popular free zones for international entrepreneurs and technology companies.

IFZA is particularly well-suited for crypto startups, blockchain developers, NFT platforms, Web3 companies, and crypto service providers that need a legitimate UAE corporate structure and a recognised free zone licence — without the higher capital and cost requirements of DMCC, ADGM, or DIFC.

Unlike VARA or ADGM, IFZA itself is not a financial services regulator — it issues trade licences for business activities conducted within the free zone. Companies using IFZA for regulated financial crypto activities (e.g., running a crypto exchange serving UAE retail clients) would need to obtain VARA authorisation in addition to the IFZA licence.

Requirements

IFZA Crypto Licence Requirements

IFZA has some of the most accessible requirements of any UAE free zone — making it a strong starting point for crypto entrepreneurs.

Share Capital

Nominal minimum share capital of AED 1,000 for a Free Zone Company (FZ-LLC). In practice, virtual asset businesses should maintain operational working capital appropriate to their activities — typically AED 500,000–1,000,000.

Office Space

IFZA offers various office options including flexi-desk, smart desk, and dedicated office. Flexi-desk (shared workspace with mailing address) is the most affordable option, starting from approximately AED 6,000/year.

Shareholders & Directors

Minimum 1 shareholder (can be a foreign individual or company), 1 director (can be the same person as the shareholder). 100% foreign ownership permitted. No UAE national sponsor required.

AML/KYC Policy

For virtual asset activities, an AML/CFT policy is required. Must comply with UAE Federal AML Law No. 20 of 2018. A designated Compliance Officer or MLRO should be appointed.

Business Plan

A description of the business activities, target market, and operational model. IFZA requirements are lighter than VARA or ADGM — a concise business plan is typically sufficient.

KYC Documents

Passport copies of all shareholders and directors, proof of address, bank reference letters, and source of funds declaration. Corporate shareholders require additional documentation (Certificate of Incorporation, etc.).

How to Get an IFZA Crypto Licence — Step by Step

1

Select Activities & Apply Online

Choose your virtual asset activities from the IFZA activity list and submit an online application. IFZA's digital portal makes the initial application fast and paperless. Name approval is typically within 24–48 hours.

1–2 days
2

Document Submission & KYC

Submit shareholder/director KYC documents, passport copies, proof of address, and source of funds. For virtual asset activities, an AML policy outline may be required at this stage.

1–2 weeks
3

Licence Payment & Issuance

Pay the IFZA licence fee. The licence is typically issued within 2–5 business days after payment and document approval. IFZA issues both digital and physical licence certificates.

3–5 days
4

Office Setup & Visa Processing

Activate your flexi-desk or office arrangement. Apply for UAE residency visas for directors and employees. IFZA visa quota is typically 1–6 visas depending on office type.

2–4 weeks
5

Bank Account Opening

Open a UAE corporate bank account. This is the most time-consuming step for crypto businesses — UAE banks are cautious with virtual asset companies. Budget 4–8 weeks and consider neobank or fintech account options as alternatives.

4–8 weeks
