Overview

What Is RAK DAO? The World's First Web3 Free Zone

RAK DAO (Ras Al Khaimah Digital Assets Oasis) was established in 2023 by the Government of Ras Al Khaimah — one of the seven emirates of the UAE — as a purpose-built free zone for the digital asset and Web3 economy.

What makes RAK DAO unique globally is its DAO legal framework — it is the first jurisdiction in the world to provide legal recognition for Decentralized Autonomous Organizations as formal legal entities. DAOs registered at RAK DAO can hold assets, enter contracts, open bank accounts, and operate in the traditional economy while maintaining their decentralized governance structure.

Beyond DAOs, RAK DAO serves the full Web3 ecosystem: DeFi protocols, NFT marketplaces, blockchain gaming studios, crypto exchanges, metaverse companies, and virtual asset service providers (VASPs). Its simplified licensing process, low costs, and UAE credibility make it one of the fastest-growing destinations for global Web3 founders.

RAK DAO is different from VARA, ADGM, and DIFC

RAK DAO is a free zone licensing authority — not a financial services regulator. For regulated financial services (retail crypto exchange, investment management, custody), VARA or ADGM/DIFC authorization is required in addition. RAK DAO is ideal as a corporate base for Web3 product companies, protocol developers, and DAO organizations.

Requirements

RAK DAO License Requirements

RAK DAO has some of the most accessible requirements in the UAE — designed to attract global Web3 talent and startups.

Share Capital

No mandatory minimum share capital for standard RAK DAO companies. Web3 businesses are expected to demonstrate sufficient working capital for their operations, but there is no prescriptive regulatory capital requirement.

Physical Office

Flexi-desk and virtual office options are available at RAK DAO — one of the few UAE free zones accepting virtual office setups. This significantly reduces overhead for remote-first Web3 teams.

Shareholders & Directors

100% foreign ownership permitted. Minimum 1 shareholder and 1 director. Can be the same person. No UAE national sponsor required. DAOs can have token-holder governance structures recognized within the framework.

AML/KYC

Basic AML/KYC policies are required for virtual asset activities. Requirements are lighter than VARA, ADGM, or DIFC. A compliance policy covering customer identification and transaction monitoring is expected.

Business Description

A brief description of business activities and structure. RAK DAO's application process is streamlined and largely digital — no lengthy regulatory business plan required for standard activities.

KYC Documents

Passport copies of shareholders and directors, proof of address, and source of funds. For DAO registration, governance token documentation and smart contract details may be requested.
