Overview

What Is the DAFZA Crypto License?

DAFZA (Dubai Airport Free Zone Authority) is a UAE government-owned free zone established in 1996, operating directly adjacent to Dubai International Airport — one of the world's busiest aviation hubs. It is the largest airport free zone in the Middle East.

A DAFZA crypto license involves two complementary layers of regulation: first, incorporating a company under the DAFZA Authority to obtain a Trading, Service, or Industrial License; and second, obtaining a VARA (Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority) license or VARA MVP (Minimum Viable Product) approval to conduct regulated virtual asset activities. This dual-layer structure is the standard model for virtual asset businesses operating in Dubai's free zones.

DAFZA is particularly well-suited to businesses with physical logistics requirements — such as crypto hardware manufacturers, mining equipment distributors, and blockchain infrastructure providers — because of its direct airport connectivity. It is also favoured by financial services firms, global crypto exchanges, and Web3 companies seeking a prestigious Dubai address alongside comprehensive VA regulatory coverage.

DAFZA + VARA: The Two-Layer Licensing Model

DAFZA provides the corporate and free zone entity; VARA provides the virtual asset regulatory authorisation. A DAFZA trade license alone does not permit regulated VA activities — VARA licensing (MVP or full) is mandatory for exchanges, custodians, brokers, and other VA service providers targeting Dubai and UAE markets.

Since VARA was established by Dubai Law No. 4 of 2022, it has become the primary licensing authority for virtual asset service providers (VASPs) operating in — or from — Dubai. DAFZA-incorporated entities can apply to VARA for the relevant activity-based license, making DAFZA one of several eligible free zone jurisdictions within Dubai's VARA framework.

Legal Framework

Regulatory Framework for DAFZA Virtual Asset Businesses

The legal and regulatory structure governing virtual asset businesses in DAFZA draws from multiple layers of UAE and Dubai law.

DAFZA Free Zone Law

DAFZA operates under UAE Federal Decree No. 22 of 2014 and subsequent amendments. Companies incorporated in DAFZA are governed by DAFZA's own company regulations, separate from UAE mainland company law.

Dubai Law No. 4 of 2022 (VARA)

Established the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority as the independent regulator for all virtual asset activities conducted in or from Dubai (excluding ADGM and DIFC). All VA service providers must be VARA-licensed.

UAE Federal AML Law No. 20 of 2018

All UAE virtual asset service providers are designated non-financial businesses and professions (DNFBPs) subject to comprehensive AML/CFT obligations. Compliance with FATF Recommendation 16 (Travel Rule) is required.

UAE Corporate Tax (CT) Law

Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022 introduced a 9% corporate tax rate, effective June 2023. However, qualifying free zone income — including most DAFZA-based businesses with adequate substance — remains at 0%.

VARA Rulebook (2023)

VARA's comprehensive rulebook covers seven VA activity categories: broker-dealers, custodians, exchanges, lending & borrowing, payment/remittance, management & investment, and advisory services. Each has distinct capital and compliance requirements.

Central Bank of UAE

The UAE Central Bank oversees payment-related aspects of virtual asset services, including crypto payment gateways and stablecoin issuance. Payment service providers may require dual CBUAE/VARA authorisation.

License Types & Activities

DAFZA License Types and VA Activities

DAFZA issues three main license types, each of which can accommodate virtual asset-related business activities when paired with appropriate VARA authorisation.

Trading License

MOST COMMON FOR VA FIRMS

Covers buying and selling of virtual assets, operating crypto exchanges and OTC desks, and distribution of blockchain-related products. Paired with a VARA Exchange or Broker-Dealer license for retail/institutional activity.

Service License

ADVISORY & TECH FIRMS

Covers blockchain technology services, virtual asset advisory, AML/compliance consultancy, DeFi protocol development, smart contract auditing, and other professional services related to the virtual asset sector.

Industrial License

HARDWARE & MINING

Covers manufacturing, assembly, and distribution of crypto mining hardware, ASICs, GPU rigs, and blockchain infrastructure equipment. Benefits directly from DAFZA's airport logistics connectivity and customs facilities.

VARA MVP License

INITIAL VA REGULATORY STEP

The Minimum Viable Product license from VARA allows early-stage virtual asset service providers to operate in a sandboxed environment while working towards a full VARA license. Typically 3–6 months to obtain.

VARA Full License (7 Categories)

FULL REGULATORY AUTHORISATION

Full VARA licenses cover Exchange Services, Broker-Dealer Services, Custody Services, Lending & Borrowing, Management & Investment, Payment & Remittance, and Advisory Services. Each has bespoke capital, compliance, and governance requirements.

Crypto Hardware Logistics

DAFZA UNIQUE ADVANTAGE

DAFZA's direct integration with DXB Airport cargo facilities makes it the optimal UAE free zone for importers, distributors, and custodians of physical crypto infrastructure — mining rigs, HSMs, cold storage hardware, and related equipment.

Requirements

DAFZA Crypto License Requirements

Meeting the requirements for a DAFZA virtual asset company involves satisfying both DAFZA's corporate formation criteria and VARA's activity-based regulatory standards.

Minimum Capital (DAFZA entity)

There is no statutory minimum share capital for DAFZA company formation — capital is set per the articles of association. However, VARA imposes activity-specific minimum capital requirements ranging from AED 300,000 for advisory services to AED 2,000,000 or more for exchange and custody activities.

Physical Office in DAFZA

A physical office within the DAFZA free zone is mandatory. DAFZA offers executive suites, serviced offices, and warehouse-office combinations suited to hardware-focused businesses. Flexi-desk options may be available but dedicated office space is strongly preferred for VARA applications.

Shareholders & Directors

100% foreign ownership is permitted with no requirement for a UAE national shareholder or local sponsor. A minimum of one director is required; directors do not need to be UAE residents. Corporate shareholders are accepted.

AML/CFT Compliance Programme

A comprehensive AML/CFT policy, customer due diligence (CDD) procedures, transaction monitoring system, and Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR) framework must be in place. A qualified Compliance Officer (MLRO) must be appointed before VARA licensing commences.

Business Plan & Financial Projections

A detailed business plan covering VA activities, target markets, revenue model, organisational chart, and 3-year financial projections. VARA requires evidence of technical and operational readiness, including IT security, custody arrangements, and liquidity management.

Fit & Proper Assessment

All UBOs (ultimate beneficial owners), shareholders holding 10%+, and directors must pass a fit and proper assessment. This includes criminal background checks, professional references, source of funds/wealth documentation, and regulatory history disclosures across all jurisdictions of operation.

Technology & Cybersecurity

VARA requires virtual asset service providers to demonstrate robust IT infrastructure, penetration testing results, cybersecurity policies, and data protection frameworks compliant with UAE PDPL (Personal Data Protection Law). Third-party security audits are recommended.

Proof of Banking Arrangements

VARA expects applicants to have or be in advanced discussions for a UAE corporate bank account. Crypto-friendly banks in the UAE include Emirates NBD, Mashreq, RAKBANK, and First Abu Dhabi Bank (FAB). Bank account opening for VA firms typically takes 4–10 weeks.

Process

How to Get a DAFZA Crypto License — Step by Step

The DAFZA licensing process runs in parallel with VARA licensing. Total end-to-end timeline is typically 4–8 months depending on VARA activity category and complexity.

1

Pre-Application: Jurisdiction and Activity Selection

Confirm DAFZA is the right free zone for your business model — particularly relevant if you have logistics, hardware, or airport-proximity requirements. Identify the VARA activity category (Exchange, Broker-Dealer, Custody, etc.) and assess whether MVP or full VARA licensing is appropriate for your stage of development.

Week 1–2
2

DAFZA Application: Company Name & License Activity

Submit initial application to DAFZA Authority — company name reservation, license activity selection (Trading, Service, or Industrial), and initial shareholder/director documentation. DAFZA typically issues initial approval within 5–10 business days.

Week 2–4
3

Company Incorporation & Office Lease

Execute the Memorandum and Articles of Association (M&AA), register the entity with DAFZA, and execute a lease agreement for a physical office within the free zone. Obtain trade license. This stage formally establishes the DAFZA legal entity.

Week 3–6
4

VARA Pre-Application & Compliance Documentation

Prepare and submit VARA pre-application via VARA's online portal. Required documents include business plan, financial projections, organisational structure, AML/CFT policy manual, technology architecture, and UBO/director fit and proper declarations. VARA typically acknowledges receipt within 30 days.

Week 4–8
5

VARA Review, Q&A, and Due Diligence

VARA conducts detailed review of all submitted documentation, may request clarifications, additional evidence, or interviews with key personnel. This is typically the longest stage — VARA's review process for full licenses can take 8–16 weeks. MVP applications are reviewed faster (4–8 weeks).

Week 8–20
6

Bank Account Opening

In parallel with VARA review, initiate corporate bank account opening with a UAE bank. Emirates NBD, Mashreq, RAKBANK, and FAB are among the most crypto-friendly institutions. Bank account opening for VA firms requires extensive KYC documentation and takes 4–10 weeks.

Week 6–16 (parallel)
7

VARA License Issuance & Operational Launch

Upon VARA approval, the virtual asset activity license is issued. The DAFZA company can commence regulated VA operations. Ongoing obligations include annual license renewal, periodic VARA reporting, AML/CFT audits, and UAE CT compliance.

Month 4–8 (total)

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions — DAFZA Crypto License

A DAFZA crypto license involves two steps: first, incorporating a company under the Dubai Airport Free Zone Authority (DAFZA) and obtaining a Trading, Service, or Industrial license; and second, applying to VARA (Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority) for the specific virtual asset activity license needed for your business (e.g., Exchange, Broker-Dealer, Custody). The DAFZA entity is your legal operating company; the VARA license provides regulatory authorisation for virtual asset activities. Both layers are required for regulated VA operations in Dubai.
The DAFZA company formation itself takes 4–8 weeks from initial application to trade license issuance. The VARA licensing process runs in parallel or sequentially and typically takes an additional 3–6 months for an MVP license, or longer for a full VARA license depending on the activity category (exchanges and custodians tend to take longer due to more stringent requirements). Total end-to-end time is typically 4–8 months for most businesses.
DAFZA is the best choice when airport proximity is a genuine business requirement — for example, if your business involves importing, distributing, or providing custody of physical crypto hardware (mining rigs, HSMs, cold storage devices), or if you have significant international logistics needs. The direct connection to Dubai International Airport (DXB), the world's busiest international airport, provides unmatched import/export efficiency. For software-only crypto businesses without logistics requirements, DMCC or IFZA may offer a more cost-effective entry point.
DAFZA itself does not impose a statutory minimum share capital for company formation. The capital requirements come from VARA: the minimum ranges from approximately AED 300,000 for advisory and lower-risk activities up to AED 2,000,000 or more for exchange services, custody, and lending/borrowing. Actual operational capital requirements may be higher depending on VARA's assessment of your business model and risk profile. Capital must be verifiable and available in a UAE bank account.
DAFZA companies benefit from 0% corporate tax on qualifying free zone income under the UAE's free zone regime (subject to maintaining adequate economic substance in the UAE under the 2023 UAE CT Law). There is 0% personal income tax in the UAE, 0% capital gains tax, and 100% repatriation of profits and capital. The UAE has no withholding tax on dividends or royalties. To maintain the 0% qualifying income status, DAFZA entities must satisfy the economic substance test — meaning real operations and personnel in the UAE, not merely a letterbox address.
The application fee for a DAFZA crypto license typically ranges from AED 15,000 to AED 50,000 depending on the specific license category and business model. Additional costs may include legal fees (AED 10,000-20,000), corporate setup, and annual compliance fees of approximately AED 5,000-10,000. Total initial investment usually falls between AED 30,000 and AED 80,000.
The standard approval timeline for a DAFZA crypto license is typically 8-12 weeks from submission of a complete application. If your documentation is incomplete or requires clarifications from the Dubai Financial Services Authority, this process can extend to 16-20 weeks. Expedited processing options may be available for an additional fee.
Minimum capital requirements vary by license type, but typically range from AED 500,000 to AED 2,000,000 for crypto trading and exchange platforms. Investment firms and custodians may require higher capital reserves. These funds must be held in a bank account in the DAFZA zone and be immediately available for regulatory inspection.
A DAFZA license requires you to establish a physical office within the Dubai Financial Services Authority zone with local management and staff present. While you can have international operations and clients, your primary business operations and decision-making must be conducted from your DAFZA office. Remote-only operations from outside the UAE are not permitted.
DAFZA offers a 50-year corporate tax exemption on profits generated within the zone for eligible businesses, though this may be subject to changes in UAE federal tax legislation through 2026. However, businesses are subject to standard DAFZA fees and must comply with international tax reporting requirements like FATCA and CRS. It is recommended to consult with a tax advisor regarding your specific jurisdiction's tax obligations.
Banking access for crypto businesses has become increasingly restricted, but several banks operating in DAFZA such as FAB, Emirates NBD, and specialized banking partners still work with licensed crypto firms on a case-by-case basis. You should expect enhanced due diligence procedures and higher fees compared to traditional businesses. We recommend engaging banking relationships during the licensing process to avoid delays after approval.
A DAFZA license typically takes 8-12 weeks and costs AED 30,000-80,000, while Malta licenses take 6-10 weeks with costs of EUR 5,000-15,000, and Gibraltar licenses take 4-8 weeks costing GBP 3,000-10,000. However, DAFZA offers stronger regulatory recognition in Middle Eastern and Asian markets, better tax incentives, and proximity to high-growth cryptocurrency markets in the region. The choice depends on your target client base and operational priorities.
