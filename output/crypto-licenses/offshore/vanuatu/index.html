Overview

Vanuatu's Established Offshore Crypto Framework

Vanuatu has been a recognised offshore financial centre for decades, and its crypto licensing regime is one of the most established in the Pacific offshore space. The Vanuatu Financial Services Commission (VFSC) issues Digital Asset Dealer licenses under the Financial Dealers Licensing Act, supplemented by specific digital asset regulation that aligns Vanuatu's framework with FATF recommendations.

The VFSC Digital Asset Dealer license authorises the holder to exchange, deal in, and facilitate transfers of digital assets on behalf of clients. The license is internationally recognised and accepted by many banking partners, payment processors, and institutional counterparties as a credible regulatory credential for a crypto business operating from an offshore jurisdiction.

Vanuatu's tax environment is exceptional for international businesses. There is no income tax, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax, and no inheritance tax. Vanuatu IBC (International Business Company) structures operating outside Vanuatu pay zero Vanuatu tax on foreign-sourced profits. This makes the total tax cost of operating from Vanuatu extremely low for properly structured international operations.

The VFSC is proactive in maintaining Vanuatu's reputation as a quality offshore jurisdiction. It conducts ongoing supervision of licensees, enforces AML/CFT standards, and has removed operators who have failed to maintain compliance. This enforcement activity supports the credibility of the license and differentiates Vanuatu from purely nominal licensing jurisdictions.

Banking Access Consideration

As with all offshore crypto licenses, banking access for Vanuatu-licensed entities requires careful planning. Many major international banks remain cautious about offshore crypto businesses, and applicants should work with advisors who have established relationships with Vanuatu-friendly banking partners. EMI accounts and crypto-native payment processors are commonly used alongside traditional banking.

Process

Obtaining Your Vanuatu Digital Asset Dealer License

Vanuatu's licensing process is streamlined and can be completed entirely remotely. An experienced VFSC-licensed agent can prepare and file the full application without the applicant needing to travel to Vanuatu.

1
Engage VFSC-Licensed Registered Agent
Select a VFSC-licensed registered agent in Vanuatu (Port Vila). The agent handles local company incorporation, maintains the registered office, liaises with the VFSC on your behalf, and prepares the application documentation. This is a critical first step as the agent's quality significantly affects application success and speed. Our network includes vetted Vanuatu agents.
1 week
2
Incorporate Vanuatu IBC and Deposit Capital
Incorporate a Vanuatu International Business Company (IBC) through your registered agent. Establish the company's memorandum and articles of association authorising digital asset dealing activities. Appoint directors and shareholders, and deposit the USD 50,000 minimum capital. Capital must be paid up and evidenced at the time of application. IBC formation in Vanuatu is typically completed in 1–2 weeks.
1–2 weeks
3
Prepare Application Documentation
Compile the VFSC Digital Asset Dealer application package: certified corporate documents, director and shareholder KYC (passport copies, proof of address, CVs, background check certificates), AML/CFT policy manual, business description, source of funds documentation for capital, and technology/security overview. All documents must be apostilled or notarised where required. We provide template AML manuals tailored to VFSC requirements.
1–2 weeks
4
Submit Application to VFSC
Your registered agent submits the complete application to the VFSC along with the applicable license fee. VFSC reviews the application and conducts fit-and-proper assessments of all directors and significant shareholders. The VFSC may request clarifications or additional documentation. For complete, well-prepared applications, the review period is typically 2–4 weeks.
2–4 weeks VFSC review
5
Receive License and Commence Operations
Upon VFSC approval, the Digital Asset Dealer license is issued and published in the VFSC's public register of licensed entities. The license certificate is provided to your registered agent. You may now operate as a VFSC-licensed digital asset dealer. Display the license number and VFSC registration on your website and promotional materials. Annual license renewal fees apply. Maintain ongoing AML/CFT compliance and file annual returns.
1 week

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

The Vanuatu Financial Services Commission (VFSC) Digital Asset Dealer license is issued under Vanuatu's Financial Dealers Licensing Act and associated digital asset regulations. It authorises the holder to deal in, exchange, and facilitate transfers of digital assets for clients. The license is published in the VFSC's public register and is accepted by many banking partners, payment processors, and institutional counterparties as a credible offshore regulatory credential.
Vanuatu is one of the fastest crypto licensing jurisdictions in the world. A complete, well-prepared application can be processed by the VFSC in 4–8 weeks from submission. Experienced Vanuatu registered agents can prepare and file a complete application package within 1–2 weeks. The entire process from initial engagement to license receipt can be completed in as little as 5–6 weeks for applicants with all required documentation ready.
Vanuatu has no income tax, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax, and no corporate tax on foreign-sourced income. A Vanuatu IBC licensed as a Digital Asset Dealer and operating outside Vanuatu pays zero Vanuatu tax on its profits from international activities. This makes Vanuatu one of the most tax-efficient offshore crypto licensing jurisdictions. Note that operators remain subject to tax obligations in their country of residence, management, and control, and should obtain specialist tax advice for their specific situation.
The Vanuatu VFSC requires a minimum paid-up capital of USD 50,000 for a Digital Asset Dealer license. This capital must be verifiably held by the applicant entity and documented in the application with supporting bank statements or equivalent evidence. The capital remains the company's working capital and is not a fee paid to the VFSC — it is the company's own funds demonstrating its financial capacity to operate.
Vanuatu VFSC licensees must comply with Vanuatu's Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorism Financing Act, VFSC AML guidelines, and FATF Recommendation 15 on VASPs. Specific obligations include: implementing a documented AML/CFT program with risk assessment; conducting customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence for higher-risk customers; maintaining transaction monitoring systems; filing suspicious transaction reports (STRs) with Vanuatu's Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU); maintaining records for a minimum of 7 years; and providing annual compliance reporting to the VFSC.
Initial application and licensing fees typically range from USD 5,000 to USD 15,000 depending on the scope of operations, with annual renewal fees of approximately USD 2,000 to USD 5,000. Additional costs include compliance officer fees, AML software subscriptions, and audit expenses, which can total USD 10,000 to USD 30,000 annually. These figures are subject to VFSC fee schedules updated periodically, so confirmation with current regulatory guidelines is essential.
Vanuatu offers faster approval timelines (30-60 days versus 3-6 months in Malta) and lower initial costs, making it attractive for startups, though Malta provides stronger EU market access and more established banking relationships. The Cayman Islands offers superior institutional credibility and asset management capabilities, while Vanuatu's lighter-touch approach appeals to businesses prioritizing speed and cost efficiency. The choice depends on your target market, client base, and long-term regulatory positioning strategy.
Vanuatu crypto licensees face significant banking challenges, as major international banks remain reluctant to serve crypto-related entities due to regulatory risk. However, some specialized banks in Singapore, Hong Kong, and the UAE have begun accepting Vanuatu VFSC licensees with enhanced due diligence. We recommend establishing banking relationships before or during the licensing process, as this is often the most time-consuming operational hurdle.
Applications require company incorporation documents, beneficial ownership declarations, director and shareholder identification, proof of office address in Vanuatu, a detailed business plan, AML/CFT compliance policies, and evidence of compliance officer appointment with relevant qualifications. Additionally, applicants must provide financial statements or proof of minimum capital, professional references, and detailed organizational charts. The VFSC may request additional documentation based on the nature and scale of proposed operations.
The VFSC can issue warning letters, impose monetary penalties ranging from USD 5,000 to USD 50,000, or suspend/revoke the license depending on violation severity. Serious breaches such as failure to report suspicious transactions or inadequate KYC procedures can result in criminal referrals and personal liability for compliance officers. Most enforcement actions include mandatory remediation periods and mandatory retraining, with continued non-compliance leading to permanent deregistration.
The VFSC does not maintain a prohibited asset list, allowing licensed dealers to offer most cryptocurrencies and tokens, though they must implement appropriate risk assessment and customer suitability controls. However, dealers should avoid assets linked to sanctions violations, known fraudulent schemes, or unregistered securities under applicable international standards. The VFSC expects licensees to conduct independent due diligence on any novel or emerging tokens before offering them to clients.
Vanuatu VFSC Digital Asset Dealer licenses require annual renewal, typically completed 60 days before the expiration date through submission of updated compliance reports, financial statements, and beneficial ownership confirmations. The renewal process usually takes 10-15 business days for straightforward applications with no compliance issues. Failure to renew on time results in automatic suspension, and reapplication may be required rather than simple renewal, making timely submission critical for business continuity.
