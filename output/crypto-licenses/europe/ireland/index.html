Overview

Ireland's CBI VASP & MiCA CASP Framework

Ireland implemented VASP registration requirements under the Criminal Justice (Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing) Act 2010, significantly updated by the 2021 and 2024 Amendment Acts to implement the EU's 5th and 6th Anti-Money Laundering Directives. The CBI maintains the VASP register and conducts detailed AML fitness reviews including background checks on directors and beneficial owners.

Ireland's appeal for crypto licensing is well-established: Coinbase Europe, Coinbase Custody, and Kraken are among the major crypto firms that established their European headquarters in Ireland, drawn by the 12.5% corporate tax rate, English common law, skilled workforce, and EU market access. Under MiCA, the CBI is Ireland's designated competent authority, making an Irish CASP license the gateway to EU passporting from a low-tax base.

Tax advantage: Ireland's 12.5% corporation tax on trading income is among the lowest in the EU. Combined with MiCA passporting, this makes Ireland particularly attractive for crypto firms needing EU access with efficient tax structuring. Note: OECD Pillar Two 15% global minimum applies to multinationals with €750m+ revenue.

How to Get an Irish Crypto License — Step by Step

1
Incorporate Irish Entity

Incorporate an Irish Private Limited Company (Ltd) with the Companies Registration Office (CRO). Establish a genuine physical office in Ireland. Appoint at least two Irish-resident directors or demonstrate real substance. Inject required capital.

2–3 weeks
2
Pre-Application Engagement with CBI

Request a pre-application meeting with the CBI's FinTech unit. The CBI encourages early engagement and can provide clarity on requirements specific to your business model. This step is highly recommended.

2–4 weeks
3
Prepare Application Package

Develop the full CASP application: AML/CFT framework, business plan with 3-year financial projections, governance documentation, fit & proper packs for all directors and significant shareholders, IT security policy, and risk assessment.

4–8 weeks
4
Submit to CBI and CBI Review

File the complete CASP application with the CBI. The CBI reviews completeness, then commences formal assessment. Expect queries and requests for additional information. Respond promptly to minimise delays.

3–6 months
5
CASP Authorization Issued; Begin Operations

CBI issues the CASP authorization. Commence operations with ongoing CBI reporting. File EU passporting notifications via the ESMA procedure for each target EU member state.

Ireland Crypto License — Common Questions

Ireland implemented VASP registration under the Criminal Justice (Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing) Act 2010, as amended by the 2021 and 2024 Acts. The Central Bank of Ireland (CBI) supervises crypto service providers for AML/CFT compliance. Under MiCA, Ireland transitions to CASP authorization with the CBI as competent authority.
Ireland has a 12.5% corporate tax rate on trading income — one of the lowest in the EU. This makes Ireland highly attractive for crypto businesses seeking EU passporting combined with low tax. A 15% minimum effective tax rate applies under OECD Pillar Two rules for large multinationals.
Yes. A MiCA CASP authorization issued by the Central Bank of Ireland provides full EU passporting into all 27 EU member states, making Ireland an attractive base for global crypto firms targeting European markets.
CBI VASP registration typically takes 4–9 months. The CBI is thorough in its AML review and fit & proper assessments. The full MiCA CASP authorization process may take 6–12 months for complex applications.
Ireland combines a 12.5% corporate tax rate with full EU access and MiCA passporting, an English-speaking common law environment, and a strong financial services ecosystem. Major crypto exchanges including Coinbase and Kraken hold Irish registrations.
The CBI application fee is approximately 2,500 to 5,000 EUR, but total costs typically range from 15,000 to 40,000 EUR when including legal, compliance, and operational setup expenses. Professional advisory services through specialized firms can add 10,000 to 25,000 EUR depending on complexity. Ongoing annual compliance and regulatory fees generally cost 5,000 to 15,000 EUR per year.
The CBI requires detailed business plans, AML/CFT policies, beneficial ownership declarations, IT security assessments, financial projections, and governance structures. You must also submit detailed information on senior management and key personnel, including CVs and fit-and-proper declarations. All documentation must be notarized and submitted in English, even if your company operates in other languages.
Most Irish banks require proof of CBI licensing or advanced stages of application before opening institutional accounts, though some may open basic accounts during the application process. Payment processors and crypto-friendly banks like Wise Business or Revolut Business may offer alternatives during the licensing period. You should begin the banking process at least 2-3 months before your expected license approval to avoid delays.
Ireland offers stricter regulatory oversight through the CBI, which provides stronger international credibility and easier EU passporting under MiCA, whereas Malta and Cyprus historically had lighter-touch regulation. Ireland's timeline is typically 3-6 months compared to 2-4 months in Malta, but Ireland provides stronger consumer protection frameworks. All three offer EU passporting, but Ireland's regulatory reputation gives better access to traditional financial partnerships.
CBI-licensed VASPs must file annual financial statements, conduct regular AML/CFT audits, and submit transaction reporting on suspicious activities within specific timeframes. You are required to maintain detailed transaction records for at least five years and conduct annual compliance certifications demonstrating adherence to CBI guidelines. Senior management must complete regulatory training, and the firm must maintain minimum capital requirements, which vary based on your business model and risk profile.
Failure to renew results in automatic deactivation of your license within 90 days, after which you cannot legally operate as a VASP in Ireland or use EU passporting rights. Renewal applications must be submitted at least 60 days before expiration, and the CBI typically processes renewals within 30-45 days if no material changes occur. Lapsed licenses carry penalties of 5,000 to 10,000 EUR and may result in regulatory investigation if you continue operating.
The CBI VASP license covers exchange services, custody, and wallet management for all MiCA-compliant digital assets, though some high-risk or unregistered tokens may be restricted. Staking services, lending, and derivatives trading fall under different regulatory categories and may require additional licensing or exemptions. You must maintain an updated approved assets list reviewed by the CBI, and any expansion into new asset classes requires prior notification or amendment to your license.
