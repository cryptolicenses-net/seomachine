Overview

Georgia — VASP Registration Plus Virtual Zone Tax Advantage

Georgia regulates virtual asset service providers (VASPs) under the Law on Payment Systems and Payment Services, with the National Bank of Georgia (NBG) as the regulatory authority. Registration as a VASP is required for crypto exchanges, custodians, and wallet providers. The NBG process is streamlined compared to EU regulators — straightforward documentation, clear requirements, and a realistic 2–4 month timeline.

Georgia is particularly attractive due to its Virtual Zone status — companies with Virtual Zone designation pay 0% corporate income tax on income earned from services delivered outside Georgia. The Virtual Zone applies to IT and software companies, and crypto businesses providing services to non-Georgian clients commonly qualify. This combination of regulatory clarity and tax efficiency is rare globally.

Georgia has EU candidate country status as of 2023, with its Association Agreement and DCFTA with the EU already in force. While Georgia is not yet an EU member and MiCA passporting is not available, the country's regulatory alignment with international standards is progressing rapidly.

Virtual Zone qualification: Virtual Zone status is a separate application from VASP registration, filed with the Revenue Service of Georgia. Not all crypto activities automatically qualify — the company must primarily provide IT or software services. Legal analysis is recommended before applying to confirm eligibility for your specific business model.

Process

How to Get a Georgia VASP License — Step by Step

1
Incorporate Georgian LLC

Register a Georgian Limited Liability Company (LLC) at the National Agency of Public Registry. The incorporation process is fast — typically completable in 1–2 days online or through a notary. Inject the minimum capital of GEL 80,000 into the company's bank account. Georgia has no minimum residency requirements for company founders or directors.

1–2 weeks
2
Apply for Virtual Zone Status (if applicable)

File the Virtual Zone application with the Revenue Service of Georgia. The application requires: company registration documents, description of IT/crypto services, evidence that services will primarily be provided to non-Georgian clients, and financial projections. Virtual Zone status is typically granted within 2–4 weeks of a complete application.

2–4 weeks
3
Prepare VASP Registration Package

Develop the complete VASP registration package for the NBG: AML/CFT policies aligned with Georgian AML Law, organizational structure and key personnel details, IT security policy, business plan describing all crypto services, and evidence of the company's capital. The package should be translated into Georgian where required.

2–4 weeks
4
Submit VASP Registration to NBG

File the VASP registration with the National Bank of Georgia. The NBG reviews the application and may request additional information. Unlike EU regulators, the NBG typically processes complete applications within 4–8 weeks. The NBG engages constructively with applicants during the review period.

4–8 weeks NBG review
5
Registration Confirmed — Commence Operations

The NBG confirms VASP registration. Open the Georgian bank account (if not already done) and commence operations. Ongoing obligations include annual NBG reporting, AML/CFT annual review, and immediate notification of material changes. Virtual Zone companies must submit annual reports to the Revenue Service demonstrating continued qualifying activity.

Georgia Crypto License — Common Questions

The Virtual Zone is a Georgian tax regime for companies providing IT or software services to international clients. Virtual Zone companies pay 0% corporate income tax on revenue earned from non-Georgian clients. Dividend payments to foreign shareholders are subject to 5% withholding tax. Many crypto companies qualify, making Georgia one of the most tax-efficient jurisdictions for internationally-focused crypto businesses. The regime is governed by the Tax Code of Georgia.
The National Bank of Georgia requires a minimum paid-in capital of GEL 80,000 (approximately USD 30,000) for VASP registration. This is one of the lowest capital requirements for a regulated crypto license globally, making Georgia exceptionally accessible for early-stage crypto companies. The capital must be in the company's Georgian bank account at the time of application.

The registration fee for a VASP license in Georgia's Virtual Zone is approximately 2,000 GEL (around 700 USD), with additional costs for legal documentation, bank account setup, and compliance infrastructure typically ranging from 5,000-15,000 USD depending on your business model. Ongoing annual compliance and reporting fees generally amount to 1,000-3,000 USD per year. These costs are significantly lower than obtaining licenses in Western Europe, making Georgia an attractive option for startups.
The standard timeline for VASP registration in Georgia is 4-8 weeks from submission of complete documentation to final approval by the Financial Action Task Force (FATF) compliant regulator. If your application requires additional clarification or documentation, the process may extend to 12 weeks. Express processing options are not typically available, so applicants should plan accordingly when scheduling their operational launch.
Licensed VASPs in Georgia can establish accounts with both local Georgian banks and international banking partners, though availability varies by business model and geographic focus. TBC Bank and Bank of Georgia have shown openness to serving regulated crypto entities as of 2026. However, banking relationships remain somewhat limited compared to jurisdictions like Switzerland, and many crypto businesses maintain multi-jurisdictional banking arrangements to ensure operational continuity.
Georgia offers a favorable tax environment with corporate income tax applied only on distributed profits, not retained earnings, making it attractive for reinvestment-focused companies. Crypto businesses must comply with standard corporate tax requirements and maintain detailed transaction records for VAT and AML reporting purposes. It is essential to consult with a Georgian tax advisor to structure your entity appropriately, as tax treatment can vary based on your specific business activities and ownership structure.
Georgia requires all licensed VASPs to implement robust know-your-customer (KYC), anti-money laundering (AML), and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) procedures compliant with FATF guidelines. Licensees must submit quarterly compliance reports to the Financial Monitoring Service of Georgia and maintain detailed records of all customer transactions for at least five years. Regular third-party audits and compliance certifications are mandatory to maintain active license status.
Georgia's licensing process is faster (4-8 weeks versus 8-16 weeks in Malta) and significantly cheaper, but Malta and Cyprus offer stronger EU banking relationships and clearer regulatory precedent as established EU jurisdictions. Georgia provides greater operational flexibility and lower compliance costs, making it ideal for bootstrap operations, while Malta and Cyprus may be preferable if you require established European banking infrastructure or plan to serve EU clients extensively. Many businesses choose Georgia for initial registration and later obtain complementary EU licenses as they scale.
Georgia crypto licenses must be renewed annually, with renewal applications typically submitted 60 days before expiration and requiring updated compliance documentation, financial statements, and ownership declarations. Modification requests for changes in business scope, ownership, or key personnel can usually be processed within 2-4 weeks if documentation is complete. The renewal fee is substantially lower than initial licensing, generally 500-1,000 GEL, and renewal refusal is uncommon for compliant operators with clean regulatory records.
