Overview

Portugal — Europe's Most Crypto-Friendly Jurisdiction

Portugal implemented VASP registration under Decree-Law 102-D/2020, with the Banco de Portugal as the AML supervisory authority. The process is pragmatic and less demanding than Germany or France. Under MiCA, the Banco de Portugal becomes the CASP authorization authority.

Portugal is attractive due to 21% corporate tax (one of the lower EU rates), the NHR scheme for crypto professionals relocating to Portugal, and a tech-friendly environment in Lisbon. Portugal does not tax crypto gains for individual holders who are not professional traders — making it highly attractive for crypto businesses and professionals.

NHR tax advantage: Portugal's Non-Habitual Resident (NHR) scheme allows qualifying individuals who relocate to Portugal to benefit from a flat 20% income tax rate for 10 years. This is particularly attractive for crypto founders and executives moving from higher-tax jurisdictions.

Process

How to Get a Portugal Crypto License — Step by Step

1
Incorporate Portuguese Entity

Incorporate a Portuguese Lda. (Lda. — Sociedade por Quotas) at the RNPC. Open a Portuguese bank account and establish a physical office in Lisbon or Porto. The Lda. is the most common structure for fintech and crypto businesses in Portugal.

1–2 weeks
2
Register with Banco de Portugal for VASP AML

If applicable (e.g. if grandfathered from prior regime), register with the Banco de Portugal for VASP AML purposes. This covers the AML/CFT supervisory relationship.

4–8 weeks
3
Prepare MiCA CASP Application Package

Develop the complete CASP application: business plan with financial projections, AML/CFT framework per Lei n.º 83/2017, IT security documentation, governance structure, and fit & proper documentation for all directors and key shareholders.

4–8 weeks
4
Submit to Banco de Portugal — Review Period

File the complete CASP application with the Banco de Portugal. The BdP reviews the application, which typically takes 2–4 months for complete, well-prepared submissions.

2–4 months
5
CASP Authorization Issued

Banco de Portugal issues the CASP authorization. Commence operations with ongoing BdP reporting. EU passporting notifications can be filed immediately. Annual audit and AML/CFT review obligations apply.

Initial licensing fees with Banco de Portugal typically range from EUR 5,000 to EUR 15,000 depending on your business model and services offered. Additional costs include legal advisory (EUR 3,000-8,000), compliance infrastructure setup (EUR 10,000-30,000), and initial capital requirements which vary by activity type but start at EUR 50,000 minimum for basic CASP services. Annual compliance and supervision fees are approximately EUR 2,000-5,000.
Banco de Portugal requires articles of association, proof of capital, detailed business plan, organizational chart, AML/CFT procedures manual, IT security documentation, shareholder registry, CVs of management with criminal background checks, and proof of suitable premises. All documents must be submitted in Portuguese or with certified translations, and your compliance officer must hold Portuguese professional qualifications or equivalents recognized under MiCA.
Portugal operates under EU's unified MiCA framework requiring Banco de Portugal registration, while Switzerland offers FINMA licensing with stricter capital requirements (typically EUR 300,000+) but faster approval for established firms. Portugal's process is more accessible for startups with lower barriers to entry, though Switzerland provides stronger international recognition and no EU-wide regulatory uncertainty. Both jurisdictions offer passport rights within their respective regulatory zones.
License holders must submit annual financial statements, maintain detailed transaction records, conduct regular AML/CFT risk assessments, and implement customer due diligence for transactions above EUR 10,000. You must also report suspicious activities to UIF (Financial Intelligence Unit) within 10 business days, undergo annual compliance audits, and maintain minimum capital buffers as stipulated by MiCA. Banco de Portugal conducts on-site inspections typically every 18-24 months.
Operating without a Portuguese bank account is not practical; Banco de Portugal requires documented banking relationships for fund custody and operations. Many traditional Portuguese banks remain cautious with crypto-related accounts, though this has improved under MiCA standardization. We recommend establishing relationships with crypto-friendly banks like Wise Business, Revolut for Business, or specialized banking partners before license approval, as this is increasingly reviewed during the application assessment.
Your license becomes invalid immediately upon expiration and you cannot legally provide crypto services; continued operations constitute a regulatory violation punishable by fines up to EUR 100,000 and potential criminal liability. Renewal applications should be submitted 90 days before expiration, and the process typically takes 30-45 days for standard renewals. Banco de Portugal may refuse renewal if you have outstanding compliance violations or have breached capital requirements.
Portugal's MiCA license permits trading, custody, and exchange services for admitted cryptocurrencies, but stablecoins require additional regulatory authorization under the separate Stablecoin Regulation. You cannot offer unregistered securities or derivatives unless you also hold securities licensing from Comissão do Mercado de Valores Mobiliários (CMVM). High-risk tokens and those subject to national sanctions are prohibited, and your service terms must explicitly list approved assets and exclude prohibited categories.
