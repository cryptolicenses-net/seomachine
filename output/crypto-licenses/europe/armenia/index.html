Overview

Armenia as a Regional Crypto Hub

Armenia has undergone a rapid transformation into a regional technology and fintech hub over the past several years. The acceleration began with the 2022 Russian exodus — following the Ukraine invasion and subsequent sanctions, tens of thousands of Russian IT professionals and companies relocated to Yerevan, drawn by visa-free access, USD-denominated banking, low costs, and a welcoming business environment.

The Central Bank of Armenia (CBA) regulates payment system operators and financial service providers including those operating in the crypto space, principally under the Law on Payment Systems and Payment Organizations. While Armenia does not yet have a standalone crypto-specific law on the scale of MiCA or dedicated legislation like Montenegro's Digital Assets Act, the CBA-supervised payment systems framework provides a workable pathway for VASP registration and operation.

A key practical advantage of Armenia is its banking environment. Unlike many post-Soviet jurisdictions, Armenia's banks — including Ameriabank, Ardshinbank, ACBA Bank, and Converse Bank — operate with USD accounts, SWIFT transfers, and relatively open correspondent banking relationships. This makes Armenia significantly more practical for international crypto business than jurisdictions where USD banking is restricted or unavailable.

Tech hub growth: Yerevan's Dalma Garden tech hub and the broader IT sector have experienced exceptional growth since 2022. Armenia's software export revenue grew significantly, and several major crypto exchanges and fintech companies established Armenian entities. The CBA has responded by developing clearer guidance for payment system operators including those handling digital assets.

Process

How to Get an Armenia Crypto Licence — Step by Step

1
Register Armenian LLC and Inject Capital

Register an Armenian LLC (ՍՊԸ) with the State Register of Legal Entities. The process requires a registered address in Armenia, charter documents, and identification of all shareholders. Inject the required minimum capital as paid-in share capital. Open an Armenian bank account (Ameriabank, Ardshinbank, or Converse Bank recommended).

1–2 weeks
2
Develop Compliance Framework and Appoint MLRO

Prepare a complete AML/CFT program in compliance with Armenia's Law on Combating Money Laundering and Terrorism Financing. Appoint a qualified MLRO and register them with the Financial Monitoring Centre (FMC). Develop KYC/CDD procedures, transaction monitoring policies, risk assessment documentation, and suspicious activity reporting workflows.

2–4 weeks
3
Prepare and Submit CBA Application

Compile the CBA licence application package: entity documents, ownership structure and beneficial ownership declarations, management CVs and fitness documentation, business plan with financial projections, AML/CFT framework, IT security policies, and capital evidence. Submit to the CBA Licensing Department with the applicable fee.

2–3 weeks
4
CBA Review and Due Diligence

The CBA conducts a thorough review including background checks on management and shareholders, assessment of the AML framework, and evaluation of the business plan. The CBA may conduct an on-site inspection or request additional documentation. The CBA is generally accessible and responsive to well-structured applications.

6–10 weeks
5
Licence Issued — Commence Operations

CBA issues the payment system operator / VASP licence. Register with the Financial Monitoring Centre. Commence regulated operations. Maintain ongoing CBA and FMC reporting, annual audited financial statements, and compliance with Armenia's AML/CFT obligations.
