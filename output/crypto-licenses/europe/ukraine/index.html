Overview

Ukraine's Virtual Assets Act and NSSMC Framework

Ukraine's Law on Virtual Assets was signed by President Zelenskyy on February 17, 2022 — just seven days before Russia's full-scale invasion. Despite the extraordinary geopolitical circumstances, the law came into effect and established a framework for VASP registration under the NSSMC. Ukraine became one of the first Eastern European states to provide explicit legal recognition of virtual assets.

Ukraine consistently ranks among the world's top countries for crypto adoption. Chainanalysis has ranked Ukraine in the top 5 globally for grassroots crypto adoption for several consecutive years. Crypto played a significant role in Ukraine's wartime economy — facilitating international donations to the government and NGOs, powering remittances from the Ukrainian diaspora, and enabling businesses to operate despite banking disruptions.

The NSSMC oversees VASP registration. Key activities covered include: exchange of virtual assets for fiat or other virtual assets, transfer services, custody and administration, and related financial services. Registration is procedurally accessible and cost-effective, though prospective registrants should conduct thorough due diligence on the current operational environment.

Wartime advisory: Ukraine crypto VASP registration is legally operational, but international banking access is limited due to NBU capital controls and correspondent banking constraints. Most Ukraine-registered VASPs use EU-based EMIs (Lithuania, Estonia) for international fiat settlement. Assess operational risk carefully before committing to a Ukrainian entity as your primary regulatory vehicle.

Process

How to Register as a VASP in Ukraine — Step by Step

1
Register Ukrainian TOV (LLC)

Register a TOV (limited liability company) with the State Registrar of Ukraine. The process requires a Ukrainian tax identification number (IPN) for shareholders and directors, registration documents in Ukrainian, and payment of the state registration fee of approximately UAH 1,000 (around USD 25). The process can be completed online through the Ministry of Justice portal.

1–2 weeks
2
Develop AML/CFT Compliance Framework

Prepare a complete AML/CFT program per Ukraine's Law on Prevention and Counteraction of Legalization of Criminal Proceeds (Anti-Money Laundering Law). Appoint a compliance officer registered with the State Financial Monitoring Service (SFMS/Derzhfinmonitoring). Implement KYC/CDD procedures, risk-based approach documentation, and suspicious transaction reporting workflows.

2–4 weeks
3
Submit NSSMC VASP Registration Application

Prepare and submit the VASP registration application to the NSSMC in Ukrainian. The application includes entity documentation, ownership structure, beneficial ownership declaration, management details, AML framework, business plan, and capital evidence. The NSSMC application fee of USD 500–2,000 is paid at submission.

1–2 weeks
4
NSSMC Review and VASP Registration

The NSSMC reviews the application and may request clarifications. Ukraine's registration process is relatively streamlined — completed applications typically receive decisions within 30–60 working days. The NSSMC issues a VASP registration certificate upon approval.

1–2 months
5
Open Banking / EMI Accounts and Commence Operations

Open a Ukrainian bank account for domestic UAH operations. Establish an EU EMI account (Lithuania, Estonia, or Poland) for international fiat transactions. Implement SFMS reporting. Commence regulated VASP operations with ongoing NSSMC and SFMS reporting obligations.

FAQ

Ukraine VASP Registration — Common Questions

The Law of Ukraine on Virtual Assets was signed on February 17, 2022, just days before the Russian invasion. It established a legal framework for virtual assets and VASP registration under the NSSMC. The law represents Ukraine's commitment to regulating crypto despite extraordinary wartime circumstances.
VASP registration and crypto operations are legally possible in Ukraine during the current conflict. Many Ukrainian crypto businesses continue to operate, and Ukraine remains one of the world's most crypto-active countries. However, practical challenges include restricted international banking access, partial disruptions to government services, and elevated operational risk. Thorough due diligence is essential before proceeding.
The National Securities and Stock Market Commission (NSSMC, or NKCPFR in Ukrainian) is the primary regulator for VASP registration. The National Bank of Ukraine (NBU) retains oversight for payment-related crypto activities. Both authorities collaborate on AML/CFT enforcement in the crypto sector.
The NSSMC registration fee is approximately USD 500–2,000. Total first-year costs including legal preparation, compliance framework, and entity setup typically range from USD 8,000–22,000, making Ukraine one of the most affordable VASP registration jurisdictions globally.
International banking for Ukrainian entities is currently constrained due to wartime conditions and NBU capital controls. Many Ukraine-based crypto businesses use EU-regulated Electronic Money Institutions (EMIs) such as those in Lithuania or Estonia for international fiat operations, while using Ukrainian banks for domestic UAH transactions.
