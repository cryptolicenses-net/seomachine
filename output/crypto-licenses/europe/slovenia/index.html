Overview

Slovenia's MiCA Framework & ATVP Regulation

Slovenia joined the European Union in 2004 and is a full participant in the EU's regulatory framework for crypto-assets. MiCA — the Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation — became fully applicable across all EU member states in December 2024, replacing national pre-MiCA regimes with a harmonized framework for Crypto-Asset Service Providers (CASPs).

Prior to MiCA, Slovenia regulated virtual currency service providers under its AML legislation, specifically ZPPDFT-2 (Zakon o preprečevanju pranja denarja in financiranja terorizma). Providers were required to register with the ATVP for AML/CFT compliance purposes. Under MiCA, this registration requirement is superseded by the full CASP authorization process, which carries significantly broader obligations — and significantly broader market access through EU passporting.

The ATVP (Agencija za trg vrednostnih papirjev) is Slovenia's Agency for the Securities Market. As the designated competent authority under MiCA, the ATVP handles all MiCA CASP authorizations for Slovenian-incorporated entities. The ATVP is a smaller regulator compared to the AMF (France) or BaFin (Germany), which may translate to more accessible communication during the application process — an advantage for first-time applicants.

Slovenia presents an interesting case for crypto licensing: a stable, eurozone EU jurisdiction with a competitive 19% corporate tax rate, a well-educated English-speaking workforce, and a growing fintech presence in Ljubljana. The primary trade-off is a small domestic market of approximately 2 million people — but with MiCA passporting, the domestic market size is largely irrelevant for operators targeting the broader EU.

MiCA applies from December 2024: All new crypto license applications in Slovenia are processed as MiCA CASP authorizations by the ATVP. Existing pre-MiCA registrants under ZPPDFT-2 benefit from an 18-month grandfathering period under MiCA's transitional provisions, ending no later than mid-2026.

MiCA CASP Services Available in Slovenia

Under MiCA Article 60, a CASP authorization issued by the ATVP may cover any combination of the following regulated crypto-asset services. Applicants must specify which services they intend to provide, and the capital requirement scales with the service class.

Investment Advice Providing personalised recommendations on crypto-assets Class 1 — €15k

Class 3 full-service authorization: Entities providing all MiCA services including transfer services must hold at least €150,000 in initial capital. The ATVP verifies capital adequacy at both application stage and on an ongoing supervisory basis.

Process

How to Get a Slovenia ATVP Crypto License — Step by Step

1
Establish a Slovenian d.o.o.

Incorporate a Slovenian d.o.o. (Družba z omejeno odgovornostjo) through a Slovenian notary and register with AJPES (Agency of the Republic of Slovenia for Public Legal Records). Appoint qualified directors, inject the required minimum share capital, and establish a genuine physical presence in Slovenia. Engage Slovenian legal counsel at this stage — the ATVP application will need to be in Slovenian.

2–4 weeks
2
Prepare the MiCA CASP Application Dossier

Compile the full ATVP application package in Slovenian. This includes: business plan and financial projections, corporate governance documentation, AML/CFT policies and procedures (compliant with ZPPDFT-2 and MiCA), IT security policy and business continuity plan, fit and proper documentation for all directors and qualifying shareholders, and a client asset safeguarding description. All non-Slovenian corporate documents must be professionally translated and certified.

6–10 weeks
3
Submit Application to the ATVP

File the complete CASP application with the ATVP. Under MiCA Article 62, the ATVP must acknowledge completeness within 25 working days and confirm whether the application is complete. Incomplete applications are returned — ensure all required documentation is included before submission to avoid delays.

Submission
4
ATVP Review Period

The ATVP conducts its substantive review of the application, including background checks on directors and shareholders, evaluation of the AML/CFT framework, IT security assessment, and business plan review. The ATVP may request additional information (RFI) — responses must be provided promptly and in Slovenian. Under MiCA, the competent authority must reach a decision within 40 working days of confirming application completeness.

3–6 months
5
CASP Authorization Issued

Upon approval, the ATVP issues the MiCA CASP authorization and registers the company in ESMA's central CASP register. The authorization specifies the permitted crypto-asset services. The company may commence operations immediately upon issuance.

Authorization issued
6
Activate EU Passport in Target States

To provide services in other EU member states, notify the ATVP of your intended target jurisdictions. Under MiCA Article 63, the ATVP then notifies the NCAs (national competent authorities) of those target states. You may commence cross-border services in each target state 15 working days after the ATVP sends notification — without any additional authorization or registration required in those states.

15 working days per jurisdiction

Class 1 services (reception and transmission of orders, execution of orders, investment advice, portfolio management) require a minimum of €15,000 initial capital. The differences are: Lithuania offers a lower corporate tax rate (15% vs Slovenia's 19%) and a well-established regulator (Bank of Lithuania) with extensive crypto experience, but the jurisdiction is increasingly saturated. Slovenia's advantage is a less crowded regulatory environment, potentially more accessible ATVP communication, and lower professional services costs — making it a solid alternative if Lithuania and Estonia feel oversaturated.
The ATVP requires all application documents to be submitted in Slovenian. This means that all corporate documents, business plans, AML/CFT policies, governance documents, and supporting materials must be either originally drafted in Slovenian or professionally translated and certified. For international applicants, this is a meaningful cost factor — engaging a Slovenian law firm or compliance specialist with experience in MiCA applications is essential. Budget for certified translation costs in addition to standard legal fees.
The initial application fee to the Slovenian Financial Administration (ATVP) is approximately EUR 2,500-4,000, with annual supervision fees ranging from EUR 3,000-8,000 depending on your business volume and classification. Additional costs include legal advisory (EUR 5,000-15,000), compliance infrastructure setup, and banking relationship establishment, typically totaling EUR 20,000-40,000 for first-year setup. Renewal fees remain consistent annually after the initial licensing phase.
The standard review period is 3-4 months from complete application submission to the Slovenian Financial Administration, though complex cases involving substantial verification requests can extend to 5-6 months. Pre-application consultation with ATVP is recommended and adds 2-4 weeks to your timeline. In 2026, expedited processing is not officially available, but maintaining clear communication with your assigned case officer can help avoid delays.
You must implement a written AML/CFT policy compliant with Slovenia's Financial Administration regulations and EU directive 2015/849, including customer due diligence procedures, beneficial ownership verification, and transaction monitoring systems. Documentation must be submitted in Slovenian or certified English translation, covering your compliance officer qualifications, staff training protocols, and incident reporting procedures. The ATVP requires evidence of integration with Slovenia's Financial Intelligence Unit reporting system before license issuance.
No meaningful exemptions exist in Slovenia as of 2026 if you handle user funds or provide custody services; MiCA licensing is mandatory for CASPs and wallet providers. Limited exceptions apply only to purely informational platforms that never hold customer assets, though you must still notify ATVP of your operations. Attempting to operate without proper licensing results in substantial fines (up to EUR 100,000+) and criminal liability under Slovenian law.
Slovenia applies standard corporate income tax of 19% on net profits, with no special crypto-specific tax regime as of 2026, making it comparable to most EU members. However, you benefit from EU headquarters advantages including VAT recovery on professional services and potential R&D tax credits if you develop proprietary trading or security technology. Malta and Cyprus offer marginally lower effective rates through IP deductions, though Slovenia's regulatory clarity and banking access increasingly offset this advantage.
NLB (Nova Ljubljanska Banka) and Abanka have established crypto-friendly banking relationships, though they require full MiCA licensing documentation and detailed AML policies before account opening. Banking relationships typically require minimum account balances of EUR 10,000-50,000 and proof of licensed operations through your ATVP license certificate. In 2026, establishing accounts takes 4-8 weeks post-licensing due to enhanced due diligence procedures specific to the crypto sector.
The Slovenian Financial Administration can issue formal remediation orders requiring correction within 30-90 days depending on violation severity, with progress reviews every 30 days. Failure to remediate results in license suspension, substantial fines (EUR 5,000-50,000), and potential revocation after 120 days of non-compliance. Maintaining documented compliance audit trails and engaging external compliance consultants immediately upon receiving ATVP findings significantly improves remediation success rates.
