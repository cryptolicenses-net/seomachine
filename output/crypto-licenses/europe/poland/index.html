Overview

Why Choose Poland for Your Crypto License?

Poland entered the regulated crypto landscape through its AML Act (Ustawa o przeciwdziałaniu praniu pieniędzy), which transposes the EU's 5th and 6th Anti-Money Laundering Directives. The country requires Virtual Asset Service Providers operating from Polish territory to register in the Register of Virtual Currency Activities maintained by the Ministry of Finance, with KNF overseeing compliance oversight.

Compared with more complex EU licensing regimes — such as Lithuania's VASP authorisation or Estonia's post-2022 framework — Poland offers a lighter-touch registration process with faster turnaround and lower upfront costs. This makes it particularly attractive for early-stage crypto businesses that want EU regulatory standing while keeping operational costs lean.

The strategic timing argument is also compelling: Poland's existing VASP registrations will benefit from MiCA grandfathering provisions, allowing registered entities to continue operating under interim rules while transitioning to the full MiCA CASP authorisation regime by the end of 2025.

MiCA Transition Note: From 30 December 2024, MiCA applies across the EU. Polish VASPs registered before that date receive a grandfathering period (until 1 July 2026 at the latest) to obtain full MiCA CASP authorisation. New applicants post-December 2024 must meet MiCA standards from day one.

Process

How to Get a Polish Crypto License — Step by Step

1
Incorporate the Polish Entity

Establish a spółka z ograniczoną odpowiedzialnością (sp. z o.o.) — Poland's equivalent of an LLC. This can be done electronically via the S24 portal in 1–3 business days, or via notary within a week. Minimum share capital of PLN 20,000 must be deposited. Register in the National Court Register (KRS) and obtain NIP and REGON numbers.

Weeks 1–2
2
Build Your AML Compliance Program

Draft comprehensive AML/KYC policies that satisfy the Polish AML Act requirements. This includes: risk assessment methodology, customer due diligence procedures (standard, simplified, and enhanced), transaction monitoring rules, STR filing procedures, employee training records, and appointment of a compliant AML Compliance Officer.

Weeks 2–4
3
Prepare and File the Registration Application

Submit the application to the Ministry of Finance for entry into the Register of Virtual Currency Activities (Rejestr Działalności w Zakresie Walut Wirtualnych). The application must include all company documents, director KYC packages, AML procedures, and a business plan. The state fee is PLN 616 (approximately EUR 140).

Week 5
4
KNF Review & Registration

The Ministry of Finance processes the application (with KNF oversight) within approximately 30 days of receiving a complete submission. Requests for additional information (RFI) are common — respond promptly to avoid delays. Upon successful review, the entity is added to the public register.

Weeks 6–8
5
Launch Operations & Maintain Compliance

Once registered, begin operations. You must implement ongoing transaction monitoring, file STRs with GIIF (General Inspector of Financial Information) when required, keep AML records for 5 years, and submit annual compliance reports. Begin planning your MiCA CASP authorisation pathway if you are within the grandfathering window.

Under the Polish AML Act, registration is required for: (1) exchange services between virtual currencies and fiat currencies, (2) exchange services between virtual currencies, (3) intermediary services for such exchanges, (4) maintaining accounts (custody wallets). Operating without registration is a criminal offence subject to fines and imprisonment.
Registered VASPs must: maintain and update their AML/KYC program, monitor transactions and file STRs with GIIF (the Polish financial intelligence unit), conduct annual internal AML audits, keep records for 5 years, report changes to company structure or activities within 7 days, and train staff on AML obligations annually.
Initial registration costs typically range from EUR 5,000 to EUR 15,000 depending on complexity and whether you engage local advisors, with annual compliance and reporting fees between EUR 3,000 to EUR 8,000. The Financial Supervision Authority (KNF) does not charge statutory licensing fees, but you must budget for legal documentation, compliance infrastructure, and potential audit costs. Costs may increase if substantial modifications to your compliance program are required during annual reviews.
Polish VASPs should expect increased banking scrutiny under EU AML/CFT standards and may require accounts with banks experienced in crypto sector relationships, such as Santander Poland, PKO BP, or specialized fintech banks. Most major Polish banks now require comprehensive compliance documentation and proof of KNF registration before opening business accounts. Relationship managers typically demand quarterly compliance certifications and may impose transaction monitoring requirements specific to cryptocurrency activities.
Polish crypto businesses are subject to corporate income tax at 19% on profits, with capital gains from crypto trading treated as ordinary income rather than capital gains. VAT may apply to certain crypto services depending on classification, and the Polish Tax Authority (Krajowa Administracja Skarbowa) has issued guidance treating crypto trading as business activity when conducted professionally. You should consult a local tax advisor, as treatment of staking rewards and DeFi activities remains evolving.
The Financial Supervision Authority can issue remedial orders ranging from minor compliance adjustments to license suspension or revocation, with penalties up to EUR 1 million or 10% of annual turnover for serious violations. Failure to remedy critical deficiencies within specified timeframes typically results in de-registration and public notification. Repeated or intentional violations can result in director bans and criminal referrals to prosecutors.
Poland offers faster KNF registration timelines (3-4 months) compared to Malta's 4-6 months, with lower initial costs than either jurisdiction, but provides narrower EU passporting recognition than Malta's more established framework. Liechtenstein's crypto licensing is more restrictive and traditionally favors asset management over trading platforms. Poland is optimal for EU-focused operations with cost sensitivity, while Malta suits entities seeking broader EU/international expansion and established regulatory precedent.

Poland KNF: What We See in Practice

  • Poland VASP registration via the Tax Administration Chamber takes 14-30 days — one of Europe's fastest.
  • No minimum capital requirement for VASP registration (only company formation capital of ~PLN 5,000 / €1,200).
  • Poland is a strong entry point for MiCA: register now as VASP, transition to full CASP by mid-2026.
  • Banking access is challenging — plan 2-3 months to open a compliant business account with a Polish bank.
