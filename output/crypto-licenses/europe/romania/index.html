Overview

Romania's Cost-Effective EU Crypto Licensing Path

Romania implemented VASP registration requirements through an amendment to Law 129/2019 (the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Law), designating the ASF (Autoritatea de Supraveghere Financiară — Financial Supervisory Authority) as the competent authority. The Romanian framework requires VASPs to register with ASF before providing virtual asset services in Romania, implementing a compliant AML/CFT program, and meeting fit-and-proper requirements for management.

Romania's standout advantages for crypto licensing are its EU membership combined with low costs. The 16% flat corporate income tax is one of the lowest in the EU. The Romanian Leu (RON) remains the national currency (Romania is not in the Eurozone), but the country is an EU member with full access to the MiCA framework. Company formation costs are minimal — an S.R.L. (Societate cu Răspundere Limitată) can be formed with approximately EUR 40 in share capital. ASF registration fees of EUR 500–1,000 are among the lowest in Europe for financial regulatory applications.

Bucharest has developed a significant technology sector with strong software development capabilities and a growing fintech community. Low office and salary costs relative to Western Europe make Romania an operationally cost-effective base. For companies seeking a low-cost EU entry point for MiCA CASP authorisation and EU passporting, Romania warrants serious consideration.

MiCA opportunity: As an EU member state, Romania's ASF is designated as the MiCA CASP competent authority. A Romanian MiCA CASP authorisation — obtainable at significantly lower operational cost than Western European jurisdictions — provides identical EU passporting rights as authorisations from France, Germany, or the Netherlands.

Process

How to Get a Romania ASF Crypto Licence — Step by Step

1
Incorporate Romanian S.R.L. and Register with ONRC

Incorporate a Romanian Societate cu Răspundere Limitată (S.R.L.) through the National Trade Register Office (ONRC). Minimum share capital is approximately EUR 40 (RON 200), though capitalise adequately per your operational needs and ASF requirements. Register with the tax authority (ANAF) and obtain a fiscal registration certificate. Total formation costs are among the lowest in the EU.

1–2 weeks
2
Develop AML/CFT Framework per Law 129/2019

Prepare a complete AML/CFT compliance program compliant with Romanian Law 129/2019 and EU AML directives. This includes written KYC/CDD procedures, customer risk-rating methodology, transaction monitoring rules, Travel Rule compliance procedures for crypto transfers, suspicious transaction reporting procedures, and GDPR-aligned data protection policies. Appoint a designated AML compliance officer.

2–3 weeks
3
Submit ASF Registration Application

File the VASP registration application with ASF. The application includes corporate documentation, description of virtual asset services to be provided, AML/CFT framework, management background documentation and declarations, capital proof, and payment of the ASF registration fee (EUR 500–1,000). ASF acknowledges receipt and begins the formal review process.

Week 4–5
4
ASF Review, Queries, and Background Checks

ASF reviews the application and conducts background checks on management and significant shareholders. ASF may issue formal queries requiring written responses. Engage a Romanian legal counsel familiar with ASF procedures to manage correspondence efficiently. Well-prepared applications typically receive registration within 2–4 months of complete submission.

2–4 months
5
Registration Granted — Commence Operations and EU Passporting

ASF grants the VASP registration. Commence providing virtual asset services to Romanian and EU customers. Establish ongoing compliance infrastructure: AML monitoring, annual ASF reporting, GDPR compliance. For MiCA CASP authorisation and EU passporting, submit a separate CASP application to ASF — building on the existing regulatory relationship and compliance infrastructure.

Romania ASF Crypto Licence — Common Questions

Romanian ASF VASP registration is a mandatory authorisation for virtual asset service providers operating in Romania, introduced by an amendment to Law 129/2019 (Romania's AML law). The ASF (Autoritatea de Supraveghere Financiară — Financial Supervisory Authority) is the competent authority. It covers custody, exchange, and transfer services. Under MiCA, ASF is also Romania's designated CASP authorisation authority.
Romania offers one of the most cost-effective EU crypto licensing paths: a 16% flat corporate tax rate (among the EU's lowest), low registration fees (EUR 500–1,000), minimal share capital for S.R.L. formation (approximately EUR 40), and relatively straightforward ASF procedures. Bucharest's growing IT sector provides tech talent. As an EU member, Romania provides access to MiCA CASP authorisation and full EU passporting.
ASF VASP registration in Romania typically takes 2–4 months from submission of a complete application. The timeline is influenced by the completeness of AML documentation, management background checks, and ASF's current processing workload. Well-prepared applications with thorough AML frameworks tend to be processed at the faster end of this range.
Romania applies a 10% flat capital gains tax on crypto profits for individual investors — one of the lowest rates in the EU. Corporate entities are subject to the standard 16% corporate income tax on crypto-related income. Romania does not have a separate crypto-specific wealth or holding tax, making it attractive for both individual and corporate crypto participants.
