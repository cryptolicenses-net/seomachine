Overview

Romania's Cost-Effective EU Crypto Licensing Path

Romania implemented VASP registration requirements through an amendment to Law 129/2019 (the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Law), designating the ASF (Autoritatea de Supraveghere Financiară — Financial Supervisory Authority) as the competent authority. The Romanian framework requires VASPs to register with ASF before providing virtual asset services in Romania, implementing a compliant AML/CFT program, and meeting fit-and-proper requirements for management.

Romania's standout advantages for crypto licensing are its EU membership combined with low costs. The 16% flat corporate income tax is one of the lowest in the EU. The Romanian Leu (RON) remains the national currency (Romania is not in the Eurozone), but the country is an EU member with full access to the MiCA framework. Company formation costs are minimal — an S.R.L. (Societate cu Răspundere Limitată) can be formed with approximately EUR 40 in share capital. ASF registration fees of EUR 500–1,000 are among the lowest in Europe for financial regulatory applications.

Bucharest has developed a significant technology sector with strong software development capabilities and a growing fintech community. Low office and salary costs relative to Western Europe make Romania an operationally cost-effective base. For companies seeking a low-cost EU entry point for MiCA CASP authorization and EU passporting, Romania warrants serious consideration.

MiCA opportunity: As an EU member state, Romania's ASF is designated as the MiCA CASP competent authority. A Romanian MiCA CASP authorization — obtainable at significantly lower operational cost than Western European jurisdictions — provides identical EU passporting rights as authorizations from France, Germany, or the Netherlands.

Process

How to Get a Romania ASF Crypto License — Step by Step

1
Incorporate Romanian S.R.L. and Register with ONRC

Incorporate a Romanian Societate cu Răspundere Limitată (S.R.L.) through the National Trade Register Office (ONRC). Minimum share capital is approximately EUR 40 (RON 200), though capitalize adequately per your operational needs and ASF requirements. Register with the tax authority (ANAF) and obtain a fiscal registration certificate. Total formation costs are among the lowest in the EU.

1–2 weeks
2
Develop AML/CFT Framework per Law 129/2019

Prepare a comprehensive AML/CFT compliance program compliant with Romanian Law 129/2019 and EU AML directives. This includes written KYC/CDD procedures, customer risk-rating methodology, transaction monitoring rules, Travel Rule compliance procedures for crypto transfers, suspicious transaction reporting procedures, and GDPR-aligned data protection policies. Appoint a designated AML compliance officer.

2–3 weeks
3
Submit ASF Registration Application

File the VASP registration application with ASF. The application includes corporate documentation, description of virtual asset services to be provided, AML/CFT framework, management background documentation and declarations, capital proof, and payment of the ASF registration fee (EUR 500–1,000). ASF acknowledges receipt and begins the formal review process.

Week 4–5
4
ASF Review, Queries, and Background Checks

ASF reviews the application and conducts background checks on management and significant shareholders. ASF may issue formal queries requiring written responses. Engage a Romanian legal counsel familiar with ASF procedures to manage correspondence efficiently. Well-prepared applications typically receive registration within 2–4 months of complete submission.

2–4 months
5
Registration Granted — Commence Operations and EU Passporting

ASF grants the VASP registration. Commence providing virtual asset services to Romanian and EU customers. Establish ongoing compliance infrastructure: AML monitoring, annual ASF reporting, GDPR compliance. For MiCA CASP authorization and EU passporting, submit a separate CASP application to ASF — building on the existing regulatory relationship and compliance infrastructure.

Romania ASF Crypto License — Common Questions

Romanian ASF VASP registration is a mandatory authorization for virtual asset service providers operating in Romania, introduced by an amendment to Law 129/2019 (Romania's AML law). The ASF (Autoritatea de Supraveghere Financiară — Financial Supervisory Authority) is the competent authority. It covers custody, exchange, and transfer services. Under MiCA, ASF is also Romania's designated CASP authorization authority.
Romania offers one of the most cost-effective EU crypto licensing paths: a 16% flat corporate tax rate (among the EU's lowest), low registration fees (EUR 500–1,000), minimal share capital for S.R.L. formation (approximately EUR 40), and relatively straightforward ASF procedures. Bucharest's growing IT sector provides tech talent. As an EU member, Romania provides access to MiCA CASP authorization and full EU passporting.
ASF VASP registration in Romania typically takes 2–4 months from submission of a complete application. The timeline is influenced by the completeness of AML documentation, management background checks, and ASF's current processing workload. Well-prepared applications with thorough AML frameworks tend to be processed at the faster end of this range.
Romania applies a 10% flat capital gains tax on crypto profits for individual investors — one of the lowest rates in the EU. Corporate entities are subject to the standard 16% corporate income tax on crypto-related income. Romania does not have a separate crypto-specific wealth or holding tax, making it attractive for both individual and corporate crypto participants.
Yes. Under MiCA, ASF is Romania's designated CASP competent authority. A Romanian MiCA CASP authorization provides full EU passporting rights into all 27 member states. Romania's 16% corporate tax and low operational costs make it one of the most cost-effective EU bases for obtaining MiCA CASP authorization with full EU passporting.
The ASF registration fee is approximately 5,000-8,000 EUR, but total costs typically range from 15,000-40,000 EUR when including legal fees, compliance setup, and initial capital requirements. Operating costs are generally lower than Western European jurisdictions, with annual compliance fees around 3,000-5,000 EUR. Costs may vary depending on your VASP category and service scope.
Required documentation includes a detailed business plan, organizational structure, compliance policies, AML/KYC procedures, IT security protocols, and proof of financial means. You must also provide biographical information for all beneficial owners and key management personnel, along with evidence of suitable premises and qualified compliance staff. All documents must be submitted in Romanian or certified English translations.
Yes, Romania requires minimum capital depending on your VASP activities, typically ranging from 50,000-250,000 EUR based on your service scope and projected transaction volumes. This capital must be held in a Romanian bank account and remain available throughout your licensing period. The ASF may request higher reserves based on your risk profile and business model.
Romania offers faster approval timelines (4-6 months versus 6-12 months in Malta) and lower operational costs, though Malta provides broader EU crypto credibility and more established precedent. Cyprus has higher minimum capital requirements but better banking infrastructure for crypto firms. Romania is ideal for cost-conscious businesses prioritizing EU access over international brand recognition.
License holders must submit annual audited financial statements, compliance reports, and updated risk assessments to the ASF by March 31st each year. You must also maintain detailed transaction records for five years, conduct regular AML/KYC reviews, and report any material changes to your operations within 10 business days. Failure to comply can result in fines up to 100,000 EUR or license suspension.
Banking access has improved significantly since 2024, with several Romanian and EU banks now accepting ASF-regulated VASPs, including OTP Bank and Raiffeisen Bank. However, international tier-one banking remains challenging, and many firms maintain accounts in Germany or France for EUR operations. You should arrange banking relationships during your licensing application to avoid operational delays.
Material changes to your business model, service offerings, or ownership structure require ASF pre-approval and typically take 2-4 weeks to process. Minor operational adjustments can be reported in your annual compliance filing, but significant expansions (such as adding lending or staking services) require formal amendment requests. Unauthorized service changes can lead to fines or license revocation.
