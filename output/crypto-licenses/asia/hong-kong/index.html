Overview

Why Hong Kong for Crypto?

Hong Kong's pivot to crypto regulation since 2022 is one of the most significant developments in global crypto policy. After years of restricting crypto access, the HKSAR government launched a comprehensive VASP licensing regime, positioning Hong Kong as Asia's leading regulated crypto hub alongside Singapore.

The regime, fully effective from June 2023, is mandatory for any platform operating a centralised virtual asset exchange serving Hong Kong clients. Unlike earlier opt-in frameworks, all VATPs must obtain SFC authorisation or cease operating. The SFC granted a transitional period for existing platforms operating before June 2023, with a deadline of 29 May 2024 for transitional arrangements.

Key advantages: zero capital gains tax, low territorial profits tax (16.5%), world-class financial infrastructure, proximity to mainland China markets (with important restrictions), and a bilingual (English/Chinese) legal system based on English common law.

Retail access unlocked

From June 2023, SFC-licensed VATPs may serve retail Hong Kong investors — a significant policy shift. Previously, crypto platforms could only access professional investors (HKD 8M+ portfolio). Retail access is subject to enhanced investor protection requirements including suitability assessments and token eligibility criteria.

Requirements

SFC VATP Licence Requirements

The SFC applies standards broadly equivalent to those for licensed securities brokers, adapted for virtual assets.

Hong Kong Substance

Must be incorporated in Hong Kong or a recognised jurisdiction with a substantive local presence. Board must include HK-resident responsible officers (ROs). No pure shell structures.

Responsible Officers (ROs)

Minimum 2 Responsible Officers — individuals approved by the SFC. ROs must have relevant financial/compliance experience and pass a fit-and-proper assessment. At least 1 must be active day-to-day.

Capital Requirements

HKD 5,000,000 (≈USD 640k) paid-up capital. Liquid assets ≥ 12 months of projected operating expenses at all times. Client assets must be segregated and held with licensed custodians.

AML/CFT Programme

Comprehensive AML/CFT policies compliant with HKMA and SFC guidelines. Travel Rule compliance (VASP-to-VASP transfers). MLRO with minimum 3 years relevant experience required.

Cybersecurity & Custody

At least 98% of client crypto assets in cold storage. Independent cybersecurity audit required annually. Insurance or equivalent financial backing for hot wallet assets.

Token Eligibility

Only tokens meeting SFC criteria may be listed for retail investors (currently limited to large-cap assets on approved indices). All new token listings require SFC pre-approval or compliance with published criteria.

Investor Protection

Suitability assessments for retail clients. Knowledge tests. Risk disclosures in Chinese and English. Cooling-off periods. Restrictions on leverage products for retail.

Business Continuity

Formal BCP/DR plan. Independent annual compliance audit. Regular reporting to SFC including financial returns, client asset reports, and incident notifications within 1 business day.

Step-by-Step Process

5 Steps to SFC VATP Approval

1
Weeks 1–8

Pre-Application Preparation

Engage Hong Kong legal counsel. Incorporate HK entity. Draft full suite of policies (AML, compliance, risk, cybersecurity, custody, token listing). Recruit and prepare Responsible Officers. The SFC expects near-final policies at submission — preparation is the longest phase.

2
Weeks 8–10

Pre-Submission Meeting with SFC

Optional but strongly recommended. SFC's Licensing Department will review your structure and flag issues before formal submission. Saves significant back-and-forth during formal review.

3
Weeks 10–12

Formal Application Submission

Submit via SFC's WINGS online system. Include: Form VA, business plan, financial projections, all policy documents, RO CVs and declarations, IT security assessment, and custody arrangements. Application fee: HKD 4,740.

4
Months 3–10

SFC Review & Due Diligence

SFC conducts detailed review including meetings with management, assessment of ROs, IT/cybersecurity audit review, and regulatory reference checks. Expect multiple rounds of questions and policy revisions. Maintain an internal project tracker for SFC queries.

5
Month 10–12

Approval & Licence Issuance

Upon satisfying all conditions, SFC issues the VATP licence. Conditions typically include: completion of independent cybersecurity audit, insurance confirmation, client asset segregation verification, and any outstanding staffing conditions.

Expedited processing is unavailable, though complete and well-prepared applications tend to receive faster approvals within the 3-month range.
Minimum paid-up capital requirements are HK$5,000,000 for a Type 1 (dealing in securities) or Type 7 (providing automated trading services) licence. For Type 4 (advising on securities), the minimum is HK$1,000,000. Capital must be held in a Hong Kong bank account and remain available for operational purposes.
Major banks accepting crypto-related accounts include HSBC, Hang Seng Bank, and Bank of China (Hong Kong), though most now require enhanced KYC documentation. Smaller banks like Fubon Bank and DBS Hong Kong have more flexible crypto-friendly policies. You should contact banks directly as policies change frequently; avoid relying on single-bank solutions.
You must appoint at least one full-time Compliance Officer with minimum 3 years' relevant experience and direct reporting to senior management. A Chief Executive Officer and Money Laundering Reporting Officer are mandatory. For larger operations, additional compliance staff may be required based on headcount and transaction volumes, typically 1 compliance officer per 50 employees.
The SFC does not maintain a whitelist, but you must conduct thorough assessment of each token's regulatory status and legitimate purpose before offering it. Tokens classified as securities require additional authorization, and tokens associated with sanctions, terrorism financing, or money laundering are prohibited. You must document your assessment framework and update it quarterly as regulations evolve.
Customer verification must comply with FATF Travel Rule standards and Hong Kong's AML Ordinance, requiring customer identification for transactions above HK$15,000. Enhanced Due Diligence is mandatory for high-risk customers and PEPs, with documentation retained for 5 years. Real-time transaction monitoring with automated alerts for suspicious patterns is mandatory for all licence holders.
Hong Kong's regulatory framework targets centralized operators; DEXs and fully decentralized protocols face legal uncertainty and generally do not require licensing if they have no Hong Kong operators or infrastructure. If you provide custody services or operate smart contracts from Hong Kong, you typically require a Type 4 or Type 1 licence. Hybrid models with some centralized elements trigger licensing requirements.
Professional indemnity insurance of minimum HK$3,000,000 is required for most licence types. Client assets must be segregated from operational funds and held with approved custodians or banks; commingled funds are prohibited. For digital asset custody, segregation must be recorded on-chain or with third-party proof, audited quarterly at minimum.
Annual licence fees to the SFC are HK$100,000-HK$300,000 depending on licence type and business volume. Ongoing compliance costs typically total HK$200,000-HK$600,000 annually, covering regulatory reporting, audits, and AML monitoring systems. Annual audits by external auditors cost HK$150,000-HK$400,000 depending on business complexity.
Staking and lending products may require a Type 4 (advising) or Type 9 (asset management) licence depending on how they are marketed and structured. If marketed as investment products with guaranteed returns, they trigger securities regulations requiring a Type 1 licence. You must obtain pre-approval from the SFC on product structure before launch; retroactive approval is not possible.
Breaches of licence conditions can result in fines up to HK$1,000,000 and licence suspension or revocation. False disclosures attract personal penalties for officers up to HK$500,000 and imprisonment. The SFC conducts unannounced audits; compliance violations discovered during audits typically result in enforcement action within 30-60 days of notification.
