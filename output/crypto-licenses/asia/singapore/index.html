Overview

Why a Singapore MAS Licence Matters

Singapore has built its reputation as Asia's most credible crypto regulatory hub through the Payment Services Act (PSA), first enacted in 2019 and significantly enhanced in 2023. Unlike many jurisdictions with lighter-touch registrations, the MAS PSA licence involves a genuinely rigorous review process — and that rigour is precisely what makes it valuable.

Holding a Singapore DPT (Digital Payment Token) service provider licence under the PSA communicates to institutional clients, banking partners, and investors that your business has passed one of Asia's most demanding regulatory assessments. This carries significant commercial weight when opening correspondent banking relationships, negotiating institutional partnerships, or raising regulated capital.

For businesses targeting Southeast Asian markets, institutional clients in the Asia Pacific region, or seeking to establish a credible Asian regulatory footprint alongside an EU licence, Singapore remains the premier choice — despite the higher bar and longer timeline compared with alternatives like Labuan or Thailand.

MAS expectations note: MAS is known for thorough review of applications and high expectations for key management's crypto expertise and Singapore residency. Applications submitted without a resident CEO or CTO with demonstrable DPT experience face significant delays or rejection. We strongly recommend substance planning before filing.

Licence Types

MPI vs SPI — Which Licence Do You Need?

Singapore's PSA creates two tiers of payment institution licence based on transaction volume thresholds. For crypto businesses, the relevant distinction is between a Standard Payment Institution (SPI) and a Major Payment Institution (MPI).

Criterion Standard Payment Institution (SPI) Major Payment Institution (MPI)
Monthly DPT transaction threshold Below SGD 3M per service Above SGD 3M per service (or SGD 6M total)
E-money float limit Below SGD 5M No limit
Min. base capital SGD 100,000 SGD 250,000
Security deposit (MAS) SGD 100,000 SGD 200,000
Safeguarding requirements Customer assets in trust Customer assets in trust (stricter rules)
Technology risk management Standard MAS guidelines Enhanced MAS Technology Risk Management guidelines
Best for Smaller exchanges, OTC desks, early-stage platforms Major exchanges, institutional brokers, high-volume operations

Process

How to Get a Singapore MAS Licence — Step by Step

1
Incorporate Singapore Pte. Ltd. & Build Substance

Register a Singapore private limited company (Pte. Ltd.) with ACRA. Critically, establish real substance: appoint a Singapore-resident CEO or senior executive with DPT expertise, secure physical office space in Singapore, and open a Singapore corporate bank account. MAS scrutinises substance heavily — nominee directors without active roles are a red flag.

Months 1–2
2
Develop Compliance & Governance Framework

Build a comprehensive compliance program: AML/CFT policies per MAS Notice PSN02, Technology Risk Management framework per MAS TRM Guidelines, DPT safeguarding procedures, customer complaint handling framework, and business continuity / disaster recovery plan. Conduct a gap analysis against all applicable MAS Guidelines and Notices for DPT service providers.

Months 2–4
3
Prepare and Submit PSA Application via MAS COSMIC

Submit the PSA licence application through MAS's COSMIC portal. The application requires: detailed business plan, financial projections (5 years), governance structure, fit-and-proper declarations for all key persons, AML/CFT program documentation, technology risk management framework, and customer safeguarding procedures. Application fee: SGD 1,000.

Month 4–5
4
MAS Review — Queries & In-Person Engagement

MAS conducts a thorough assessment over 6–12 months. Expect multiple rounds of clarification requests (CQs) on business model, risk management, and governance. MAS typically requests in-person interviews with the CEO and key management. Respond to all CQs comprehensively within stated deadlines — delays reset review timelines.

Months 5–12
5
Grant of Licence & Ongoing Obligations

Upon MAS approval, pay the annual licence fee (SGD 1,000–5,000 depending on services). Lodge the security deposit with MAS (SGD 100,000–200,000). Commence operations. Ongoing obligations include: quarterly MAS reporting, annual audit by MAS-registered auditor, annual AML/CFT review, Travel Rule compliance, and immediate notification of material changes.

Month 10–16+

FAQ

Singapore Crypto Licence — Common Questions

A Standard Payment Institution (SPI) is for businesses with monthly DPT transactions below SGD 3 million per service (and total monthly transactions below SGD 6 million). An MPI is required above these thresholds. Most operating crypto exchanges require an MPI. The MPI has higher capital requirements (SGD 250k vs SGD 100k), higher security deposit with MAS (SGD 200k vs SGD 100k), and more rigorous Technology Risk Management obligations. The application process and scrutiny level are broadly similar — but MAS expectations for MPI substance are higher.
MAS typically takes 6–12 months to review a complete PSA application for DPT services. Preparation of the application (substance establishment, documentation drafting) typically adds 3–4 months before filing. Total timeline from project start to licence grant: 9–16 months for a well-prepared application. Applications with insufficient substance or incomplete documentation take significantly longer. MAS has been known to take up to 24 months in complex cases.
The applicant entity must be a Singapore-incorporated Pte. Ltd. A foreign company must establish a Singapore subsidiary before applying. There is no restriction on foreign shareholder nationality. However, MAS places significant weight on local substance — a Singapore-resident CEO and/or senior compliance officer with relevant DPT expertise is effectively a practical requirement for MPI applications. Nominee-director-only structures will not satisfy MAS's fit-and-proper and substance expectations.
Yes. MAS mandates FATF Travel Rule compliance for DPT service providers under MAS Notice PSN02. VASPs must collect, verify, and transmit originator and beneficiary information for DPT transfers above SGD 1,500 (approximately USD 1,100). Integration with an approved Travel Rule solution (such as Notabene, Sygna, Elliptic, or others) is required. This must be operational at licence commencement.
Singapore's corporate tax rate is a flat 17% on chargeable income. New companies benefit from a partial tax exemption for the first 3 years: 75% exemption on the first SGD 100,000 and 50% exemption on the next SGD 100,000 of chargeable income. There is no capital gains tax in Singapore. GST (9%) applies to certain crypto services — DPT exchange for Singapore residents may be GST-exempt as a financial service; this should be confirmed for your specific business model.
Ongoing obligations include: quarterly MAS regulatory returns, annual audited financial statements by a MAS-registered auditor, annual AML/CFT independent review, Technology Risk Management annual assessment, Travel Rule compliance, safeguarding of customer assets (segregation and regular reconciliation), and immediate notification to MAS of any material changes to business, key personnel, or technology. Failure to meet ongoing requirements can result in licence suspension or revocation.
The application fee for a Direct Payment Token (DPT) licence is SGD 1,500, with annual renewal fees of SGD 1,000. However, most applicants budget between SGD 50,000 to SGD 150,000 for professional advisory, legal, and compliance setup costs. These figures can vary depending on your business model complexity and existing infrastructure.
You should secure relationships with at least one major Singapore bank or fintech-friendly financial institution before submitting your application, as MAS requires proof of adequate banking arrangements to support your operations. Banks like DBS, OCBC, and UOB have specific crypto divisions, though many require you to already have preliminary regulatory approval. It is advisable to begin discussions 2-3 months before your planned application submission.
MAS will provide written reasons for rejection, typically citing deficiencies in governance, AML/CFT controls, or operational readiness. You may request a meeting with MAS to discuss the decision and reapply within 6 months, though you will need to address the specific concerns raised. There is no formal appeals process, but companies have successfully reapplied after strengthening their compliance frameworks.
MAS does not maintain a whitelist of approved cryptocurrencies, but you must conduct proper due diligence on each token you intend to offer and ensure they do not violate securities or derivatives regulations. Stablecoins and wrapped tokens require additional scrutiny regarding reserve backing and issuer jurisdiction. Your compliance framework must document risk assessments for each cryptocurrency offered.
Singapore crypto licences must be renewed annually, with renewal applications typically submitted 30 days before the expiration date. The renewal process involves submitting updated financials, compliance certifications, and any material changes to your business operations. Failure to renew on time can result in licence suspension and regulatory penalties, so it is critical to maintain a renewal calendar.
Singapore remains more restrictive than the UAE but more business-friendly than Hong Kong for crypto companies, with clearer MAS guidance on operational standards and lower barriers to banking relationships. Unlike the UAE, Singapore requires stricter AML/CFT controls, while Hong Kong's regulations have become more burdensome following recent policy shifts. Singapore's tax efficiency on capital gains and grey market flexibility make it preferable for many trading and DeFi firms compared to Hong Kong.
