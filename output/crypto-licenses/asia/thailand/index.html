Thailand Crypto License: SEC Digital Asset Business 2026

Thailand's Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) regulates digital asset businesses under the Emergency Decree on Digital Asset Businesses B.E. 2561 (2018) — one of Asia's first dedicated crypto laws. The SEC licenses five categories: Digital Asset Exchanges, Brokers, Dealers, ICO Portal operators, and Digital Asset Fund Managers. Thailand has a large and growing crypto market with over 1 million registered crypto investors and clear regulatory frameworks providing legal certainty.

Thailand Crypto License — Common Questions

Thailand SEC issues five digital asset business licenses: Digital Asset Exchange (trading platform), Digital Asset Broker (trading on behalf of clients), Digital Asset Dealer (proprietary trading), ICO Portal operator (token issuance platform), and Digital Asset Fund Manager. Each has separate capital requirements and operational rules.
Minimum capital ranges from THB 5 million (Fund Manager) to THB 40 million (Exchange). All capital must be paid-up at the time of application. The SEC may require higher capital based on business model.
3–9 months depending on the license category and application quality. Exchange licenses take longer due to more complex system requirements. Well-prepared applications with clear technical documentation are processed faster.
Thailand has Foreign Business Act restrictions. Foreigners can typically hold up to 49% in Thai companies operating financial services, though BOI (Board of Investment) promotion can allow higher foreign ownership for qualifying businesses. Legal counsel is essential for ownership structure.
Thailand has a growing domestic crypto market, clear regulatory framework, and SEC that is actively developing crypto policy. The 20% corporate tax rate is competitive in Asia. Main challenges are foreign ownership restrictions and significant capital requirements for exchange licenses.
The Thailand SEC charges an application fee of approximately 500,000 THB (USD 14,000) plus ongoing annual fees ranging from 100,000-500,000 THB depending on your business type. Additional costs for legal consultation, compliance systems, and audit services typically add another 1-2 million THB to your first-year expenses. Total initial investment should be budgeted between 2-3 million THB (USD 55,000-85,000).
You must provide proof of capital, business plan, organizational structure, compliance policies, AML/KYC procedures, financial statements, board member backgrounds, office lease agreement, and detailed operational manuals. The SEC also requires evidence of cybersecurity measures, insurance policies, and copies of agreements with banks or payment service providers. All documents must be notarized and submitted in both English and Thai languages.
Thailand does not currently have a specific corporate tax exemption for crypto businesses, meaning standard corporate income tax of 20% applies to profits. This differs favorably from Malaysia (higher compliance costs) but less favorably than Singapore's potential tax incentives for licensed crypto firms. The Thai Revenue Department has been gradually clarifying crypto tax rules since 2024, with expectations of more specific guidance by 2026.
Siam Commercial Bank (SCB) and Bangkok Bank have established dedicated onboarding processes for SEC-licensed crypto operators as of 2025, though requirements remain stringent. Kasikornbank also accepts crypto clients but with higher due diligence requirements. Most banks require minimum monthly transaction volumes of 10 million THB and comprehensive compliance documentation before account opening.
The primary risks include stricter AML enforcement following Thailand's 2025 FATF mutual evaluation, potential sudden regulatory changes mirroring global trends, and cybersecurity audit failures. Additionally, the SEC can impose fines up to 5 million THB for compliance violations and can revoke licenses for inadequate customer verification or transaction monitoring. Regular third-party audits and maintaining updated compliance systems are essential to mitigate these risks.
Licenses must be renewed annually before the expiration date, typically in the month prior to your license anniversary. The renewal requires updated financial statements, compliance audit reports, board certifications, and payment of annual fees. The SEC typically processes renewals within 30-60 days if all documentation is complete and no compliance issues have been flagged during the previous year.
Thailand's SEC licensing is faster (4-6 months versus 6-12 months in Zug) and has lower upfront capital requirements, but Zug offers stronger regulatory clarity and global recognition as a crypto hub. Switzerland's licenses carry higher prestige for institutional clients and simpler cross-border operations within Europe, while Thailand licenses are better for accessing Southeast Asian markets. Thailand is ideal for startups with limited capital, while Zug suits established firms seeking international credibility.
