Philippines Crypto Licence: BSP VASP Registration 2026

The Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP), the Philippine central bank, regulates Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs) through Circular 1108 (2021) and subsequent guidelines. The Philippines has one of Asia's most active crypto adoption rates, with millions of Filipinos using crypto for remittances and payments. BSP VASP registration covers exchanges, custodians, and OTC desks — with a tiered approach based on transaction volumes. The Philippines offers access to 115 million people and a major global remittance corridor.

Philippines Crypto Licence — Common Questions

The Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) requires all Virtual Asset Service Providers operating in the Philippines to register under BSP Circular 1108 (2021). VASPs include crypto exchanges, custodians, and OTC desks. Registration involves BSP review of AML compliance, governance, and technical systems.
Class A VASPs handle lower transaction volumes (under PHP 100 million per day) and have lower capital requirements (PHP 10 million). Class B VASPs handle higher volumes and require PHP 50 million+ capital plus stricter operational requirements. Most new entrants start as Class A.
BSP VASP registration typically takes 1–6 months depending on completeness of application and business model complexity. Well-prepared applications with clear AML programmes are processed faster.
