Overview

Cape Verde as a Crypto Jurisdiction

The Republic of Cabo Verde (Cape Verde) is an archipelago of ten islands located in the central Atlantic Ocean, approximately 570 kilometres off the west coast of Africa. What the country lacks in size — around 600,000 people across its inhabited islands — it more than compensates for in strategic positioning, political stability, and institutional alignment with European standards.

Cape Verde consistently ranks among the most politically stable and democratically governed countries in Africa. It holds an EU Special Partnership — a unique bilateral arrangement that gives it closer economic and regulatory alignment with the European Union than any other non-candidate African country. The country uses the Cape Verdean escudo, which is pegged to the euro through a fixed exchange rate arrangement guaranteed by Portugal under a monetary cooperation agreement, providing currency stability unusual in Sub-Saharan Africa.

For crypto businesses, Cape Verde's significance in 2025 is primarily forward-looking. The BCV is actively working on a regulatory framework for virtual asset service providers, informed by European developments including MiCA and FATF guidance on virtual assets. When that framework launches, Cape Verde will offer something currently rare: a sub-Saharan African jurisdiction with credible EU-adjacent regulation, euro-linked currency stability, and a legal system rooted in Portuguese civil law with strong institutional integrity.

The opportunity for crypto businesses is to monitor framework development, build relationships with local counsel and regulatory contacts, and be positioned to apply promptly when the VASP framework is formalized — capturing first-mover advantage in a jurisdiction that is expected to attract considerable interest once regulation crystallizes.

Regulatory Development

BCV & AGMVM — Framework Timeline

Two regulatory bodies share oversight of Cape Verde's financial sector: the Banco de Cabo Verde (BCV), the central bank, oversees monetary policy, payments systems, and banking regulation. The AGMVM (Autoridade de Gestão de Mercados de Valores Mobiliários) regulates capital markets and securities. For crypto regulation, BCV is expected to take the primary role, particularly for payment-related VASPs, with AGMVM potentially relevant for tokenized securities or investment products.

Strategic Position

Why Cape Verde Matters for Crypto Businesses

Cape Verde's strategic relevance to the global crypto industry extends well beyond its domestic market size. It functions as a genuine Atlantic crossroads jurisdiction with institutional quality above its economic weight class.

The EU Special Partnership is the most significant structural differentiator. This bilateral agreement provides for regulatory coordination, economic integration discussions, and the potential for Cape Verde-licensed entities to benefit from facilitated market access to EU jurisdictions — something no other African country currently has in equivalent form. As MiCA creates a clear EU licensing regime, Cape Verde's alignment with EU regulatory standards positions it as a potential bridge jurisdiction between the EU single market and African crypto markets.

The country's Portuguese language status creates commercial access to a massive crypto-active Portuguese-speaking world: Brazil (one of the world's top 5 crypto markets by adoption), Portugal, Angola, and Mozambique. For a crypto business seeking to build a Portuguese-language presence with regulatory credibility, Cape Verde offers something no other Portuguese-speaking African jurisdiction currently does.

Tax Environment

Cape Verde Tax Overview for Crypto Businesses

Cape Verde is not a tax-optimized offshore jurisdiction — it has a standard corporate tax rate of 25% and operates a conventional tax system. This positions it differently from Anjouan, Seychelles, or other low-tax offshore options. However, for businesses seeking a credible, EU-aligned jurisdiction with a real regulatory environment rather than an offshore shell, 25% corporate tax is comparable to many European jurisdictions.

No specific crypto tax treatment exists currently. As of 2025, the BCV has not introduced capital gains tax or transaction tax provisions for crypto assets, though this will likely be addressed when the VASP framework is formalized. Given EU MiCA alignment aspirations, the tax treatment of crypto gains is expected to follow EU-adjacent principles rather than the aggressive gross-value DAT model seen in Kenya.

Cape Verde has double tax treaties with Portugal, which may be relevant for businesses with Portuguese operational links. The euro peg eliminates FX risk for EU-denominated operations, reducing one of the common complications of operating in African jurisdictions.

Market Context

Digital Economy & Crypto Market

Cape Verde's domestic crypto market is nascent, with limited data on adoption rates given its small population. However, the country has several structural characteristics that favor digital financial services growth. Remittance inflows are substantial — Cape Verde's diaspora, predominantly in Portugal, the Netherlands, and the United States, sends significant value annually, and crypto-based remittance represents a natural use case. Remittances account for roughly 12–15% of GDP.

The government has expressed interest in digital economy development as part of its economic diversification strategy away from tourism dependency. The Cape Verde Digital Economy Platform initiative and related government programs signal institutional appetite for fintech and digital financial infrastructure investment.

The growing digital nomad community on islands like Sal and Santiago (Praia) creates a small but internationally connected population with crypto familiarity, and the tourism sector's international nature means dollar and euro denomination is common — reducing local currency friction for crypto operations.

For businesses targeting the Portuguese-speaking Atlantic corridor — connecting West Africa, Europe, and Latin America — Cape Verde offers a uniquely positioned operational base. Once the regulatory framework launches, it is expected to attract interest from Brazilian, Portuguese, and Angolan crypto businesses seeking a regulated operational hub for their Atlantic-facing services.

For crypto businesses, this is significant because it suggests Cape Verde's VASP framework will be designed with MiCA and FATF compatibility in mind, rather than as a low-standard offshore regime.
