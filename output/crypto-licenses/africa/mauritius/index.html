Overview

Why Mauritius Is Africa's Premier Regulated Crypto Hub

Mauritius stands apart from every other African crypto jurisdiction through a combination of regulatory credibility, FATF compliance, functional banking, and a sophisticated double tax treaty network. While Seychelles attracts volume through low cost and speed, Mauritius attracts quality through institutional-grade regulation and tier-1 banking relationships.

The Financial Services Commission (FSC) of Mauritius has regulated international financial services — including fund administration, securities dealing, and insurance — for over two decades. It is a member of IOSCO (International Organisation of Securities Commissions) and IAIS, and operates under a governance framework aligned with international best practice. This institutional heritage gives FSC-licensed crypto businesses credibility with banks, institutional counterparties, and sophisticated clients that offshore licenses cannot match.

Not on FATF Grey List — Key Competitive Advantage

Mauritius is NOT on the FATF grey list. This is the single most important distinction between Mauritius and Seychelles for businesses that require traditional banking. FSC-licensed companies can access EUR/USD accounts with tier-1 banks including MCB (Mauritius Commercial Bank), AfrAsia Bank, and SBI Mauritius without the enhanced due diligence burden that grey-listed jurisdictions trigger.

Mauritius occupies a strategic geographic and economic position as the gateway between Africa and Asia. Its double tax treaty network of 43 countries — including India, China, France, South Africa, UAE, and Singapore — provides meaningful tax efficiency for cross-border operations. The Global Business Company (GBC) structure, which is the standard vehicle for international crypto businesses in Mauritius, benefits from an 80% partial exemption on foreign-source income, resulting in an effective corporate tax rate of approximately 3%.

The VAITOS Act 2021 (Virtual Asset and Initial Token Offering Services Act) established Mauritius as one of the first African jurisdictions with bespoke, dedicated crypto legislation. The Act introduced five distinct license categories covering the full spectrum of crypto financial services, allowing operators to obtain precisely-scoped regulatory authorisation rather than relying on a single catch-all license.

Legal Framework

VAITOS Act 2021 and Supporting Legislation

Mauritius crypto regulation rests on three primary legislative pillars: the VAITOS Act 2021, the Financial Services Act 2007, and the Financial Intelligence and Anti-Money Laundering Act (FIAMLA). Together they create a comprehensive, internationally-credible regulatory framework.

VAITOS Act 2021

Virtual Asset and Initial Token Offering Services Act — primary legislation governing crypto licensing. Defines five license categories, establishes FSC as licensing authority, mandates AML/CFT compliance, and sets out ongoing obligations for licensees. First enacted 2021; amended 2023.

Financial Services Act 2007

Governs the FSC's general powers of licensing, supervision, enforcement, and sanction. VASP licensees are regulated under the FSA as a licensed financial services category. Provides the FSC with inspection rights, information-gathering powers, and revocation authority.

FIAMLA — AML/CFT

Financial Intelligence and Anti-Money Laundering Act. VASP licensees are designated "reporting persons" under FIAMLA, requiring KYC/CDD, enhanced due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious transaction reporting to the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU).

GBC / Companies Act

Global Business Companies are incorporated under the Companies Act 2001 and licensed under the Financial Services Act. GBC1 status (now simply "Global Business Company") provides access to Mauritius double tax treaties. Standard corporate vehicle for international crypto operations.

VAITOS Act 2023 Amendments

The VAITOS Act was amended in 2023 to strengthen AML/CFT requirements, introduce Travel Rule obligations (for transfers above $1,000), and expand the FSC's supervisory powers. Licensees must maintain real-time transaction monitoring systems and submit annual compliance reports to the FSC.

Mauritius is a member of ESAAMLG (Eastern and Southern Africa Anti-Money Laundering Group), the FATF-style regional body for the region, and its AML/CFT framework is assessed as substantially compliant with FATF Recommendations. This contributes to its white-list status and banking-sector confidence.

License Types

Five Mauritius FSC VASP License Categories

Unlike jurisdictions with a single catch-all crypto license, Mauritius operates a differentiated, activity-specific licensing framework under the VAITOS Act. This allows businesses to obtain precisely-scoped authorisation and demonstrates a sophisticated regulatory approach.

VABD

Virtual Asset Broker-Dealer

Covers buying and selling of virtual assets as principal or agent. The most common license for spot exchange operations. Capital requirement: MUR 1,000,000 (~$22,000). Suitable for OTC desks, market makers, and spot trading platforms.

VAC

Virtual Asset Custodian

Covers safekeeping, administration, and control of virtual assets on behalf of third parties. Higher capital and security requirements — cold storage policies, insurance arrangements, and security audit documentation required. Essential for custodians and wallet providers.

VAM

Virtual Asset Marketplace

Covers operation of an exchange or marketplace where multiple buyers and sellers interact. The most comprehensive (and demanding) license for centralised exchange operators. Requires robust technology documentation, AML infrastructure, and higher capital buffers.

VAAdv

Virtual Asset Advisor

Covers provision of advice on virtual assets to clients — portfolio advice, investment strategy, due diligence. Lighter capital requirement than broker-dealer. Suitable for advisory boutiques, family offices, and research firms with crypto advisory operations.

VAMgr

Virtual Asset Manager

Covers discretionary management of virtual asset portfolios on behalf of clients. Relevant for crypto hedge funds, venture capital structures, and managed accounts. Higher AML burden — client onboarding, suitability assessments, and reporting requirements.

Combined

Combined Activities License

Operators conducting multiple regulated activities (e.g. broker-dealer + custodian, or marketplace + custodian) may apply for a combined license covering all activities. Single application. Fee and capital requirements reflect the most demanding activity licensed.

Tax Structure

Mauritius Tax: 3% Effective Rate via GBC

Mauritius applies a headline corporate tax rate of 15%. However, Global Business Companies (GBCs) engaged in international activities qualify for an 80% partial exemption on foreign-source income — reducing the effective tax rate to approximately 3%. This is the standard structure for international crypto businesses licensing in Mauritius.

The GBC partial exemption requires meeting substance requirements: economic substance in Mauritius, with genuinely managed-and-controlled criteria. In practice this means at least 2 Mauritius-resident directors, a physical office (or serviced office), and demonstrable local management activity. Substance requirements have been strengthened since 2019 OECD pressure on Mauritius.

Requirements

FSC VASP License Application Requirements

The FSC operates a thorough application process reflecting its status as a serious regulatory authority. Requirements are more demanding than Seychelles but significantly less demanding than EU or Singapore regulators.

GBC Incorporation

Incorporate a Global Business Company under the Companies Act 2001. Requires a Management Company (licenced by FSC) to act as company secretary and provide domicile services. GBC incorporation: 2–4 weeks.

Business Plan

Detailed business plan: services, target markets, revenue model, platform description, AML risk assessment, 3-year financial projections, and description of the technology infrastructure. Typically 30–60 pages.

AML/CFT Program

Comprehensive AML policy: KYC/CDD procedures for each client category, enhanced due diligence protocols, transaction monitoring system specification, Travel Rule compliance mechanism ($1,000+ threshold), and STR reporting process to FIU.

Fit & Proper — Key Persons

Directors, CEOs, compliance officers, and UBOs assessed. Required: notarised passport, proof of address, police clearance, detailed CV, 3 professional references, personal financial statement, and declaration of interests.

Capital Requirements

VABD: MUR 1,000,000 (~$22,000). VAC (Custodian): higher — typically MUR 3,000,000+. VAM (Exchange): typically MUR 5,000,000+. Capital must be held in a Mauritius bank account or demonstrate equivalent financial soundness.

Mauritius Substance

GBC substance requirements: minimum 2 Mauritius-resident directors, physical or serviced office address, local bank account, and demonstrable management in Mauritius. Annual substance confirmation required for GBC tax status maintenance.

Compliance Officer / MLRO

Designated Compliance Officer and Money Laundering Reporting Officer (can be same person). Must be sufficiently qualified and experienced. FSC may approve or reject the nominated CO/MLRO. Annual compliance reports required.

Technology & Cybersecurity

Platform architecture documentation, hot/cold wallet ratios (custodians), penetration testing reports, incident response plan, DR/BCP documentation, and third-party provider agreements. FSC increasingly scrutinises technology risk for exchange applicants.

Application Process

How to Obtain a Mauritius FSC VASP License: 6 Steps

1
Month 1

Incorporate Global Business Company (GBC)

Engage a licensed Management Company in Mauritius. The Management Company incorporates the GBC, provides registered address, and acts as company secretary. GBC incorporation typically takes 2–4 weeks. Obtain GBC Category 1 status from the FSC (required for treaty access and VASP licensing). Set up the statutory records, shareholder register, and appoint initial directors including at least 2 Mauritius residents.

2
Month 1–2

Pre-Application Consultation (Optional but Recommended)

The FSC offers informal pre-application guidance for VASP applicants. A meeting or correspondence with the FSC allows applicants to clarify the scope of the proposed license, confirm which license category applies, and receive guidance on specific documentation requirements. This step reduces the risk of an RFI or rejection at formal review stage and is strongly recommended for exchange (VAM) applicants.

3
Month 2–3

Prepare and Compile Application Package

Compile the full FSC application dossier: GBC corporate documents, business plan, AML/CFT program, fit-and-proper documentation for all key persons (notarised passport, police clearance, CVs, references, personal declaration), technology documentation, financial projections, ownership structure chart, and source of funds evidence. All documents in English; translations required for non-English materials.

4
Month 3–4

Submit Application and Pay Government Fee

Submit the completed application to the FSC via the Management Company, accompanied by the application fee ($3,500–$5,000 depending on license type). The FSC issues an acknowledgement and assigns a case officer. Application enters the formal review queue — initial completeness check typically 2–4 weeks. Incomplete applications are returned; complete applications enter substantive review.

5
Month 4–7

FSC Substantive Review and Due Diligence

The FSC conducts its full review: fit-and-proper assessment of all key persons (may include background checks and reference calls), AML program evaluation, business plan viability, technology documentation review, and capital adequacy assessment. The FSC typically issues 1–3 rounds of Requests for Information (RFIs). Prompt RFI responses are critical — delays extend the overall timeline significantly. Complex exchange (VAM) applications receive the most intensive review.

6
Month 7–8+

License Issued — Ongoing Obligations

The FSC issues the VASP license (VABD, VAC, VAM, VAAdv, or VAMgr). The company is listed on the FSC public register. Ongoing obligations: annual license renewal, annual compliance report, substance maintenance (Mauritius resident directors, local activity), AML monitoring, Travel Rule compliance, STR filing with FIU, and prompt notification of material changes to the FSC.

Banking

Banking for Mauritius Crypto Businesses

Banking access is Mauritius's key competitive advantage over Seychelles. FSC-licensed companies operate in a FATF-compliant jurisdiction, which removes the enhanced due diligence burden that grey-listed jurisdictions trigger.

MCB — Mauritius Commercial Bank

The largest bank in Mauritius. Provides multi-currency accounts (USD, EUR, GBP) to FSC-licensed entities. MCB has an established track record with crypto-related financial services businesses. Requires full AML documentation but does not routinely decline FSC-licensed crypto companies.

AfrAsia Bank

A regional bank with strong cross-border capability in Africa, India, and Asia. Known for nimble onboarding of international business clients including FSC-licensed entities. Provides trade finance, FX, and multi-currency banking solutions relevant to crypto businesses.

SBI Mauritius

State Bank of India's Mauritius subsidiary. Particularly relevant for businesses with India-linked operations, leveraging the India-Mauritius double tax treaty and established relationships with Indian financial institutions.

International Correspondent Banks

FATF-compliant status means Mauritius entities do not trigger enhanced scrutiny at correspondent bank level. USD and EUR correspondent banking is achievable. EU correspondent banks apply standard (not enhanced) due diligence to Mauritius entities, unlike grey-listed jurisdictions.
