Overview

What a Crypto Gambling Licence Covers

A crypto gambling licence authorises an online gambling platform to offer games of chance and skill — including casino games (slots, table games, live dealer), sports betting, poker, virtual sports, and lottery products — while accepting cryptocurrency as a payment method. The licence is issued by a gambling regulator, not a financial regulator, which means the primary compliance obligations relate to gaming integrity, player protection, and responsible gambling rather than capital adequacy or payment services rules.

The key distinction is that a gambling licence covers the gambling business; if your platform also operates as a crypto exchange (allowing players to convert fiat to crypto within your platform), you may need a separate VASP or payment institution licence depending on the jurisdiction. Many crypto casinos operate a "bring your own crypto" model — accepting crypto deposits from external wallets — which typically falls within the gambling licence's scope without requiring separate crypto licensing.

All gambling licences require AML/KYC compliance. FATF Recommendation 22 extends AML obligations to gambling operators, and most licensing jurisdictions require a written AML programme, customer due diligence procedures, and — for large players — enhanced due diligence including source of funds verification. Crypto acceptance introduces additional AML complexity because blockchain transactions are pseudonymous; Travel Rule considerations apply to licensed VASPs sending crypto to gambling platforms.

Crypto Integration

Accepting Cryptocurrency in a Licensed Casino

Accepting cryptocurrency in a licensed gambling operation introduces specific compliance considerations that traditional fiat-only casinos do not face. The pseudonymous nature of blockchain transactions creates AML risk — a licensed crypto casino must implement blockchain analytics tools to screen incoming deposits for high-risk wallet exposure, such as darknet market links or sanctioned addresses.

Most licensed crypto casinos accept Bitcoin, Ethereum, USDT, and other major cryptocurrencies directly from player wallets. The platform converts crypto to a functional currency (USD, EUR) for the purpose of game play and accounting, then pays out winnings in crypto at the current exchange rate. This "crypto-as-payment" model keeps the casino in a payment acceptance role rather than operating as a crypto exchange.

Blockchain analytics tools such as Chainalysis, Elliptic, or TRM Labs are increasingly expected by gambling regulators. Malta MGA has explicitly stated that licensed operators accepting crypto must implement appropriate screening. The AML programme must address crypto-specific risks including the source of funds for large crypto deposits.

Curaçao Reform 2023: Curaçao restructured its gambling regulation in 2023, replacing the master licence system with direct licensing by the new Gaming Control Board (GCB). Operators previously running under a master licence sublicence must apply for their own GCB licence by 2025. Cost and requirements have increased significantly under the new framework.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

A crypto gambling licence is a regulatory authorisation allowing an online gambling platform to accept cryptocurrency for casino games, sports betting, poker, and other gambling products. It is a gaming licence — not a crypto/VASP licence — issued by gambling regulators like Malta MGA, Gibraltar GRA, Isle of Man Gambling Commission, or Curaçao GCB.
The cheapest options are the Anjouan gaming licence (approximately $30,000–$50,000 all-in, 2–4 months) and Curaçao GCB licence (approximately $30,000–$100,000, 3–6 months). Both are significantly cheaper than Malta MGA (€100K+ year 1) or Gibraltar (£100K+ annual fee). However, Curaçao and Anjouan have lower international credibility with major payment processors and banking partners.
In most jurisdictions, a gambling licence covers the acceptance of crypto payments within the gambling context. However, if the platform also operates a crypto exchange or provides crypto transfer services outside the gambling context, a separate VASP licence may be required. The specific answer depends on your jurisdiction and operational model — always obtain legal advice tailored to your situation.
RNG (Random Number Generator) certification verifies that the random outcomes in casino games are genuinely random and not manipulated. Most gambling regulators require RNG certification from an accredited testing laboratory (eCOGRA, BMM Testlabs, GLI, or iTech Labs) before issuing or renewing a gambling licence. Certification must typically be renewed whenever the gaming software is updated.
Accepting EU players depends on both the gambling licence and the laws of each EU member state. Malta MGA provides the strongest EU player coverage as an EU member state regulator. Curaçao and Anjouan licences do not confer EU player access rights. EU countries like Germany, Netherlands, and Sweden require their own national gambling licences to service residents. Operating a Curaçao-licensed site targeting EU players without national licences risks enforcement action.
The application process typically takes 3-6 months from initial submission to final approval, depending on the complexity of your business model and the completeness of your documentation. The Swiss gaming regulator (cantonal authorities) may request additional information or clarifications, which can extend the timeline. Express processing is not available, but early engagement with licensing consultants can streamline the review process.
Initial licensing costs range from CHF 50,000 to CHF 250,000 depending on the canton and your gaming offerings, while annual renewal fees typically cost CHF 20,000 to CHF 80,000. Additional compliance costs include RNG certification (CHF 10,000-30,000), annual audits (CHF 15,000-40,000), and legal/consulting fees (CHF 30,000-100,000 for the first year). Total first-year investment should be budgeted at CHF 150,000 to CHF 500,000.
Most traditional Swiss banks remain reluctant to serve crypto gambling platforms, but specialized banks like Sygnum, Crypto Finance, and certain regional cantonal banks have begun offering accounts to licensed operators. You must present your valid gambling licence and complete enhanced due diligence procedures. Banking relationships often require proof of robust AML/KYC systems and compliance with FINMA guidelines.
Required documents typically include: detailed business plan, beneficial ownership declaration, source of funds verification, anti-money laundering procedures, responsible gaming policies, technical specifications of your platform, RNG certification, proof of financial capability, and personal background checks for all directors and senior management. Each canton may have slightly different requirements, so consulting with local authorities before submission is essential.
Crypto gambling operators are subject to cantonal gaming taxes (typically 10-20% of gross gaming revenue), corporate income tax at federal and cantonal levels, and VAT considerations where applicable. Each canton sets its own tax rates, with Zug and Vaud offering more competitive structures for licensed operators. You should engage a tax advisor familiar with crypto gaming to structure your entity optimally and ensure compliance with Swiss tax authorities.
Operating without a licence exposes you to significant penalties including fines up to CHF 5 million, criminal prosecution of company officers, immediate cessation of operations orders, asset seizure, and permanent barring from obtaining a licence in the future. Switzerland actively prosecutes unlicensed gaming operators, and FINMA coordinates with cantonal authorities to identify and shut down illegal platforms. Additionally, your company faces civil liability and reputational damage that makes future banking and business relationships extremely difficult.
Crypto gambling licences in Switzerland require annual renewal with submission of updated compliance reports, financial statements, and continued adherence to regulatory standards. Significant changes such as modifications to game offerings, ownership changes exceeding 20%, relocation, major platform technology updates, or changes in beneficial owners trigger immediate notification and potential re-evaluation by authorities. Failure to report changes can result in licence suspension or revocation.
