Overview

Bulgaria FSC: The Cost-Efficient EU Hub

Bulgaria's Financial Supervision Commission (FSC — Комисия за финансов надзор) is the competent authority for investment firms, insurance, pension funds, and capital markets in Bulgaria. As an EU member state since 2007, Bulgaria fully transposes EU financial services legislation including MiFID II, EMIR, MAR, and the 5th AML Directive. An FSC investment firm license is therefore a genuine EU credential, carrying the same passporting rights as a CySEC, BaFin, or AFM license.

Bulgaria's competitive advantage is straightforward: it offers full EU regulatory status at the lowest operational cost in the EU. The 10% flat corporate income tax is the lowest in the European Union — no other EU member state offers a lower headline corporate tax rate. Sofia has a growing fintech and financial services sector, an English-speaking professional talent pool, and office costs that are 60–70% lower than comparable cities in Cyprus, Malta, or the Netherlands.

The FSC regulates under the Markets in Financial Instruments Act (MFIA — Закон за пазарите на финансови инструменти), which directly transposes MiFID II into Bulgarian law. Investment firms authorized by the FSC may passport their services across all 30 EEA states by filing a standard notification with the FSC, which then notifies the host member state regulator.

Why Bulgaria Over Cyprus?

CySEC (Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission) is the traditional choice for EU forex licensing, but Bulgaria offers several advantages: faster application timelines (6–9 months versus 9–12 months for CySEC), lower operating costs, a less saturated regulatory environment (fewer applicants means more attention from FSC staff), and a lower corporate tax rate (10% versus Cyprus's 12.5%). For brokers not requiring the specific prestige of a Cyprus license, Bulgaria represents superior value.

MiFID II Framework

Investment Firm Categories and Capital Tiers

Under MiFID II as transposed in Bulgaria, investment firms are categorized by the services they provide and the associated capital requirements:

Category 1 — Full Service

€730,000

Deals on own account, portfolio management, underwriting. Market maker / STP brokers often require this tier.

Category 2 — Agency Broker

€125,000

Receives and transmits orders, executes on behalf of clients. Suitable for pure agency model forex brokers.

Category 3 — Advisory Only

€75,000

Investment advice only — no execution. Rarely sufficient for active forex broker operations.

Ongoing Capital Ratio

IFR/IFD rules

Investment Firms Regulation requires ongoing capital adequacy reporting.

Practical Note: Most forex brokers operating as market makers require €730,000 initial capital. Pure agency (A-Book) brokers who do not hold client money directly may qualify for the €125,000 tier. Legal advice on the appropriate tier for your specific business model is essential before application.

EU Passporting

30 EEA States: Full Passporting Rights

MiFID II passporting is one of the most valuable features of EU investment firm licensing. An FSC Bulgaria authorization enables a firm to provide investment services in any of the 30 EEA member states (the 27 EU member states plus Iceland, Liechtenstein, and Norway) without requiring separate local authorization in each country.

Passporting is achieved through a formal notification procedure. The FSC submits a passport notification to the host member state's regulator on behalf of the firm. For freedom of services passporting (cross-border, without a permanent establishment), the process typically takes 1–2 months. For freedom of establishment passporting (opening a branch), the timeline is 2–3 months.

  • Access to all 27 EU member states plus Iceland, Liechtenstein, and Norway
  • Serve retail and professional clients across the EEA without local licenses
  • Open branches in host member states via passport notification
  • Accept EU institutional counterparty business (ECPs) under MiFID II
  • Access EU retail trading venue connectivity and clearing infrastructure
  • Market financial products under EU product governance (PRIIPS/KID) framework

Application Process

Step-by-Step: FSC Bulgaria License Application

1

Pre-Application Meeting with FSC

Request an informal consultation with FSC's Investment Supervision Division. Present your business model, ownership structure, and intended investment services. The FSC provides guidance on the appropriate license category and documentation requirements.

2–4 weeks
2

Incorporate Bulgarian Entity

Register an OOD (LLC) or AD (JSC) with the Bulgarian Commercial Register. Minimum capital must be deposited in a Bulgarian bank account. Appoint directors who pass FSC fit-and-proper criteria. The Commercial Register process takes 3–5 business days.

1–2 weeks
3

Prepare Application Documentation

Compile FSC application package: business plan with 3-year financials, organizational chart, internal control policies, risk management framework, AML/CFT program (5th Directive compliant), IT system descriptions, officer CVs and criminal record certificates from all relevant jurisdictions, and ownership structure with UBO declaration.

4–8 weeks
4

Submit Application to FSC

File complete application with FSC, paying the €2,500 state fee. The FSC checks completeness and formally accepts the application within 10 business days. The 6-month statutory review period begins from formal acceptance.

Submission event
5

FSC Review — Questions and Responses

The FSC reviews all documentation and submits written questions. Respond within specified deadlines (typically 14 calendar days per question round). The FSC may conduct interviews with proposed key officers.

3–6 months
6

License Granted — Passport and Launch

FSC issues the investment firm license. File passport notifications for target EU/EEA markets. Establish banking relationships, connect liquidity providers, and implement all ongoing regulatory reporting obligations.

1–2 months post-approval
