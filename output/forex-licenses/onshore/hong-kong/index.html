Overview

SFC Type 3 and Leveraged Forex Trading in HK

The Securities and Futures Commission (SFC) is Hong Kong's primary financial markets regulator, overseeing securities, futures, and leveraged forex activities. Type 3 (Leveraged Foreign Exchange Trading) is the specific SFC licence category covering OTC forex with leverage, as defined in the Securities and Futures Ordinance (SFO) and the Leveraged Foreign Exchange Trading (Arbitration) Ordinance (LFETRSO).

Hong Kong is Asia's leading international financial center, consistently ranked among the world's top three global financial hubs alongside London and New York. The HK banking and financial system is internationally integrated, with HKD freely convertible and major global banks maintaining principal operations in the city. For forex brokers, Hong Kong provides access to Greater China institutional relationships, a large population of sophisticated investors, and a common law legal system with strong contract enforceability.

The SFC maintains a Register of Licensed Persons and Registered Institutions that is publicly searchable. An SFC Type 3 licence on the public register provides immediate verification of regulatory status to counterparties, prime brokers, and institutional clients — critical for building trust in Asian markets where SFC regulation is the recognized gold standard for financial service providers.

Capital Requirements

Capital Adequacy & Financial Resources Rules (FRR)

SFC Type 3 licensees are subject to the Financial Resources Rules (FRR), which impose ongoing capital adequacy requirements in addition to the initial minimum paid-up capital:

Requirement Amount Frequency Notes
Minimum paid-up capital HK$5,000,000 At licensing Cannot be withdrawn below this threshold
Minimum liquid capital (FRR) HK$3,000,000 Ongoing daily Or 5% of total liabilities, whichever is higher
Liquid capital trigger ratio 120% of FRR requirement Monitoring threshold Must notify SFC if breached
Client money requirement 100% segregated Daily reconciliation Held in SFC-approved trust accounts

FRR buffer: SFC expects licensees to maintain liquid capital significantly above the FRR minimum. A firm operating at exactly the minimum threshold will trigger enhanced SFC supervision. Best practice is to maintain 150–200% of the FRR minimum.

Requirements

Responsible Officers & Compliance Framework

  • Minimum 2 SFC-approved Responsible Officers (ROs) for Type 3 activity, with at least 1 executive RO
  • ROs must have relevant qualifications (SFC licensing examinations or equivalents) + 3 years experience
  • Hong Kong company incorporation (limited company) — overseas branches require HKMA approval
  • At least one director resident in Hong Kong — ROs must be accessible to SFC
  • SFC-registered compliance officer and Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO)
  • AML/CFT procedures compliant with AMLO (Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Ordinance)
  • Client money trust account at SFC-approved financial institution
  • Daily client money reconciliation and monthly reporting to SFC
  • Financial Resources Rules (FRR) — daily liquid capital calculation
  • Monthly FRR return to SFC; immediate notification if liquid capital falls below trigger level
  • Annual audit by SFC-recognized auditor
  • Internal audit function with annual internal audit report to board
  • Complaint handling procedures meeting SFC requirements
Application Process

Step-by-Step SFC Type 3 Application

1
Hong Kong Company Incorporation
Incorporate a Hong Kong limited company with the Companies Registry. Open HK corporate bank accounts. Prepare corporate governance structure, appoint directors, and identify Responsible Officers. Engage SFC-experienced legal counsel. Deposit initial paid-up capital of at least HK$5,000,000.
Month 1–2
2
Responsible Officer Preparation
Ensure Responsible Officers have completed required SFC licensing examinations (Licensing Exam papers 1, 3, and 8 for Type 3). Prepare detailed CVs and qualifications evidence for each RO. Submit Individual Licence Applications (Form LC-4) for each Responsible Officer to SFC alongside the corporate application.
Month 1–3
3
SFC Application Submission (WINGS Portal)
Submit corporate licence application via SFC's WINGS (Web-Based Integrated New Generation System) portal. Submit Form LC-3 (corporate applicant), supporting business plan, compliance manual, AML procedures, financial projections showing FRR compliance, and full KYC on beneficial owners and directors.
Month 3
4
SFC Review & Fit-and-Proper Assessment
SFC reviews the application, conducts fit-and-proper assessments for all Responsible Officers and directors, and reviews business plan viability. SFC may issue requisitions requesting additional information. Regulatory interviews of key personnel may be conducted. Criminal record checks and HKMA/overseas regulator checks on individuals are routine.
Month 4–10
5
Licence Grant & Commencement
SFC grants the Type 3 licence and individual Responsible Officer licences simultaneously. Annual licence fees are payable. Commence LFET operations per approved business plan. First monthly FRR return due at the end of the first operating month. Establish SFC-compliant client money trust accounts before accepting client funds.
Month 6–12

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

SFC requires a minimum paid-up capital of HK$5,000,000. Additionally, licensees must comply with the Financial Resources Rules (FRR), which require maintaining liquid capital of at least HK$3,000,000 or 5% of total liabilities (whichever is higher) on an ongoing basis. Market makers or firms dealing with significant client assets may face higher FRR requirements in practice.
SFC Type 3 covers Leveraged Foreign Exchange Trading (LFET) — the regulated activity of offering leveraged OTC forex trading contracts to clients in Hong Kong. Any company offering forex CFDs, spot forex with leverage, or rolling spot contracts to retail or professional clients in Hong Kong must hold a Type 3 licence. Forex dealing between licensed banks (HKMA-regulated) does not require SFC Type 3.
SFC requires each licensed corporation to appoint at least 2 Responsible Officers (ROs) for each regulated activity, with at least 1 RO being an executive director. ROs must be SFC-approved, pass the SFC's fit-and-proper assessment, hold relevant qualifications (SFC licensing examinations), and have at least 3 years of relevant industry experience. ROs bear personal regulatory responsibility and can be individually sanctioned.
SFC's Client Money Rules require Type 3 licensees to segregate all client funds in designated client trust accounts at SFC-approved financial institutions. Client money cannot be commingled with the firm's own assets. Daily reconciliation is required, with monthly reporting to SFC. Any interest earned on client trust accounts must be allocated to clients in accordance with the client agreement.

The SFC application fee is HKD 15,000, with additional costs typically ranging from HKD 200,000 to HKD 500,000 for professional advisory services, compliance setup, and initial operational expenses. Timeline for approval is usually 6-9 months from submission, depending on the completeness of your application and SFC's review workload. Total first-year costs often reach HKD 800,000 to HKD 1.5 million when including staff salaries, premises, and technology infrastructure.
Annual renewal fees to the SFC are HKD 15,000, but total compliance costs including compliance officer salaries, audit fees, and regulatory consulting typically range from HKD 300,000 to HKD 600,000 per year. You must also conduct annual audits by an SFC-approved auditor, which costs approximately HKD 50,000 to HKD 150,000 depending on your business volume. Additional costs arise from mandatory continuing professional education for your responsible officers and periodic fit-and-proper assessments.
Hong Kong offers stronger regulatory prestige and access to Asian markets, while Cyprus provides easier access to European clients and typically lower operational costs (approximately EUR 50,000 annually versus HKD 400,000+). Hong Kong requires higher initial capital (HKD 3 million minimum) and stricter due diligence on beneficial owners, whereas Cyprus allows more flexible ownership structures. Hong Kong's approval timeline is 6-9 months compared to Cyprus's typical 3-4 months, but Hong Kong carries greater international credibility in institutional finance.
You must establish segregated client money accounts with an authorized institution in Hong Kong, separate from your operational accounts, as mandated by SFC rules. Most licensed banks require minimum deposits of HKD 1-5 million and conduct enhanced due diligence on beneficial owners, which can take 4-8 weeks. You'll also need to maintain detailed reconciliation records monthly and obtain an auditor's report confirming proper segregation of client funds annually.
Essential documents include detailed business plans, organizational structure charts, CVs and police clearance certificates for all responsible officers, proof of capital adequacy calculations, and financial projections for three years. You must also submit risk management policies, anti-money laundering procedures, complaint handling procedures, and proof of suitable office premises in Hong Kong. The SFC requires certified copies of incorporation documents, shareholder registers, beneficial ownership declarations, and professional indemnity insurance certificates.
Hong Kong offers significant advantages with a flat 16.5% corporate tax rate on profits and a territorial tax system meaning only Hong Kong-sourced income is taxed. If your clients are primarily overseas, most trading revenues may qualify as non-Hong Kong source income and face no local tax. However, you must carefully structure operations to ensure compliance with IRAS (Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore) and HMRC regulations if you have significant operations in those jurisdictions.
The SFC requires licence renewals annually, with applications typically submitted 60 days before expiration; the review period is 4-6 weeks for compliant applicants. Missing the renewal deadline results in automatic suspension of your ability to conduct regulated activities, which can expose you to penalties up to HKD 1 million and potential criminal prosecution. You must continue meeting all capital and compliance requirements without interruption, even during the renewal period, or face immediate enforcement action by the SFC.
