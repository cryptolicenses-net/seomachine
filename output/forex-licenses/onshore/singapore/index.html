Overview

MAS CMS Licence for Forex Dealing

The Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) is Singapore's central bank and integrated financial regulator. For forex brokers, MAS issues Capital Markets Services (CMS) Licences under the Securities and Futures Act 2001 (SFA). The relevant regulated activity for OTC forex is "Dealing in Capital Markets Products" which includes over-the-counter foreign exchange contracts, futures, and leveraged currency trading.

Singapore consistently ranks among the world's top three forex trading centers alongside London and New York, according to BIS Triennial Central Bank Surveys. The city-state's position as Asia's financial hub is underpinned by its stable regulatory environment, strong rule of law, sophisticated banking infrastructure, and strategic geographic position connecting Asian and European/North American trading sessions.

MAS is known for applying one of the world's most rigorous licensing processes — the regulator conducts extensive background checks on beneficial owners, reviews business model viability in detail, and expects applicants to demonstrate comprehensive knowledge of regulatory requirements before authorization is granted. This rigor is precisely what makes a MAS CMS Licence so valuable: it signals to institutional counterparties, prime brokers, and clients that the operator has been thoroughly vetted.

Accredited Investor definition: Under MAS rules, an Accredited Investor is an individual with net personal assets exceeding S$2,000,000 or net financial assets exceeding S$1,000,000, or annual income of at least S$300,000. Institutional investors include licensed banks, insurers, and funds.

Requirements

MAS Compliance & Ongoing Requirements

  • Singapore-incorporated company (Pte Ltd or public company) — no foreign branches
  • At least 2 Singapore-resident directors, with at least 1 executive director
  • CEO/MD must be Singapore-resident and MAS-approved
  • Representative's Licence required for each dealer conducting regulated activities
  • Compliance officer and AML Compliance Officer appointed (Singapore-based)
  • MAS Notice SFA04-N02: Business Conduct obligations for CMS Licence holders
  • MAS Notice SFA04-N07: Prevention of Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing
  • Client money held in separate trust accounts (MAS Notice SFA04-N01)
  • Financial Resources Rules (FRR) — maintain 120% of operational risk requirement
  • Quarterly and annual financial returns to MAS via MASNET
  • Incident reporting: material operational incidents within 14 days
  • Annual AML/CFT return and independent AML audit every 3 years
Application Process

Step-by-Step MAS CMS Application

1
Singapore Company Incorporation & Structure
Incorporate a Singapore Pte Ltd with ACRA. Appoint Singapore-resident directors and CEO. Open Singapore corporate bank account. Engage MAS-specialist counsel. Identify and appoint Compliance Officer. All beneficial owners (5%+ shareholding) must be disclosed to MAS.
Month 1–2
2
Documentation Preparation
Prepare business plan and financial projections (3 years), AML/CFT program, IT risk assessment, compliance manual, and governance framework. Compile KYC for all directors, shareholders, and key personnel. Prepare capital adequacy projections demonstrating FRR compliance on an ongoing basis.
Month 2–3
3
MASNET Application Submission
Submit CMS Licence application through MAS's MASNET portal. Pay application fee (S$1,000). Submit Form 1 (entity application), Form 4 (Director/CEO approval), and individual Representative's Licence applications for each designated dealer. MAS acknowledges receipt within 2 weeks.
Month 3
4
MAS Due Diligence Review
MAS conducts comprehensive review including background checks on beneficial owners through international databases, business model viability assessment, and detailed review of compliance systems. MAS may request additional documentation, conduct interviews with key personnel, or request clarifications. Respond promptly and comprehensively.
Month 4–10
5
CMS Licence Grant
MAS grants the CMS Licence and Representative's Licences simultaneously. Conditions of authorization are specified. Annual licence fees are payable. Commence operations per approved business plan. First quarterly financial return due 30 days after the end of the first business quarter.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

MAS requires minimum base capital of S$1,000,000 for a CMS Licence holder dealing in capital markets products (including OTC forex) to retail clients. Institutional-only forex dealers may qualify for a lower S$250,000 base capital threshold. Additionally, MAS requires ongoing financial resources adequacy at 120% of the operational risk requirement, calculated quarterly.
A full CMS Licence permits dealing with retail, accredited, and institutional investors. A restricted CMS Licence for accredited/institutional clients only may qualify for lower capital (S$250,000) and reduced conduct obligations. Retail forex dealing requires the full licence, S$1M+ capital, and compliance with MAS conduct rules including suitability assessments and risk disclosures.
MAS is one of the world's most thorough regulators. The licensing review covers beneficial ownership background checks (including global database searches), business model viability assessment, IT systems security review, AML/CFT risk assessment, and governance quality evaluation. MAS expects applicants to demonstrate deep knowledge of SFA requirements. Applications with incomplete criteria or unclear beneficial ownership are routinely rejected.
There is no formal ASEAN-wide financial services passport. However, Singapore has bilateral recognition agreements with certain jurisdictions, and MAS-licensed entities are recognized as reputable counterparties across the ASEAN region. For retail forex marketing in other ASEAN countries, separate local licenses are typically required (Thailand's SEC, Malaysia's SC, Indonesia's OJK).
Ongoing obligations include: quarterly financial returns to MAS via MASNET, annual audited financial statements, AML/CFT returns, incident reporting within 14 days of material operational events, ongoing FRR adequacy monitoring, client money trust account reconciliations, and maintenance of MAS-approved directors and CEO. Annual AML audit required every 3 years by independent auditors.
The application fee is SGD 1,500, but total costs typically range from SGD 50,000 to SGD 150,000 when including legal fees, compliance setup, and initial capital requirements. Costs vary significantly based on your business model, whether you need prime office space in Singapore, and the complexity of your compliance infrastructure. We recommend budgeting an additional SGD 20,000-30,000 annually for ongoing compliance and regulatory reporting.
The MAS typically takes 6 to 12 months to review and approve a CMS Licence application, though this can extend to 18 months if additional information is requested. The timeline begins after you submit a complete application and depends on the thoroughness of your submission and MAS's current review queue. Engaging experienced compliance consultants can reduce delays by ensuring your application meets all requirements on the first submission.
Core documents include detailed business plans, financial projections for three years, organizational charts, CVs of key personnel with financial services experience, director and shareholder profiles, compliance policies, and risk management frameworks. You must also provide proof of premises in Singapore, bank references, and audited financial statements if applicable. The MAS Guidance Note 2 (Capital Adequacy) and Guidance Note 3 (Internal Control) outline the specific documentation standards required.
Singapore operates a territorial tax system, so forex trading profits are taxable at the corporate tax rate of 17% (as of 2026), though certain incentives may apply for approved financial services. You may qualify for tax exemptions on foreign-sourced income if you meet specific criteria under Singapore's tax laws. We recommend consulting with a Singapore tax advisor early in your licensing process, as proper structuring can significantly optimize your tax position.
Tier-1 banks like DBS, OCBC, and UOB typically work with licensed forex operators, though they conduct stringent due diligence given increased regulatory scrutiny post-2023. Smaller banks and digital banking partners may offer faster onboarding but with higher fees. We recommend securing a banking relationship during your application process, as MAS approval may be contingent on demonstrating access to a regulated bank account.
A UK FCA Authorisation typically costs GBP 5,000-10,000 in application fees but total setup costs range from GBP 150,000-300,000, with approval timelines of 4-8 months in 2026. Singapore's MAS Licence has lower initial fees but comparable total costs and slightly longer timelines, though Singapore offers easier regional expansion within ASEAN. Both jurisdictions require similar capital adequacy standards, but Singapore's process is generally more predictable and faster if documents are well-prepared.
The MAS requires annual renewal, and failure to submit renewal applications 60 days before expiration can result in license suspension and significant regulatory penalties. Operating without a valid license is illegal and exposes you to enforcement action, substantial fines, and reputational damage that may prevent future licensing. We strongly recommend implementing a compliance calendar with 90-day renewal reminders to avoid any lapses in your authorization.
