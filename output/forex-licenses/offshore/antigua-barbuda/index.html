Overview

Why Antigua & Barbuda for Forex Licensing?

Antigua and Barbuda has operated as an international financial services center since the late 1980s. The Financial Services Regulatory Authority (FSRA) — previously known as the Financial Services Commission — oversees offshore brokers, insurance companies, and banking entities under the International Business Corporations Act.

The jurisdiction offers a genuine regulatory license with fit-and-proper requirements for directors, an AML framework aligned with FATF standards, and regular FSRA oversight — placing it a notch above pure registration jurisdictions like SVG. The $100,000 minimum capital requirement, 2–3 month processing timeline, and zero tax on foreign income make Antigua a cost-effective option for serious startup brokers.

Antigua has benefited from ECCU (Eastern Caribbean Currency Union) membership and CARICOM trade relationships, giving licensed entities access to a range of Eastern Caribbean banking facilities. The English common law legal system provides predictable contractual frameworks for global broker operations.

The standard application-to-approval timeline is 6-12 weeks from submission of a complete application package to the FSRA. Antigua requires minimum paid-up capital of $100,000 USD for a standard Forex dealing license, though higher amounts ($250,000+) are recommended for operational credibility and banking relationships. Capital must be held in an approved local or international bank account and verified through audited financial statements. The FSRA may request additional capitalization if the broker's projected client volumes exceed initial estimates.
Core requirements include: completed application forms, business and compliance plans, director/beneficial owner background documentation, proof of capital funding, bank reference letters, and audited financial statements for the previous 2 years (if applicable). You'll also need articles of incorporation, an AML/CFT policy framework, risk management procedures, and proof of office premises in Antigua and Barbuda. A personal declaration of no criminal convictions and professional references strengthen the application.
Antigua offers favorable corporate tax treatment with no income tax on profits derived from offshore financial services when structured correctly. However, you must maintain compliance with FSRA substance requirements and file annual tax returns with the Antigua and Barbuda Inland Revenue Department. Professional tax advisory is recommended, as tax obligations vary significantly based on where your clients are located and your company's ultimate beneficial ownership.
The FSRA typically provides 30-90 days notice before implementing new compliance requirements, allowing licensees time to adjust operational procedures. Antigua has modernized its framework twice since 2023, and additional updates are expected in 2026 related to cryptocurrency and ESG standards. Failure to implement required changes by the deadline can result in warnings, fines, or license suspension, making active monitoring of FSRA communications essential.

Antigua licenses are particularly advantageous for brokers targeting emerging markets and jurisdictions outside strict EU regulation, whereas Cyprus/Malta licenses provide better institutional banking partnerships and EU client acquisition.
