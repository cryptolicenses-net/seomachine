Overview

Why Vanuatu for Forex Broker Licensing?

Vanuatu has been a recognized offshore forex licensing jurisdiction since the early 2000s. The Vanuatu Financial Services Commission (VFSC) operates under the Financial Dealers Licensing Act and the Vanuatu Financial Services Commission Act, providing a regulatory framework that includes mandatory annual audits, AML/CFT compliance, and ongoing oversight — placing Vanuatu clearly above pure registration jurisdictions like SVG or Marshall Islands.

The key differentiator between Vanuatu and SVG is regulatory substance. SVG explicitly states it does not regulate forex; VFSC actively licenses, monitors, and can revoke forex dealer licenses. This difference matters: VFSC-licensed brokers have access to a significantly wider range of PSPs, payment processors, and liquidity providers than SVG entities.

Vanuatu's zero-tax regime covers all income derived from foreign sources — corporate tax, capital gains, and withholding taxes are all zero for international business activities. Combined with low operational costs (a Port Vila registered office is affordable), Vanuatu offers one of the best cost-to-credibility ratios in offshore forex licensing.

  • Genuine VFSC regulatory oversight — not just company registration
  • $50,000 minimum capital — lower barrier than Belize or Bahamas

Banking

Banking for Vanuatu-Licensed Forex Brokers

Vanuatu has a functional local banking sector including ANZ Vanuatu, Bred Bank Vanuatu, and BSP Financial Group. Local accounts in USD are available to VFSC-licensed entities and are used primarily for operating expenses and regulatory capital maintenance.

For client funds and primary forex operations, VFSC brokers typically supplement local banking with international accounts in Mauritius (SBM, AfrAsia Bank), Georgia (TBC Bank, Bank of Georgia), or through EMI solutions. The VFSC license provides adequate credibility for most second-tier PSPs and regional payment processors serving Asia-Pacific, MENA, and African markets.

Annual Compliance

Ongoing VFSC Compliance Requirements

VFSC-licensed brokers must maintain the following annual compliance obligations:

  • Annual license renewal (due before expiry; VFSC fee $5,000–$8,000)
  • Audited financial statements filed within 90 days of fiscal year-end
  • Annual AML/CFT program review and update
  • MLRO annual report submitted to VFSC
  • Ongoing KYC maintenance for all clients (CDD/EDD records)
  • Transaction monitoring records maintained for 7 years
  • Notification to VFSC of any material changes (directors, ownership, activities)

Required documents include a detailed business plan, proof of initial capital (minimum 100,000 USD), director and shareholder identification and background checks, compliance officer CV, office lease or purchase agreement, and banking relationship letters. All documents must be certified and typically require professional translation if not in English.
Vanuatu offers favorable tax treatment with no corporate income tax, capital gains tax, or withholding tax on dividends, which can provide significant savings compared to EU or US-regulated jurisdictions.
