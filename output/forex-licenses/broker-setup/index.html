Technology

MT4, MT5, cTrader: Choosing Your Platform

MetaTrader 4 (MT4) remains the most widely used forex trading platform globally, with the largest community of Expert Advisors (EAs), plugins, and third-party tools. It is ideal for pure forex brokers targeting retail traders familiar with the MT4 ecosystem.

MetaTrader 5 (MT5) is the successor platform supporting multi-asset trading including equities, futures, and crypto CFDs alongside forex. MT5 offers better backtesting, faster execution, and more timeframes. Most new brokers in 2025 choose MT5 for its expanded capabilities.

cTrader is a premium alternative popular with ECN brokers and institutional clients, offering depth-of-market (DOM) display, Level II pricing, and a modern interface. More expensive than MT4/MT5 white labels but preferred by sophisticated traders.

Banking & PSP

Forex Broker Banking: Challenges & Solutions

Banking is consistently the hardest part of launching a forex brokerage. Most traditional banks in the EU, UK, and US refuse forex broker accounts due to compliance risk and regulatory exposure. Offshore brokers must rely on Electronic Money Institutions (EMIs) and offshore banking centers.

Commonly used EMIs for forex brokers include Credorax, iSignthis, PayRetailers, and various Eastern European e-money providers. Offshore banking options include banks in Saint Kitts, Belize, Georgia, and Latvia. Having a legitimate regulatory license — Seychelles FSA or above — significantly improves account approval rates.

Banking tip: Never rely on a single banking relationship. Multi-bank setups (one EMI + one offshore bank at minimum) protect against account freezes and ensure uninterrupted client payment flows.

The complete licensing process typically takes 6 to 12 months from initial application to final approval by FINMA. The timeline depends on the completeness of your submission, the complexity of your business model, and FINMA's current workload. Most delays occur during the documentation review phase when regulators request additional information about your risk management frameworks.
You must secure at least one correspondent banking relationship with a tier-1 bank and establish a segregated client escrow account before submitting your FINMA application. Most forex brokers work with banks like UBS, Credit Suisse's successor entities, or regional Swiss banks that accept forex broker clients. Your bank must confirm they will handle client fund transfers and provide documentation of their AML procedures.

The limited activities license is most cost-effective for starting brokers at CHF 100,000-300,000 in capital, though it restricts you from certain activities like proprietary trading or advisory services. Your choice depends on your planned service offerings and growth timeline.
FINMA requires detailed business plans, organizational charts, risk management policies, AML/KYC procedures, IT security audits, financial projections, shareholder information, and proof of banking relationships. You must also submit CVs and background checks for all board members and senior management, plus documentation showing your compliance officer has relevant experience. All documents must be in German, French, or Italian, or accompanied by certified translations.
Swiss forex broker licenses must be renewed annually through FINMA with updated financial statements, risk assessments, and compliance reports costing approximately CHF 10,000-25,000 per year. You must demonstrate ongoing compliance with capital requirements, which increase proportionally with your client fund volumes. Renewal is typically straightforward if you've maintained clean regulatory standing and updated your compliance frameworks.
The primary risks include FINMA enforcement actions for inadequate AML controls, capital ratio breaches, or client fund mishandling, which can result in fines up to CHF 5 million or license revocation. Leverage rules are also strictly enforced, with FINMA limiting retail client leverage to 30:1 for major pairs and lower for exotic pairs. You should budget for ongoing compliance monitoring and legal counsel, as regulatory expectations have tightened significantly in 2024-2026 following increased scrutiny of the retail forex sector.
