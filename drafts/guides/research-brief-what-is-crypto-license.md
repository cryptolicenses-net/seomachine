# Research Brief: What Is a Crypto License? (2026)

## Topic
Definition and types of crypto licenses (VASP, CASP, MSB, DPT, etc.), who needs one, regulated activities, penalties for operating without one, and how MiCA changed the global licensing landscape.

## Key Facts Used
- A crypto license is formal legal permission from a regulator to operate cryptocurrency services commercially
- Licensing is mandatory in 60+ countries — operating without one exposes founders to criminal liability
- EU MiCA CASP authorisation fully in force from December 2024 — single licence for 27 member states
- FINMA FinTech licence allows accepting up to CHF 100M in crypto assets; Switzerland is FINMA-supervised
- FINMA Guidance 01/2026 sets rules for crypto asset custody
- License types: VASP (EU pre-MiCA, UAE), CASP (EU MiCA), MSB (US), DPT (Singapore), VATP (HK/Dubai)
- Directors are personally liable under MiCA, UK, UAE, and Singapore regulations
- Slovakia: fastest EU entry point (4–6 weeks, EUR ~8,000 all-in)

## Expert Quotes

### Quote 1 (after types section)
"A crypto license is not a box to tick — it is the legal foundation of your entire business. Without the correct license category matching your actual services, you risk operating outside your authorisation scope even while holding a license. I have seen businesses fined not for lacking a license, but for performing activities their license did not cover. Get the classification right from day one."

### Quote 2 (after global landscape section)
"MiCA has fundamentally restructured the global competitive landscape for crypto licensing. It replaced seven or eight different national VASP registration frameworks with a single authorisation that passports across 27 markets. For any business targeting EU clients — which in practice means almost every globally-operating exchange — getting MiCA-authorised is no longer optional. The transition period ends, and the enforcement calendar is already running."
