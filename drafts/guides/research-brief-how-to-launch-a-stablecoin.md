## Verified facts (every figure traces to a source)

### Two MiCA stablecoin categories
- **MiCA = Regulation (EU) 2023/1114.** Two relevant titles:
  - **Title III — Asset-Referenced Tokens (ART)** (Articles 16–47).
  - **Title IV — E-Money Tokens (EMT)** (Articles 48–58).
  - Both titles applied from **30 June 2024**. (Source: EUR-Lex Reg. 2023/1114; ESMA/EBA.)

### EMT (e-money token)
- Pegged to **a single official currency** (e.g. EUR-pegged or USD-pegged stablecoin).
- Must be issued **only by an authorised credit institution (bank) or electronic money institution (EMI)** under existing EU law, plus a MiCA white paper notification. (Source: EBA "Asset-referenced and e-money tokens (MiCA)"; multiple secondary.)
- **Redeemable at par value at any time** by holders (Article 49). (Source: MiCA Art. 49.)
- 1:1 reserve / treated as electronic money.

### ART (asset-referenced token)
- References **a basket / other value** — multiple currencies, commodities (e.g. gold), other crypto-assets, or a combination.
- Requires a **fresh ART authorisation from a national competent authority** under MiCA Title III (not just an EMI licence).
- **Own funds (Article 35):** the highest of:
  - (i) **EUR 350,000**;
  - (ii) **2% of the average amount of the reserve of assets**;
  - (iii) **a quarter (25%) of fixed overheads of the preceding year**.
  - Average reserve = end-of-each-calendar-day amount over preceding six months. (Source: MiCA Art. 35; mica.wtf; A&O Shearman.)

### EMT capital
- Issued via EMI/credit institution — EMI own-funds floor under EMD2 is **EUR 350,000** initial capital. (Source: secondary; EMD/EMI rules. EMT prudential additions for significant EMTs apply on top.)

### Reserve / redemption
- Reserve fully covers liabilities to holders; ART reserve liquidity RTS: at least **20% of reserve** sellable within one day, **30% within five days** (significant: 40% / 60%). (Source: EBA RTS on highly liquid instruments.)
- EMT redemption at par at any time (Art. 49).

### Significant token thresholds (EBA supervision)
- Classified **significant** if meets at least three criteria, including:
  - more than **10 million holders**;
  - issued value / market cap / reserve over **EUR 5 billion**;
  - more than **2.5 million daily transactions** and **EUR 500 million daily volume**.
- Significant ART → **EBA direct supervision**; significant EMT → EBA + national authority joint supervision.
- Significant tokens: higher own funds up to **3% of average reserves**, ≥60% reserve at multiple credit institutions, enhanced liquidity, recovery/redemption plans. (Source: EBA "supervisory role under MiCA"; secondary.)

### Transitional / timeline
- ART & EMT obligations from **30 June 2024**.
- EU-wide MiCA transitional period for CASPs ends **1 July 2026**. (Source: secondary, sumsub/eco.)
