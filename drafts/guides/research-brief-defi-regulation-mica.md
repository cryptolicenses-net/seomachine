## Core thesis (no fabrication)
There is **no blanket DeFi exemption** under MiCA. Whether DeFi is regulated depends on decentralisation *in fact*, assessed case by case. Most "DeFi" has identifiable controllers, admin keys, or front-ends and may be in scope.

## Verified facts → sources

| Claim | Source |
|---|---|
| MiCA Recital 22: "Where crypto-asset services are provided in a fully decentralised manner without any intermediary, they should not fall within the scope of this Regulation." | EUR-Lex Reg. (EU) 2023/1114, Recital 22 (quoted via BCAS analysis) |
| Exemption is in a **recital**, not the binding operative articles; "fully decentralised" is not defined in the enacting terms | BCAS; Aurum law analysis |
| Two cumulative conditions: (1) fully decentralised AND (2) no intermediary; if any part is intermediated, MiCA applies | ESMA/EBA analysis; bitcoin.com news |
| Test in practice: who holds admin keys/upgrade rights, who can pause/reroute, who sets parameters, whether front-end is a practical gateway, whether a service-provider/client relationship exists | BCAS; ESMA consultation |
| Article 142 MiCAR mandate: Commission report by **30 December 2024** on DeFi developments + need/feasibility of regulating DeFi, after consulting EBA + ESMA | ESMA Art.142 page; MiCAR Art.142 |
| Joint EBA-ESMA Article 142 report (ESMA75-453128700-1391) published **mid-January 2025** | ESMA / EBA press release |
| DeFi TVL = **~4% of all crypto-asset market value** globally; EU adoption above global average but below US/South Korea | EBA-ESMA Joint Report |
| Report does not recommend regulating DeFi; defers to Commission to assess need/feasibility | EBA-ESMA press release |
| FATF "owner/operator control" test: creators/owners/operators who maintain control or sufficient influence over a DeFi arrangement may be VASPs even if it "seems decentralized" | FATF 2021 Updated VA/VASP Guidance |
| FATF factors: sufficient influence over assets/protocol, ability to set/change parameters, ongoing business relationship, profiting from the service | FATF 2021 Guidance; Merkle/Sumsub summaries |
| FATF: underlying software/dApp itself is not a VASP; standards apply to persons, not technology | FATF 2021 Guidance |
