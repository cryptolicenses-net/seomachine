## Verified facts (every figure traced to source)

### Business model (Step 1)
- A-book / STP / agency / matched-principal: broker routes client orders to liquidity providers (LPs), earns spread/commission, no principal market risk.
- B-book / market maker / dealing on own account: broker is the counterparty to the client, takes principal risk, higher capital. Source: FCA matched-principal vs dealing-as-principal classification; FinanceMagnates/Leverate explainers.

### CySEC (Cyprus) — current capital under IFR/IFD (in force 26 June 2021)
Authoritative current figures (Regulation (EU) 2019/2033 IFR + Directive (EU) 2019/2034 IFD):
- **€75,000** — reception/transmission of orders, execution, advice, portfolio management WITHOUT holding client funds (advisory/Class 3-type).
- **€150,000** — same services PLUS authorised to hold client money/instruments (STP broker that holds client funds).
- **€750,000** — dealing on own account / underwriting / operating an MTF or OTF (market maker / B-book).
Source: CySEC "Practical Guide for the implementation of IFR/IFD" (cysec.gov.cy GetFile guid 20d9a1f9...); SALVUS Funds; Citius Trust.
NOTE: The widely quoted **€125,000 (STP) / €730,000 (market maker)** figures are from the PRE-2021 MiFID/CRD prudential regime. They are still cited in many secondary sources but the IFR/IFD figures (€150k / €750k) are the legally current ones. I present current figures as primary and note the legacy figures.
- CySEC = MiFID II investment firm (CIF); license passportable across EEA. Timeline ~9–14 months for a full CIF.
- DORA applies to all CIFs since Jan 2025 (ICT risk framework, incident reporting per CySEC Circular C700).

### FCA (UK)
- Matched-principal (STP/A-book) firm: base own-funds requirement aligns with €125,000 class under UK IFPR (post-Brexit retained MiFID classes).
- Dealing on own account (market maker/B-book): £750,000 class.
- Regime: Investment Firms Prudential Regime (IFPR). Source: FCA IFPR; arincen/leverate explainers. Figures presented as approximate/class-based.

### ASIC (Australia) — AFSL retail OTC derivative issuer
- Net Tangible Assets (NTA): the greater of **AUD 1,000,000** or 10% of average revenue.
- 50% of NTA in cash/cash equivalents, remainder in liquid assets.
- 12-month cash-flow projections; notify ASIC within 3 business days if NTA falls below 110% of required.
- If broker acts as principal to client (even if STP-hedged) it is a "retail OTC derivative issuer" and must meet full AUD 1m NTA.
Source: ASIC RG 166; financemagnates; afslhouse; cnmlegal.

### Mauritius FSC — offshore option (Investment Dealer)
- Investment Dealer (Broker): minimum stated capital **MUR 700,000 (~USD 15,500)** — acts as agent.
- Investment Dealer (Full Service Dealer, excluding underwriting): **MUR 1,000,000 (~USD 22,000)** — principal.
- Full Service incl. underwriting: MUR 10,000,000 (~USD 222,000).
- Capital must be fully paid and unimpaired, maintained at all times (license condition).
- Processing fees MUR 5,000–100,000; annual fees MUR 60,000–290,000.
Source: FSC Mauritius Securities Act / Investment Dealer rules; privacy-solutions; allfx-consult.

### Cross-checks
- Offshore (SVG historically registration-only, FSA SVG; Comoros/Anjouan, Seychelles FSA, St Vincent) = lower/no minimum capital but weaker reputation, harder banking. Onshore (CySEC/FCA/ASIC) = higher capital + credibility + bank/PSP access + passporting.
