## Angle
There is no single "best" jurisdiction. Best = best FIT against seven weighted criteria:
1. Target market / passporting
2. Capital requirements
3. Timeline
4. Tax
5. Banking access
6. Reputation / FATF status
7. Substance / physical presence

## Grounded facts (with sources)

### MiCA / EU (esma.europa.eu)
- CASP capital tiers by activity: EUR 50,000 (advice / order reception-transmission), EUR 125,000 (exchange, custody, execution, placing), EUR 150,000 (operating a trading platform / dealing on own account). Sources vary on "Class I/II/III" labels, so present by ACTIVITY not by class number.
- Transitional period: VASPs authorised under national law before 30 Dec 2024 may continue until **1 July 2026** or until MiCA authorisation is granted/refused.
- Single MiCA authorisation passports across all 27 EU + EEA states. Grandfathered VASPs CANNOT passport during transition — only fully authorised CASPs.
- Well-prepared MiCA application in efficient EU jurisdiction: ~6–8 months.
- Source: ESMA MiCA page; supporting industry confirmation of EUR 50k/125k/150k tiers.

### UAE / VARA (vara.ae)
- VARA (Dubai) processing fast (industry: 4–8 weeks initial, but full to operate longer). Setup ~$50k–$100k. Capital for exchange/custody can reach AED 1,500,000 (~$390,000).
- 0% personal capital gains; 9% corporate (free-zone exemptions for qualifying activity).

### Singapore / MAS (mas.gov.sg)
- MAS DPT / Major Payment Institution; MPI capital SGD 250,000. Process thorough/slow: 6–12 months. Strongest institutional credibility in Asia.

### El Salvador (covered on-site /crypto-licenses/latam/el-salvador/)
- DASP / BSP regime. Affordable entry, FATF-aligned. Timeline ~2–5 months. Zero-tax on qualifying digital-asset activities (corporate income tax + VAT exemption for licensed DASP/BSP).

### Cayman Islands (covered on-site /crypto-licenses/americas/cayman-islands/)
- VASP regime (CIMA). No statutory minimum capital. 0% corporate income tax. Institutional / fund credibility.

### Lithuania (covered /crypto-licenses/europe/lithuania/) & Estonia (/europe/estonia/)
- Both EU — fall under MiCA now. Historically fast/cheap VASP regimes; now must transition to MiCA CASP by July 2026. Lithuania = popular EU entry point.

### Switzerland (covered /crypto-licenses/europe/switzerland/)
- FINMA; DLT Act; FinTech licence. High reputation, Crypto Valley Zug. Not EU (no MiCA passport) but strong banking + credibility.

### FATF status (fatf-gafi.org)
- As of June 2026: 22 jurisdictions under increased monitoring (grey list). Blacklist = Iran, North Korea, Myanmar (3, unchanged since Oct 2022).
- June 2026 plenary: added Bosnia & Herzegovina and Iraq; removed Algeria and Namibia.
- Grey-list exposure not inherently illicit but triggers enhanced due diligence; affects banking access and counterparty trust.
