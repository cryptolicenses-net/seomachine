## Key claims → sources (every figure traced)

### What makes a bank "crypto-friendly"
- A crypto-friendly bank/EMI is one that will onboard and bank a crypto business (exchange, VASP, Web3 startup), provides fiat IBAN rails, supports on/off-ramp flows to exchanges/stablecoins, and does not de-risk crypto activity by policy. Combines fiat + digital-asset infra under one roof. Source: Binderr; BUH Consulting; Verifo.

### Switzerland / Liechtenstein crypto banks
- FINMA granted banking licences to Sygnum and SEBA (now AMINA) in August 2019 — world's first two crypto banks. Source: swissinfo.ch; finews.com.
- Sygnum = "world's first regulated digital asset bank" (custody, trading, staking, tokenization). Source: sygnum.com.
- AMINA Bank AG (formerly SEBA), HQ Zug, first FINMA-regulated institution to offer crypto custodial services (2019); entered profitability Q4 2024. Source: finews; Medium/Liu.
- Swiss cluster also includes Bitcoin Suisse, Hypothekarbank Lenzburg, InCore. Source: disruptionbanking.com; blockzeit.com.
- FINMA pragmatic frameworks (ICO/token classification/AML) underpin Crypto Valley Zug. Source: alpenpartners.com.

### EU EMIs (fiat onramp banking)
- EMIs authorised under PSD2 / e-money rules provide dedicated IBANs + SEPA and explicitly onboard crypto businesses. Lithuania is the EMI hub. Source: thebanks.eu; innreg.com.
- Named (source-confirmed): Bankera (Lithuania EMI, crypto-friendly business accounts), Monerium (EU-authorised EMI, MiCA-compliant, fiat on/off-ramp), Fiat Republic (DNB-authorised EMI, virtual IBANs for crypto businesses), 3S Money (EMI UK/Luxembourg). Source: thebanks.eu; monerium.com; binderr.com.

### US state-chartered trusts + national trust banks
- Wyoming SPDI (Special Purpose Depository Institution) — fully-reserved banks under SPDI Act (HB74, 2019); custody + fiduciary. Custodia (Avanti) approved Oct 2020. Source: Wyoming Banking Division; Hunton.
- NYDFS limited purpose trust charter — Coinbase, Fidelity, Gemini, Bakkt, Paxos; MoonPay Trust Co LLC authorised Nov 2025. Source: ABA Business Law Today; cryptobriefing.
- OCC national trust bank charter — Anchorage (2021); Dec 12, 2025 OCC conditionally approved five crypto firms: Ripple, BitGo, Paxos, Circle, Fidelity Digital Assets. OCC final rule live Apr 1, 2026 expanding national trust banks to digital-asset custody. Source: Cadwalader; Banking Dive; OCC.

### Singapore
- MAS-licensed DPT (Digital Payment Token) service providers under Payment Services Act 2019 (Major Payment Institution licence) — trading, custody, exchange. Source: capitalmarkets.sg.
- Five MAS digital banks (2026): Trust Bank, GXS, MariBank, ANEXT, Green Link; no new licences issued. Source: Aspire; Statrys; Airwallex.
- April 2026: MAS consultation proposing flexible prudential / capital treatment for cryptoassets on public blockchains. Source: Bird & Bird; Blockhead.

### 2023 de-banking wave → 2025/2026 US reversal
- Jan 2023 joint Fed/FDIC/OCC statement: issuing/holding crypto on public decentralized networks "highly likely inconsistent with safe and sound banking practices." Source: CoinDesk.
- 2023 collapses of Silvergate, Signature, Silicon Valley Bank cut key crypto banking rails ("Operation Chokepoint 2.0" narrative). Source: Blockspace; CoinDesk.
- Apr 24, 2025: Federal Reserve withdrew crypto-asset guidance for banks. Source: federalreserve.gov press release bcreg20250424a.
- Apr 10, 2026: OCC + FDIC final rule codifying removal of reputation risk from supervision. June 2, 2026: Fed/FDIC/OCC reissued 15 interagency documents stripping reputation-risk references. Source: OCC bulletin 2026-23; BPI; Paul Hastings.
- Nov/Dec 2025: OCC letters — national banks may hold digital assets as principal for network ops/testing + riskless-principal crypto transactions. Source: Elliptic; Davis Polk.

### How a licence unlocks banking
- Banks/EMIs onboard crypto businesses far more readily when the business holds a VASP/CASP/DPT licence — the licence is the AML/KYC + Travel Rule evidence the bank needs to satisfy its own de-risking checks. Categorical / practice-based claim consistent with sourced de-risking + EMI onboarding material.
