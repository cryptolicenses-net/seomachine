## Core thesis

A crypto MLRO (Money Laundering Reporting Officer) is the named, accountable individual responsible for a crypto firm's AML/CFT programme: receiving internal suspicion reports, deciding on and filing Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs/STRs) to the national FIU, owning the AML policy framework, and reporting to the board. The role is a hard licensing precondition under MiCA (CASP authorisation), national VASP regimes, and the FCA cryptoasset registration. The post-holder must pass a "fit and proper" / approved-person assessment and, increasingly, demonstrate local substance.

## Verified facts → sources

1. **MLRO has two core duties** — (a) the legal obligation to report suspicion of money laundering to the national FIU (e.g. UK National Crime Agency), and (b) ensuring the firm's compliance with money laundering regulations. *Source: ICAEW / SRA / Sanction Scanner / Skillcast.*

2. **SAR workflow** — the MLRO receives internal SARs from staff, investigates, and decides whether to file a formal SAR with the authorities (NCA in UK / national FIU). *Source: Signzy, Fraxtional, SRA.*

3. **Appointment is mandatory in regulated crypto** — most countries legally require crypto firms ("reporting entities" / "obliged entities") to appoint an MLRO and maintain internal controls. *Source: AMLBot UK crypto guide, Sanction Scanner.*

4. **FATF Recommendation 18** — AML/CFT programmes must include "the appointment of a compliance officer at the management level," with internal policies, procedures, controls and screening. *Source: FATF R.18 (cfatf-gafic.org, fatf-gafi.org explanatory materials R18/R23).*

5. **FCA SMF17** — In the UK, the Money Laundering Reporting Officer is Senior Management Function 17 (SMF17) under the Senior Managers & Certification Regime. The FCA pays particular attention to competence/capability of SMF17 candidates; skills must match firm size and risk of harm. *Source: fca.org.uk "Heads of compliance and MLROs", FCA Handbook SUP 10C.6, glossary G729 "money laundering reporting function".*

6. **MiCA CASPs = obliged entities** — CASPs authorised under MiCA are obliged entities under the EU AML framework; full AML/CFT applies (CDD aligned with AMLD, EDD for higher risk, Travel Rule EUR 1,000, transaction monitoring, 5-year record retention, appoint a qualified MLRO). *Source: MiCA / EU AML guides (maxcorp, itispay, finconduit, Bitcoin.com MiCA decoded).*

7. **Compliance function cannot be outsourced to a revenue role or shared across entities** — NCAs reject applications where compliance is combined with a revenue role or split. MLRO typically must be on payroll; fit-and-proper assessment applies to key function holders (CEO, CFO, CRO, MLRO, Head of Custody, Head of IT); deputies expected. *Source: Bitcoin.com "MiCA Decoded", itispay.*

8. **VASP vs CASP transition** — VASP = legacy national registration regimes pre-MiCA; CASP = MiCA authorisation. Transitional grandfathering for legacy VASPs expires for most member states between 1 July 2026 and 30 December 2026. *Source: finconduit, adamsmith.lt MiCA guide.*

9. **Crypto-specific MLRO competence** — a crypto MLRO must understand FATF crypto guidance, wallet tracing, and the Travel Rule. *Source: Fraxtional / Signzy.*

10. **2025 SAR guidance** — UKFIU issued updated SAR reporting guidance in 2025; firms reminded to keep MLRO contact details current in the SARs system. *Source: AMLBot UK 2025 guide.*
