## Every figure → source (verified June 2026 via web research)

### Zero / low-tax (personal)

| Jurisdiction | Personal CGT on crypto | Notes | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **UAE** | 0% | No personal income tax, no CGT for individuals; crypto VAT-exempt since Nov 2024. Corporate Tax 9% on business profits above AED 375,000 may apply to crypto businesses. | Multiple 2026 guides citing UAE Federal Tax Authority; CCN, Koinly |
| **Cayman Islands** | 0% | No income tax, no CGT, no corporate tax for residents/businesses. Crypto = personal investment, exempt. | CIMA; CCN "0% crypto tax 2026" |
| **El Salvador** | 0% | Foreign investors exempt from CGT on Bitcoin profits; 2023 "technological innovation" law removed income, capital gains, property tax on crypto for individuals and certain businesses. | imidaily, tokentax 2026 |
| **Switzerland** | 0% (private investor) | Capital gains on movable private wealth tax-free for private investors. BUT annual **wealth tax** (cantonal, ~0.3%–1%) on holdings. Staking/mining/lending = taxable income. **Professional traders** taxed as income (income tax). Circular 36 / 5 safe-harbour criteria define private vs professional. | estv.admin.ch; FTA Circular No. 36; Koinly/Blockpit 2026 |
| **Germany** | 0% after 1-year holding | Crypto = "other assets" §23 EStG (private sale). Held >12 months → tax-free. Held <12 months → taxed at personal income rate (0–45%) on gains above €1,000 Freigrenze (per year, from 2024). | §23 EStG (gesetze-im-internet.de); BMF guidance; Blockpit/Koinly 2026 |
| **Portugal** | 0% if held ≥365 days | Held <365 days → 28% flat. ≥365 days → exempt (Category G). Crypto-to-crypto does not reset (cumulative holding). Staking/passive yield 28%. Business-like activity → progressive 14.5%–53%. Black-list counterparties excluded from exemption. | Portuguese IRS Code (CIRS); tokentax/koinly 2026 |
| **Singapore** | 0% (no CGT) | No CGT for individuals on crypto held as investment (IRAS, since mid-2010s). Income tax (up to 24% resident) applies if trading "in the nature of trade", mining, staking, or paid in crypto. | iras.gov.sg; tokentax/koinly 2026 |

### High-tax (personal)

| Jurisdiction | Personal CGT on crypto | Notes | Source |

Form 1099-DA broker reporting began for 2025 transactions (filed 2026); cost-basis reporting from 2026 tx. Form 8949 + Schedule D + 1040. | irs.gov; metamask/turbotax/H&R Block 2026 |

### Corporate note
- Switzerland: corporate income tax ~12%–21% effective (cantonal) on crypto company profits.
- UAE: 9% corporate tax above AED 375,000 may catch crypto businesses despite 0% personal.
- Germany/Portugal/Singapore: business-classified crypto activity taxed under corporate/business income rules, not the personal exemption.

### OECD CARF (Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework)
- 2023-11-10 joint statement: 54 jurisdictions committed to implement CARF.
- OECD 2025 Monitoring Update: **52 jurisdictions** undertaking first exchanges **by 2027**; a further **15** by 2028 (incl. UAE, US, Singapore, Hong Kong).
- Industry "48 countries activated" headlines refer to a narrower activation cohort; I use OECD's own 52-by-2027 figure to be precise, and note "48+".
- Data collection by reporting crypto providers begins **2026**; first automatic exchanges **2027**.
- CARF reports holdings/transactions to tax authorities; **0% local tax does NOT remove CARF reporting**.
- Source: oecd.org CARF monitoring/implementation update 2025; commitments-carf.pdf

### CARF rollout timeline (for ig-timeline)
1. 2022 Oct — OECD publishes CARF
2. 2023 Nov — 54 jurisdictions commit (joint statement)
3. 2026 — Reporting crypto-asset providers begin data collection
4. 2027 — First automatic exchanges of information (52 jurisdictions)
5. 2028 — Second cohort joins (UAE, US, Singapore, Hong Kong + 11 more)
