# Research Brief — Offshore vs Onshore Forex Broker License (2026)

## Thesis
Onshore (FCA, CySEC/MiFID, ASIC) = high capital, leverage caps, client-money segregation, strong credibility and bank/PSP access, long timelines, high cost. Offshore (Mauritius, Seychelles, Vanuatu, BVI) = low capital, leverage flexibility, fast and cheap, but weaker banking/PSP access and reputational risk. SVG no longer a "no-questions" forex haven since Jan 2023. Balanced verdict: hybrid model (onshore entity for credibility + offshore entity for high-leverage clients) is common in practice.

## Verified facts → sources

### Onshore
- **CySEC CIF** (Cyprus, MiFID II): STP/dealing-on-own-account-without-market-making minimum capital **EUR 125,000**; full market-maker (dealing on own account) **EUR 730,000**. Law 144(I)/2017; MiFID II/MiFIR. EU/EEA passporting. Source: CySEC; atomiqconsulting/tetraconsultants summarise; primary: cysec.gov.cy.
- **FCA (UK):** MiFID-aligned conduct; retail leverage cap **1:30 majors, 1:20 minors/exotics** (FCA adopted ESMA permanent rules, effective 1 Aug 2019). Negative balance protection, client-money segregation (CASS), FSCS cover. Capital for matched-principal/dealing varies; tier-1 all-in typically high. Source: fca.org.uk.
- **ASIC (Australia):** AFSL; retail leverage cap **30:1 majors, 20:1 minors** (Product Intervention Order, in force from 29 March 2021). NTA requirement (commonly AUD 1,000,000 or 10% of avg revenue for market-maker MDP). Source: asic.gov.au.

### Offshore
- **Mauritius FSC Investment Dealer (Full Service Dealer):** Securities Act 2005; min stated capital MUR 1,000,000 (~USD 22k) for full service excluding underwriting; full-service practical capital cited ~USD 180k by advisers — use the FSC statutory MUR 1m figure as the anchored number. Timeline ~4-6 months. Mid-tier reputation, treaty network, low tax (~3% effective via partial exemption). Source: fscmauritius.org.
- **Seychelles FSA Securities Dealer License (SDL):** Securities Act 2007; paid-up capital **increased from USD 50,000 to USD 100,000** (FSA may require USD 250,000+ for higher-risk). Timeline 3-6 months. Source: fsaseychelles.sc.
- **Vanuatu VFSC Financial Dealers License (FDL):** one-time bond/security deposit ~**VT 5,000,000 (~USD 44,000-50,000)**; no leverage caps; 4 classes; local director/MLRO presence; 5 yrs experience; 3-6 months. Source: vfsc.vu.
- **SVG (St Vincent):** FSA does NOT license/regulate forex; since **6 Jan 2023** memorandum, BCs engaged in forex must show a foreign license/approval. Existing cos had until 10 Mar 2023. Effectively ended "no-questions" registration. Source: svgfsa.com.
- **BVI:** SIBA (Securities and Investment Business Act 2010) Category 2/dealing license required for genuine forex dealing — mid-cost offshore, more substance than SVG. (General knowledge; cross-reference only, not a hard figure.)

### Leverage
- ESMA/FCA/ASIC: 30:1 majors, 20:1 minors. Offshore (Vanuatu, Mauritius, Seychelles): commonly 100:1-500:1+, no statutory retail cap (Vanuatu explicitly no leverage caps).
