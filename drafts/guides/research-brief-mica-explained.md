# Research Brief: MiCA Regulation Explained

## Topic
EU Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (EU Regulation 2023/1114) — comprehensive guide covering asset categories, CASP authorization, passporting, capital requirements, and compliance steps.

## Key Facts
- MiCA in force: CASP provisions from December 30, 2024
- 18-month transitional period ended June 30, 2026 — all CASPs must hold MiCA authorization
- Three asset categories: ART (Asset-Referenced Tokens), EMT (E-Money Tokens), other crypto-assets
- CASP capital requirements: Class 1 €50k, Class 2 €125k, Class 3 €150k
- EU passporting: single CASP authorization covers all 27 EU member states via notification
- Travel Rule (TFR): applies to all crypto transfers, no minimum threshold for CASPs
- Lithuania, Poland fastest NCA processing: 3–4 months average from complete application
- Switzerland (FINMA) has parallel crypto framework — FinTech licence, DLT trading facility (first approved 2025)
- FINMA Guidance 01/2026 issued January 12, 2026 on custody of crypto-based assets
