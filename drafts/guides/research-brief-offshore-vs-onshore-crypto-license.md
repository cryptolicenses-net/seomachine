## Core thesis (balanced, honest)
Offshore licenses (Cayman VASP Act, BVI VASP Act 2022, Seychelles, SVG) = lower cost, faster, 0% tax, but weaker banking access, reputational/FATF scrutiny, and economic substance obligations. Onshore (EU MiCA, Switzerland) = higher cost/capital, but EU/EEA passporting, banking access, and credibility. The banking-access tradeoff is the decisive variable.

## Verified facts with sources

### Offshore
- **Cayman VASP regime (CIMA):** two-tier — VASP registration (lower-risk) vs full VASP licence (higher-risk). Registration 2–4 months; full licence 6–10 months. Government fees KYD 1,000 to KYD 100,000+. Minimum paid-up share capital US$100,000. Local AML officer, CIMA-approved director, registered office, business plan. 0% corporate/capital gains/withholding tax. CIMA scrutiny on custody architecture, source of funds, Travel Rule. Source: gofaizen-sherle.com/crypto-license/cayman-islands; cima.ky/vasp-faq; globallegalinsights.com (Cayman 2026).
- **BVI VASP Act 2022:** effective 1 Feb 2023. Three categories — Custody Provider, Exchange, General VASP. Timeline 3–6 months. Application fee $5,000 (general) / $10,000 (custody/exchange). Total cost ~USD 15,000–40,000+. NO fixed statutory minimum capital; FSC assesses adequacy case-by-case, expects 6–12 months operating reserves. No corporate/capital gains/withholding tax. Source: cryptoverselawyers.io/bvi-vasp-license-cost-approval-timeline; zitadelleag.com BVI VASP.
- **Seychelles:** FSA VASP Act regime. Was previously FATF grey-listed; FATF Feb 2026 plenary confirmed Seychelles remains OFF the grey list, easing correspondent banking friction for substance-backed VASPs. Source: easyglobalbanking.com 2026 offshore reset; zitadelleag.com Seychelles; sanctionslawyers.net FATF grey list 2026.
- **SVG (St Vincent & the Grenadines):** historically light-touch, low cost; banking access weakest.

### FATF offshore VASP risk
- FATF report "Understanding and Mitigating the Risks of Off-shore VASPs" published **11 March 2026**. Flags regulatory arbitrage, weak supervision, nested relationships (unlicensed oVASPs accessing licensed VASPs by posing as private customers), use in scam-compound laundering and terrorist financing. Finds only **46% of jurisdictions** apply activity-based supervision. Recommends FIs/VASPs refrain from relationships with unlicensed/unregistered oVASPs. Source: fatf-gafi.org/en/publications/Virtualassets/Understanding-Mitigating-Risks-Offshore-VASPs.html; regulationtomorrow.com 2026/03.
- FATF grey list ("jurisdictions under increased monitoring") = 23 countries as of Feb 2026 plenary. Grey-listing triggers enhanced bank due diligence, higher fees, de-risking. Source: sanctionslawyers.net FATF grey list 2026.

### Onshore
- **EU MiCA (Regulation (EU) 2023/1114):** CASP capital tiers — EUR 50,000 (Class 1: advice/order transmission/placing), EUR 125,000 (Class 2: exchange/trading platform), EUR 150,000 (Class 3: custody). Own funds also ≥ one quarter of prior year's fixed overheads. Identical across all 30 EEA jurisdictions. One CASP licence passports to all 27 EU states + Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway (30 EEA). MiCA full-application transition deadline 1 July 2026. As of 18 June 2026, 204 CASPs hold full authorisation per ESMA interim register. Licensing fees one-time EUR 4,500–100,000 depending on jurisdiction. Source: esma.europa.eu MiCA; gofaizen-sherle.com/casp-license; paybis.com MiCA list.
- **Switzerland:** FINMA, FinTech licence, DLT Act, Crypto Valley Zug; high credibility, banking access via Swiss crypto banks (SEBA/AMINA, Sygnum). Higher cost/capital. (Project canon — master.md / regulation/switzerland.)
- **CARF / OECD:** Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework — automatic exchange of crypto account info, narrowing offshore tax-opacity advantage. OECD economic substance / BEPS underpins offshore substance rules. Source: oecd.org CARF.

### Banking reality
- Offshore VASPs face harder fiat banking: correspondent de-risking, grey-list-driven enhanced due diligence, FATF oVASP scrutiny. Onshore (MiCA/Swiss) firms get banking from EU/Swiss institutions and credibility with counterparties.
