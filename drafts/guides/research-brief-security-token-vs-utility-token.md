# Research Brief: Security Token vs Utility Token (2026)

---

## Core distinction (verified)

- **Security token** = a crypto-asset that represents an investment / financial instrument. It triggers full securities law (registration, disclosure, anti-fraud). In the US it is analysed under the **Howey test**; in the EU it falls under **MiFID II**, carved OUT of MiCA.
- **Utility token** = a crypto-asset that provides access to a product, service or network function (consumptive use), not an investment. In the US, if it fails Howey it is not a security (may be a commodity under CFTC). In the EU it is an "other crypto-asset" under MiCA.

---

## The Howey Test — four prongs (SEC v. W.J. Howey Co., 1946)

An arrangement is an "investment contract" (security) if there is:
1. **Investment of money** — usually satisfied for token sales; consideration can be fiat, another digital asset, or other value.
2. **In a common enterprise** — courts require horizontal commonality (pooling of investor assets / pro-rata profit) or vertical commonality.
3. **Reasonable expectation of profits.**
4. **Derived from the efforts of others** (entrepreneurial/managerial efforts of a third party / promoter).

If all four met -> security. Source: SEC "Framework for 'Investment Contract' Analysis of Digital Assets," published by FinHub **3 April 2019**.

**Factors weighing TOWARD security (efforts of others / expectation of profit):** active development team / "AP" (Active Participant) essential to network success; promoter markets price appreciation; token not yet usable; secondary trading enabled; supply controlled to support price.

**Factors weighing TOWARD utility (consumptive use):** network fully functional/decentralised; token used to access a good or service now; marketed for use not investment; price stable / tied to usage; holders are users not speculators.

**Key nuance:** the framework + Hinman (June 2018 speech) state a digital asset that was sold as part of a securities offering **may, over time, cease to be a security** if the network becomes sufficiently decentralised. Bitcoin and Ether were stated not to be securities. The token *itself* is distinct from the *investment contract* it may be sold under.

## SEC vs CFTC treatment (US) — MAJOR 2026 UPDATE

- **17 March 2026:** SEC and CFTC issued a **landmark joint interpretation** on the application of federal securities laws to crypto assets (SEC press release 2026-30). One week earlier (11 March 2026) the two agencies signed an MOU on overlapping jurisdiction.
- Introduces a **five-part token taxonomy**: digital commodities, digital collectibles, digital tools, payment stablecoins, and digital securities (tokenized securities). Only **digital securities** are fully within SEC jurisdiction.
- The interpretation **expressly named 18 crypto assets as digital commodities** (CFTC jurisdiction), incl. BTC, ETH, SOL, ADA, XRP, LTC, DOGE, AVAX, LINK, DOT, etc.
- **Digital commodities** -> CFTC, lighter requirements (reduced disclosure, simpler custody, commodity exchanges). **Digital securities** -> SEC, full securities regime.
- Related US legislation: **GENIUS Act** (stablecoins) enacted July 2025, full implementing regs expected Nov 2026; **CLARITY Act** passed House July 2025 (294-134), cleared Senate Agriculture Committee Jan 2026.

So: security token = digital security (SEC); a pure utility/commodity token tends to fall to CFTC oversight as a digital commodity.

## EU / MiCA token taxonomy (verified)

MiCA (Regulation (EU) 2023/1114) covers three crypto-asset categories:
1. **Asset-referenced tokens (ART)** — stablecoins referencing a basket / multiple assets or non-fiat values.
2. **E-money tokens (EMT)** — stablecoins pegged to a single fiat currency.
3. **Other crypto-assets** — anything not ART/EMT and NOT a MiFID II financial instrument (this is where utility tokens, governance/platform tokens sit).

**MiFID II carve-out (the security token route):** crypto-assets that qualify as **financial instruments** under MiFID II (e.g. transferable securities) are **excluded from MiCA** (MiCA Art. 2(4)(a)). They are regulated under MiFID II / Prospectus Regulation — i.e. the EU "security token" regime. For hybrid assets, the MiFID II qualification prevails over MiCA.

**ESMA Guidelines (published 19 March 2025, ESMA75453128700-1323)** set the conditions/criteria for when a crypto-asset qualifies as a financial instrument. For a **transferable security**, three cumulative criteria: (1) not a payment instrument; (2) belongs to a class of securities, interchangeable, same issuer; (3) negotiable on the capital market. Substance-over-form approach. NFTs generally excluded (non-fungible), case-by-case. Guidelines apply 60 days after publication.

## Legal consequences contrast

| | Security token | Utility token |
|--|--|--|
| US regulator | SEC (digital security) | CFTC (digital commodity) per 2026 interpretation, if not a security |
| US test | Howey (4 prongs) | Fails Howey |
| EU framework | MiFID II / Prospectus Reg (carved out of MiCA) | MiCA "other crypto-asset" (white paper, CASP authorisation) |
| Offering | Security Token Offering (STO) — registration / prospectus / exemption | Token sale under MiCA white paper |
| Examples | tokenized equity, tokenized bonds, tokenized funds | filecoin-style access tokens, governance tokens, platform-utility tokens |
