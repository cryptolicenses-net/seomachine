# Research Brief: No-KYC Exchanges — Legal Risks

## Topic
Legal status, regulatory crackdowns, and operator/user risks for no-KYC crypto exchanges. Covers enforcement cases 2023–2025 and compliant alternatives.

## Key Facts
- No-KYC exchanges illegal in: EU, USA, UK, UAE, Singapore
- FATF Travel Rule enforced in 60+ jurisdictions as of 2026
- MiCA (EU) from Dec 2024: all CASPs must implement full KYC, no threshold exemption
- TFR (Transfer of Funds Regulation): Travel Rule applies to all crypto transfers, no minimum
- Major enforcement cases:
  - Tornado Cash: OFAC designation; Roman Storm convicted Aug 2024
  - Binance: $4.3B DOJ/FinCEN/CFTC settlement Nov 2023; CEO sentenced to 4 months
  - KuCoin: DOJ indictment March 2024 for no-KYC practices
  - Samurai Wallet: DOJ prosecution April 2024
  - LocalBitcoins: voluntary shutdown Feb 2023
- Swiss SECO maintains sanctions list — relevant for AML screening obligations
- FINMA actively supervises Swiss institutions on AML/KYC for crypto: Guidance 01/2026 covers custody obligations

## Expert Quotes Used

**Quote 1 (after Legal Status section):**
"The question I am asked most often is: can I operate without KYC if I incorporate offshore? The answer, in 2026, is unambiguously no — not if you have any users in the EU, US, or UK. The DOJ, FinCEN, and ESMA do not limit their enforcement jurisdiction to companies incorporated within their borders. They follow the users, and the users are everywhere."

**Quote 2 (after User Risks section):**
"Tiered KYC is the legitimate answer to the conversion problem. A Tier 1 onboarding that requires only a phone number and selfie, with a €1,000 monthly limit, serves the user's need for low-friction access while fully satisfying FATF Recommendation 10 simplified due diligence thresholds. The technology exists, the compliance case is solid, and it eliminates the business argument for no-KYC entirely."
