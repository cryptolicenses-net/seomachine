## Verified facts → sources

### MiCA / EU (statutory clock, Article 63)
- NCA assesses **completeness within 25 working days** of receipt.
- NCA has **40 working days** from receipt of a complete application to grant or refuse, with a fully reasoned decision.
- Assessment may be **suspended up to 20 working days** while awaiting requested missing info (Art. 63(9)/(12)); the 40-day clock itself cannot be paused beyond that.
- 25 + 40 working days ≈ ~3 months of statutory review **once the file is complete** — NOT end-to-end.
- Realistic end-to-end incl. prep: **6–12 months**.
- Source: MiCA Art. 63; bitcoin.com decode; EY Greece; FMA Austria; mica.wtf Art. 63.

### Lithuania (fast)
- Bank of Lithuania CASP review one of the shorter EU timelines: **~3–6 months**; can be as short as 3–4 months total.
- Source: Bank of Lithuania authorisation page; LegalBison; Consulting24.

### Poland (fast in theory)
- Typical processing **4–8 months** (mid-sized exchange ~6 months if docs complete).
- Note: as of 2026 Poland's national act empowering KNF was still being finalised (regulatory holding pattern). Treat "fast tier in theory" carefully.
- Source: cryptotimes.io Poland guide; Consulting24 Poland.

### Czech Republic (fast)
- ~3–6 months typical CASP review window (EU NCA, MiCA statutory clock applies). Grouped with fast EU tier.

### Singapore MAS (slow)
- Well-prepared applicant: **~6–9 months** prep-to-grant.
- Processing stage: SPI **6–12 months**, MPI/major **9–18 months**.
- DTSP regime (FSMA Part 9) effective 30 June 2025 for overseas-serving entities; DPT under PSA for local.
- Source: Hacken Singapore playbook; MAS DTSP guidelines.

### Hong Kong SFC VATP (slow)
- SFC performance pledge: up to **15 weeks (~3.5 months)** processing, but real-world VATP licensing runs much longer (9–18 months+) given build-out + external assessment.
- Jan 2025: expedited single external assessment (replaced Phase 1 + Phase 2 reports) for applicants after 18 Dec 2024.
- Source: PwC HK VATP; Regulation Tomorrow (SFC expedites, Feb 2025); SFC.

### New York BitLicense (slowest)
- No official statutory timeframe.
- Simple models (exchange/wallet): **12–18 months** average.
- Complex/institutional (custody, stablecoin): **up to 24 months+**.
- Historical: Genesis Global Trading waited ~3 years (applied Aug 2015); some firms ~4 years (2015→2019).
- Source: InnReg BitLicense; NYDFS; Fortune (2018 bottleneck); Wikipedia BitLicense.

## Stage breakdown (end-to-end)
1. Preparation (2–6 months): structuring, capital, AML/KYC framework, key personnel, policies, documentation.
2. Submission: filing complete application + fees.
3. Review (statutory): completeness check + substantive assessment (MiCA: 25 + 40 wd).
4. Approval/conditions: grant, conditions, or refusal.
5. Post-license (weeks–months): banking, onboarding controls, ongoing supervision/reporting.
