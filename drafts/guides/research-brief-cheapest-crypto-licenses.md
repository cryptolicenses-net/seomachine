# Research Brief: Cheapest Crypto Licenses Guide

## Topic
Cheapest crypto licenses globally in 2026 — real government fees, legal costs, hidden fees, banking access scores, and total year-1 expenses for SVG, Georgia, Poland, Slovakia, Bulgaria, Estonia, Lithuania, and other budget jurisdictions.

## Key Facts Gathered

### Cost Rankings (Total Year-1, All-In)
- SVG (St. Vincent & the Grenadines): ~$5,000 total; banking score 1/5; near-zero institutional acceptance
- Vanuatu: ~$7,000; banking score 2/5
- Georgia (NBG): ~€7,000; banking score 3/5; no meaningful capital requirement; good for CIS/MENA markets
- Slovakia (NBS): ~€9,000; €700 gov fee; €5,000 minimum capital — lowest in EU; banking score 3/5
- Bulgaria (NRA): ~€12,000; €1,000 gov fee; €50,000 min capital; banking score 4/5
- Poland (KNF/MiCA): ~€12,000; €1,750 gov fee; banking score 4/5; best EU value
- Seychelles (FSA): ~$10,000; banking score 2/5
- Estonia (FIU): ~€15,000; €2,000 gov fee; €100,000 min capital; banking score 5/5; genuine substance required
- Lithuania (VASP/MiCA CASP): ~€20,000; €3,000 gov fee; €125,000 min capital; banking score 5/5

### Six Cost Components Every Budget Must Include
1. Government Fee: $500-€3,000 (usually quoted)
2. Legal & Consulting: $2,000-€15,000 (partially quoted)
3. Company Formation: $500-€3,000 (rarely quoted)
4. Bank Account: $1,000-$20,000+ (never quoted)
5. Office/Substance: $500-€8,000/year (rarely quoted)
6. Annual Compliance: $1,500-€8,000/year (never quoted)

### Hidden Costs Analysis
- Banking rejections for offshore jurisdictions: $500-$2,000 per attempt; typical SVG entity makes 8-10 attempts
- Re-licensing cost pattern: $4,000-7,000 offshore + 18 months maintenance + re-license in EU = 2-3x a proper EU license
- FATF grey-listed jurisdictions create reputational risk that damages client conversion rates

### FINMA/Swiss Context
- FINMA FinTech license: accepts deposits up to CHF 100M; requires crypto-based asset custody capability
- FINMA Guidance 01/2026: New custody rules requiring segregation, prudential supervision equivalence for foreign-held assets
- Swiss Crypto Valley (Zug) remains the premium jurisdiction for high-end crypto businesses but costs far exceed EU budget options

### Poland-Specific (Best Value EU 2026)
- KNF (Komisja Nadzoru Finansowego) registration state fee: approximately €616
- Transitioning to full MiCA CASP authorisation — Polish CASPs gain EU-wide passporting under MiCA
- Fastest EU entry point with strong banking relationships

### Slovakia-Specific
- NBS (Narodna Banka Slovenska) state fee: €3,000 (fixed)
- Minimum share capital: €5,000 — lowest in EU
- MiCA implementation advancing; NBS review process becoming more thorough

### Estonia-Specific
- FIU (Financial Intelligence Unit) supervision
- Post-2022 reforms: genuine substance mandatory (real office + local MLRO)
- e-Residency program; established crypto-banking relationships

## Expert Quotes Used

### Quote 1 (after True Cost section)
"When clients ask me for the 'cheapest' license, I always ask them a different question first: what is the cheapest outcome you can afford? A $4,000 offshore registration that results in six months of failed banking attempts, €20,000 in re-licensing costs, and zero institutional counterparties is not cheap — it is the most expensive option available. In 2026, the Poland KNF registration at €12,000 total year-1 cost delivers more commercial value per euro than any offshore jurisdiction I know of."

### Quote 2 (before False Economy section)
"I have seen this pattern more times than I can count: a founder spends $5,000 on an SVG registration to save money, then spends the next 18 months failing to open a bank account, losing institutional clients, and eventually paying €18,000 to re-license in Estonia. The total cost exceeds €25,000 — more than double what a Poland or Estonia license would have cost from day one. In crypto licensing, optimising for Year 1 cost at the expense of commercial viability is not a financial strategy — it is a false economy."
