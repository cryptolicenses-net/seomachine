## Verified facts by jurisdiction (every claim traces to a source)

### Singapore (MAS)
- DPT services to customers in Singapore regulated under Payment Services Act 2019 (PSA); licensed firms hold Major Payment Institution licence. Regulator: Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS).
  Source: mas.gov.sg PS-G02 Guidelines; Types of Payment Services.
- Digital Token Service Provider (DTSP) regime under Financial Services and Markets Act 2022 (FSM Act), Part 9, effective 30 June 2025. Captures Singapore entities providing digital token services SOLELY to customers OUTSIDE Singapore; MAS has signalled it will license such DTSPs only in extremely limited circumstances.
  Source: mas.gov.sg "MAS Clarifies Regulatory Regime for Digital Token Service Providers" (2025); Guidelines on Licensing for DTSPs.
- 2026: draft stablecoin legislation pending. Source: MAS / signzy guide.

### Hong Kong (SFC)
- VATP regime live since 1 June 2023. Dual licensing under Securities and Futures Ordinance (Cap. 571, SFO) and Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Ordinance (Cap. 615, AMLO). Regulator: Securities and Futures Commission (SFC).
- Retail access permitted, subject to investor protection measures.
- As of early/Feb 2026: 13 SFC-licensed VATPs (incl. OSL, HashKey).
  Source: sfc.hk; Davis Polk; Fintech Hong Kong (2026); Slaughter and May.
- April 2026: SFC circular permitting secondary trading of tokenised SFC-authorised investment products on licensed VATPs.

### Japan (FSA)
- Currently crypto-asset exchange service providers register under the Payment Services Act (PSA); self-regulatory body = Japan Virtual and Crypto Assets Exchange Association (JVCEA). Regulator: Financial Services Agency (FSA).
- Major shift: FSA proposes moving crypto-asset regulation from PSA to the Financial Instruments and Exchange Act (FIEA), treating crypto like securities; bill greenlit 2026. Stablecoins remain under PSA; NFTs excluded.
- PSA amendments take effect 13 June 2026. Penalties for unlicensed operators raised (prison up to 10 yrs; fines up to ¥10m).
  Source: globallegalinsights.com Japan 2026; CoinGeek; BeInCrypto; Baker McKenzie.

### South Korea (FSC)
- Act on the Protection of Virtual Asset Users (VAUPA) promulgated 18 July 2023, in force 19 July 2024. Regulator: Financial Services Commission (FSC).
- Requires segregation of customer funds, >=80% of customer virtual assets in cold storage, market-abuse/unfair-trading prohibitions, suspicious-activity monitoring.
- Feb 2025: FSC announced plans to gradually allow institutional participation.
  Source: fsc.go.kr; Library of Congress; Lexology.

### Australia (ASIC + AUSTRAC)
- Dual-regulator model: AUSTRAC (AML/CTF, DCE/VASP registration) + ASIC (consumer protection, AFSL). AUSTRAC DCE registration mandatory.
- FATF Travel Rule effective 31 March 2026 (legislated via AML/CTF Amendment Act 2024). New AML/CTF obligations for newly regulated services take effect 1 July 2026; "DCE" terminology being replaced by "VASP".
- Digital Assets Framework / ASIC framework commences ~9 April 2027 after 18-month transition; platforms above thresholds need AFSL.
  Source: Transak 2026 guide; Chainalysis; KuCoin; Squire Patton Boggs.

### India / Thailand / Malaysia (brief)
- India: no comprehensive licensing law; 30% flat tax on VDA gains + 1% TDS. From April 2025 SEBI oversees crypto tokens behaving like securities; framework discussions ongoing (Budget 2026-27). Source: globallegalinsights India 2026; coindcx.
- Thailand: SEC regulates under the Digital Asset Business Decree; 2025 amendment extended licensing to offshore operators serving Thai persons; THB-stablecoin framework in progress. Source: Chambers Fintech 2026 Thailand.
- Malaysia: trading, issuance and safekeeping of digital assets regulated by the Securities Commission (SC) Malaysia (Capital Markets and Services Act; DAX operators). Source: sc.com.my Digital Assets.
