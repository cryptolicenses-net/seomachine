## Key facts (every figure traced to source)

### The legal stack
Swiss crypto regulation rests on: Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA, SR 955.0), Banking Act (SR 952.0, incl. Art. 1b FinTech licence), Financial Market Infrastructure Act (FMIA/FinMIA), Collective Investment Schemes Act (CISA), and the DLT legislative package (2021). [Global Legal Insights Switzerland 2026; FINMA]

### Route 1 — SRO membership (AMLA financial intermediary)
- Entry route for pure AML-subject activity: exchanges, brokers, OTC desks, payment processors, certain wallet providers treated as financial intermediaries under AMLA (SR 955.0). [gofaizen-sherle; key2law]
- SRO membership is NOT a FINMA licence; it is fulfilment of the AML obligation under AMLA. FINMA-recognised SROs supervise members for AML/CFT. [geckocompliance; Global Law Experts]
- Switzerland has 11 FINMA-recognised SROs (e.g. VQF, PolyReg, SO-FIT). [search summary]
- Does NOT permit taking deposits or holding client assets in a banking sense — only intermediary activity within AML scope.

### Route 2 — FinTech licence (Banking Act Art. 1b)
- Allows acceptance of public deposits up to CHF 100 million (or crypto-based assets), provided they are NOT invested and NO interest is paid. [FINMA finma.ch/en/authorisation/fintech/fintech-bewilligung]
- Funds may not bear interest or be used for lending — prohibition of active banking business (no maturity transformation). [FINMA]
- Applicant must be AG, corporation with unlimited partners, or GmbH, registered office + business activity in Switzerland. [FINMA, Art. 1b Banking Act]
- In bankruptcy: client assets are neither privileged nor protected by deposit protection; clients must be informed. [FINMA]

### Route 3 — DLT trading facility licence (DLT Act, FMIA)
- Created by DLT legislative package; FMIA amendments entered into force 1 August 2021. [globalcompliancenews; sif.admin.ch]
- Permits multilateral trading of DLT securities (also admits retail participants directly, unlike traditional exchanges).
- Minimum capital: CHF 1m if no custody/settlement/clearing; CHF 5m if those services provided. Small DLT trading facilities: reduced to CHF 500,000 (or 5% of DLT securities in custody, min CHF 500,000 with custody). [swisscryptoguide; FINMA]
- First-ever FINMA DLT trading facility licence: BX Digital AG, March 2025. [FINMA news 2025/03/20250318]

### Route 4 — Full banking / securities licence
- Statutory minimum fully paid-in share capital CHF 10 million (Art. 15(1) Banking Ordinance). [GLI Switzerland 2026; FINMA]
- FINMA in practice expects substantially more (CHF 20m+ for niche banks) depending on business model/risk. [lawsupport.ch]
- Required for full banking business: interest-bearing deposits, lending, maturity transformation. Sygnum and SEBA/AMINA obtained Swiss banking licences for crypto.

### Token taxonomy (FINMA ICO guidelines, 16 Feb 2018)
- Payment tokens: synonymous with cryptocurrencies, no further function; subject to AML but NOT treated as securities. [FINMA news 2018/02/20180216]
- Utility tokens: digital access to an app/service; no supervisory approval if fully functional at issue and sole purpose is access (not securities).
- Asset tokens: represent assets / participations / debt / dividend or interest claims; treated as SECURITIES (prospectus + securities-law requirements).
- Hybrid tokens possible; classification by underlying purpose + transferability/tradeability. [FINMA; Lenz & Staehelin]

### 2026 forward look (note for accuracy, not over-claim)
- FinIA reform consultation closed 6 Feb 2026 → proposes replacing FinTech licence with two new FINMA categories: Payment Instrument Institution + Crypto-Institution; entry into force expected ~2027 with 1-year transition. [Deloitte CH; Loyens & Loeff]
- FINMA Guidance 01/2026 on custody of crypto-based assets; CARF (OECD) implementation. [CVJ.CH]
- Frame as upcoming/proposed, NOT current law.

## Route-fit logic (which route for which activity)
- Pure exchange/broker/OTC/payment, no deposit-taking → SRO membership (AML only).
- Wants to accept public funds/crypto deposits (no interest, no lending) up to CHF 100m → FinTech licence.
- Multilateral trading of tokenised securities (DLT securities), incl. retail → DLT trading facility.
- Interest-bearing deposits + lending + full banking → full banking licence.
- Asset-token issuance → securities/prospectus law in addition to chosen route.
