Section 1: The Basics

VARA vs ADGM, Properly Defined

VARA and ADGM are the two headline routes to a UAE crypto license, and confusing them is one of the most common and most expensive mistakes founders make. They are not two flavours of the same regime. They are different regulators, in different Emirates, operating under different legal systems, with different client orientations. The right choice flows from your business model, not from which name you heard first.

VARA, the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority, is the world's first dedicated, standalone virtual asset regulator. It governs crypto across the Emirate of Dubai, with one important carve-out: the Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC), which has its own regulator. ADGM, the Abu Dhabi Global Market, is a financial free zone whose regulator, the Financial Services Regulatory Authority (FSRA), supervises crypto as one part of a broad, English common-law financial-services framework. For the wider context of how these fit the global picture, see our guide on what crypto regulation is and how it works.

Both sit inside the UAE's deliberate push to become a regulated crypto hub, and both are credible, internationally respected regimes. The decision is rarely about quality and almost always about fit: who your customers are, what assets you list, how much capital you can deploy, and which legal framework your counterparties and investors expect to see.

Section 2: VARA

What VARA Is and Who It Suits

VARA was established by Dubai Law No. 4 of 2022 as a standalone authority dedicated entirely to virtual assets. That focus is its defining feature: rather than retrofitting crypto into older financial-services rules, VARA built a purpose-made rulebook with separate compliance, market-conduct, technology, and activity-specific requirements. It regulates virtual assets in and from the Emirate of Dubai, covering the mainland and the commercial free zones, but not the DIFC.

VARA licenses seven distinct virtual asset activities: Advisory, Broker-Dealer, Custody, Exchange, Lending and Borrowing, Management and Investment, and Transfer and Settlement Services. A firm applies for each activity it intends to perform, and capital scales with the breadth of permissions sought. The application path runs from an Initial Disclosure Questionnaire to an Approval to Incorporate, then a provisional permit while the firm builds out, and finally a Full Financial Services Permit once VARA completes its operational readiness assessment. Our dedicated VARA license overview walks through each stage in detail.

Crucially, VARA permits licensed firms to serve both retail and institutional clients, with discretionary conditions for qualified or institutional-only designations where appropriate. That makes it the natural home for consumer-facing exchanges, broker-dealers, and regional businesses that want a mainland Dubai presence and a rulebook written specifically for their sector.

Section 3: ADGM

What ADGM Is and Who It Suits

ADGM is a financial free zone in Abu Dhabi established under UAE federal legislation, and its standout feature is its legal system: it applies English common law directly, with its own ADGM courts. For institutional players, fund managers, and global firms whose investors and counterparties expect a familiar contract and dispute framework, that common-law foundation is a powerful draw. Crypto is regulated here by the FSRA as part of a wider financial-services regime rather than under a crypto-only rulebook.

The FSRA authorises virtual asset activities including broker-dealer, operating a multilateral trading facility (the exchange equivalent), custody, and managing assets, and in 2025 it added a DeFi protocol category and refreshed its broader framework. Capital requirements are generally aligned with those for the equivalent traditional regulated activity, with the higher of base, risk-based, or expense-based capital applying. Our ADGM crypto license overview sets out the category map in full.

ADGM also runs a stricter asset-eligibility regime. Only Accepted Virtual Assets may be used in regulated services, and the framework expressly prohibits privacy tokens, algorithmic stablecoins, and similar technologies. Its institutional credibility was underlined when Binance secured a license under the ADGM framework, a milestone that cemented its reputation as a venue for serious, globally minded operators.

Section 5: Client Base

Retail vs Institutional Positioning

The single most decisive factor in choosing between VARA and ADGM is who you intend to serve. VARA was designed to regulate a full crypto market, retail included, and its rulebook contains detailed market-conduct, marketing, and consumer-protection rules precisely because retail participation is in scope. If your business model depends on onboarding individual users in and from Dubai, VARA is the route built for that reality.

ADGM leans the other way. As a financial free zone under English common law, it has historically attracted institutional firms, asset managers, custodians, and global operators who value the legal certainty and international recognition that common law brings. That orientation is reinforced by its asset-eligibility regime and its profile as the venue where Binance obtained a license under the ADGM framework. Retail-facing models are possible, but the centre of gravity is institutional.

In practice, this means the same business idea can point to different regulators depending on its go-to-market. A consumer exchange app for the region tilts toward VARA; an institutional custody or trading desk serving funds and family offices tilts toward ADGM. Mapping your customer profile first, before comparing capital tables, is the discipline that prevents expensive re-licensing later.

From Our Practice

In our UAE licensing work, the clients who struggle are almost always the ones who picked a regulator on reputation before defining their customer base. We have seen an institutional custody desk start down the VARA retail-conduct track, then have to restructure governance and disclosures when it became clear ADGM's common-law framework was what its fund clients actually required. The reverse happens too: a consumer exchange chases ADGM prestige and finds its retail go-to-market sits more naturally under VARA's bespoke rulebook.

We also see capital underestimated. Both regimes apply the higher of base, risk-based, or expense-based capital, so a firm planning to lean on a low headline base figure can be caught out when expense-based or risk-based capital turns out to bind. We model all three early, alongside the AML and governance build, so the capital number a client commits to is the one the regulator will actually require, not the most flattering line in a comparison table.

Section 6: Capital & Categories

Capital and License Categories

Capital is where the two regimes diverge most concretely. Under VARA, requirements are activity-specific and expressed as the higher of a base figure or a percentage of overheads, plus expense-based liquid capital. Indicative paid-up minimums run from around AED 100,000 for advisory, AED 400,000 to 600,000 for broker-dealer, AED 600,000 for custody, and AED 800,000 to 1,500,000 for exchange services, with the lower figures generally available where the firm uses a VARA-licensed custodian.

ADGM ties its crypto capital to its established financial-services categories. Base capital starts at around US$250,000 for most virtual asset activities, with the binding figure being the higher of base, risk-based, or expense-based capital. In June 2025 the FSRA raised the base capital for custodians to AED 5 million, and exchange operators running a multilateral trading facility must hold capital equivalent to roughly six months of operating expenses. Always check the latest figures, as both regulators update them.

Section 7: Decision

How to Choose Between Them

The decision becomes simple once it is sequenced correctly. The five steps below are the order we run with clients: define the business before comparing regulators, not the other way round. Our team can run this assessment with you through a free consultation and turn it into a costed licensing plan.

1
Define
Map your client base and assets
Decide whether you are retail-facing or institutional, and list the tokens you will offer. Retail and regional tilts toward VARA; institutional and global tilts toward ADGM. Check ADGM asset prohibitions early.
2
Locate
Pick the Emirate and legal framework
Choose mainland Dubai under VARA's dedicated rulebook, or the ADGM free zone in Abu Dhabi under English common law. The legal system your investors and counterparties expect often settles this.
3
Cost
Model the binding capital
Both regimes apply the higher of base, risk-based, or expense-based capital. Model all three for your activities, including the June 2025 AED 5M custodian floor in ADGM, before committing.
4
Apply
Run the authorisation process
VARA: IDQ, Approval to Incorporate, provisional permit, then Full Financial Services Permit, around twelve months. ADGM: FSRA application to in-principle approval to authorisation, roughly four to twelve months by complexity.
5
Operate
Maintain capital and compliance
Both regulators expect ongoing capital maintenance, AML controls, reporting, and supervision fees. Build the compliance function as a permanent operating cost, not a one-off application exercise.
