## Verified facts (every claim traces to a source)

### IFRS (no crypto-specific standard)
- There is **no IFRS standard written specifically for crypto**. Treatment derives from existing standards via the IFRS Interpretations Committee (IFRIC) **June 2019 Agenda Decision "Holdings of Cryptocurrencies."** (ifrs.org)
- IFRIC June 2019 concluded a cryptocurrency holding is **NOT cash** (IAS 7) and **NOT a financial asset** (IAS 32), because it is not a contractual right to receive cash/another financial asset and does not give a right to a fixed/determinable number of currency units. (ifrs.org agenda decision)
- Default: account under **IAS 38 Intangible Assets** (it is separable and is a non-monetary asset). (ifrs.org)
- Exception: if held **for sale in the ordinary course of business**, apply **IAS 2 Inventories**; **commodity broker-traders** may measure at **fair value less costs to sell** with changes in profit or loss (IAS 2 para 3(b)). (ifrs.org)
- Under IAS 38 the entity chooses:
  - **Cost model** — cost less accumulated amortisation and impairment; crypto with indefinite life is not amortised but is impairment-tested; impairment losses to profit or loss, limited reversal.
  - **Revaluation model** — only permitted if an **active market** exists; revaluation **gains go to Other Comprehensive Income (OCI)** (revaluation surplus), not net income; downward revaluations below cost go to P&L. (ifrs.org, PwC)

### US GAAP (post ASU 2023-08)
- **Before** ASU 2023-08: crypto = indefinite-lived intangible asset under **ASC 350**; **cost-less-impairment** model — write down on price drops, **no write-up** on recovery (asymmetric). (Deloitte, Grant Thornton)
- **FASB ASU 2023-08** "Intangibles—Goodwill and Other—Crypto Assets (Subtopic 350-60)" issued **December 13, 2023**. (FASB ASU 2023-08 PDF)
- Requires in-scope crypto measured at **fair value (ASC 820) with changes in net income** each reporting period. Replaces the asymmetric impairment model. Presented separately on balance sheet. (FASB, Grant Thornton, Deloitte)
- **Effective for fiscal years beginning after December 15, 2024**, including interim periods; early adoption permitted; modified retrospective with cumulative-effect adjustment to opening retained earnings. (FASB, Deloitte)
- **Scope — all six conditions:** (1) meets definition of intangible asset; (2) does not provide enforceable rights/claims on goods, services, or other assets; (3) created/resides on a distributed ledger using blockchain or similar; (4) secured through cryptography; (5) fungible; (6) not created/issued by the reporting entity or its related parties. (Grant Thornton)
- **Excluded:** NFTs (not fungible), wrapped tokens / tokens with enforcement rights, the entity's own issued tokens / related-party tokens, internally generated intangibles. Bitcoin and Ether are in scope. (Grant Thornton, PwC, RSM)

### Divergence / what it means
- Result: post-2024, **US GAAP = fair value through net income**; **IFRS default = IAS 38 cost (or OCI revaluation)**. The two frameworks now diverge: US GAAP recognises unrealised gains in net income, IFRS generally does not (gains sit in OCI under revaluation, or are not recognised under cost until sale).
- For a firm reporting under both (e.g. dual-listed), this creates different net income and equity outcomes for the same holdings.
