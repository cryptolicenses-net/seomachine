## Verdict (answer block)
Both are EU member states now operating under MiCA, so a CASP licence from either passports across the EEA. Lithuania is generally the faster, more predictable route to a live MiCA CASP authorisation; Estonia offers the unique 0% corporate tax on retained profits. For speed and regulatory certainty, Lithuania; for tax-efficient reinvestment, Estonia.

## Key verified facts (with sources)

### Lithuania
- Competent authority: **Bank of Lithuania (Lietuvos bankas, lb.lt)**. Law on Markets in Crypto-Assets enacted 11 July 2024, designating Bank of Lithuania as competent authority under MiCA Title V. [Source: ComplyFactor; Coincub; Bank of Lithuania]
- Minimum capital: **€50,000–€125,000** depending on CASP class (Class 1 €50k, Class 2 €125k, Class 3 €150k per MiCA Annex IV). Lithuania pushed the €125,000 figure early. [Source: Coincub, Hacken, MiCA Art. 67 / Annex IV]
- Grandfathering / transition deadline: **31 December 2025** — final deadline; all legacy VASPs had to convert to MiCA CASP or cease. As of 2026 the transition is complete; Bank of Lithuania actively enforcing. [Source: Coincub, COREDO]
- Corporate income tax: **16%** standard from 1 January 2025 (raised from 15%). Dividends 16%. Small companies (<10 employees, <€300k revenue) 6%. [Source: PwC Tax Summaries, Demire, Eurofast]
- Timeline to license: typically ~3–6 months for a well-prepared MiCA CASP application.

### Estonia
- Old regime: FIU (Financial Intelligence Unit, fiu.ee) issued virtual currency service provider (VASP) licences under MLTFPA.
- New regime: **Estonian Financial Supervision Authority (Finantsinspektsioon / FSA, fi.ee)** is sole licensing & supervisory body for MiCA CASPs from **January 2025**. Crypto-Asset Market Act (KAVS / CMA) supplements MiCA nationally. [Source: Hacken, Copla, FSA fi.ee]
- Transition: FIU-issued VASP licences valid only until **1 July 2026** (18-month grandfathering). No automatic conversion; must file FSA CASP application. [Source: Tetra Consultants, Charltons Quantum, Hacken]
- Minimum capital under MiCA: **€50,000 (Class 1)**, **€125,000 (Class 2 — exchange/custody)**, **€150,000 (Class 3 — trading platform)**. [Source: Hacken Estonia, MiCA Annex IV]
- Corporate income tax: **0% on retained/reinvested profits**; **22% on distributed profits** (22/78 of net distribution) from 2025. The earlier 14% preferential rate on regular distributions was abolished in 2025. [Source: PwC Tax Summaries Estonia, EMTA emta.ee, EY Estonia]
- Timeline to license: typically ~6–12 months for FSA CASP authorisation.

### Shared / MiCA
- Both EU member states; MiCA fully applicable for CASPs from **30 December 2024**. CASP licence from either passports across all 30 EEA states. [Source: EUR-Lex MiCA Reg. (EU) 2023/1114]
- EU Transfer of Funds Regulation 2023/1113 (Travel Rule, no de-minimis) applies in both from 30 Dec 2024.

## Note on capital
MiCA harmonises CASP minimum capital across the EU (Annex IV): Class 1 €50k, Class 2 €125k, Class 3 €150k. Therefore post-MiCA, Estonia and Lithuania share the SAME statutory capital floors. The historic divergence (Estonia €100k/€250k under the old FIU regime) is now legacy. The real differentiators in 2026 are: tax, regulator style/speed, and ecosystem. The prompt's "€100k/€250k Estonia" and "€125k Lithuania" figures reflect the OLD national regimes; the guide will state the current MiCA-harmonised position and reference the old figures as legacy.
