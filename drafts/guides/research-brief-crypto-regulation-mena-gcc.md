## Core thesis
The UAE is not one crypto regulator but a layered map of several, each governing a different geographic/legal zone. Founders must pick the regulator that matches where and to whom they operate. The GCC neighbours (Bahrain, Qatar, Saudi) add a wider regional picture.

## The UAE regulator map (every claim sourced)

### 1. SCA → CMA (federal, mainland)
- The federal Securities and Commodities Authority (SCA) was replaced by the **Capital Market Authority (CMA)** effective **1 January 2026** under **Federal Decree-Law No. 32 of 2025** and **No. 33 of 2025 (FDL33)**. CMA is the legal successor to SCA; all references to SCA in legislation now read CMA. (King & Spalding; CMS; Dechert; Cleary Gottlieb; regulationtomorrow.com — Jan/Feb 2026)
- Under FDL33, CMA maintains an **official list of virtual assets** that may be traded in the UAE; no virtual asset can be traded unless it is on the CMA list, registered with CMA, and operated by a CMA-licensed platform. This gives CMA de-facto veto even over VARA-listed assets. (pnyxhill.co; King & Spalding)
- Cabinet Decisions No. 111 and 112 of 2022 (which let SCA delegate VA regulation to local authorities like VARA) remain in effect until repealed/amended. (CMS)
- Federal scope: mainland UAE outside Dubai and outside the financial free zones; extraterritorial reach over activities targeting UAE clients from abroad or from a free zone. (DLA Piper; King & Spalding)
- One-year transitional period to ~**September 2026** for firms to obtain proper licensing / partner / cease. (databirdjournal; pnyxhill.co)
- Source domain for site link: **sca.gov.ae** (federal Securities & Commodities Authority / now CMA).

### 2. VARA — Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (Dubai, onshore, excl. DIFC)
- Dubai's dedicated VA regulator, established 2022 under Dubai Law No. 4 of 2022; world's first tailor-made virtual-asset regime. Regulates all VA activity in/from the Emirate of Dubai EXCEPT the DIFC free zone. (vara.ae; u.ae)
- As of **February 2026, VARA had licensed 507 VASPs**, with combined assets exceeding **USD 25 billion**. (digitaldubai.ai; mco)
- 2026: stricter AML guidelines — firms must update risk profiles at least every 3 months; VARA pushes firms to track FATF blacklists. (news.bitcoin.com; mco)
- Role shift in 2026: VARA increasingly operates as a delegated local licensing authority within CMA's federal framework rather than fully sovereign. (pnyxhill.co)
- Retail-friendly: VARA recommended for retail-facing exchanges, NFT marketplaces, payment processors, GCC-focused startups. (getdefy.co)
- Source domain: **vara.ae**.

### 3. ADGM / FSRA — Abu Dhabi Global Market (free zone, Abu Dhabi)
- The **Financial Services Regulatory Authority (FSRA)** regulates financial services in ADGM, a common-law free zone. Operates a dedicated virtual-asset / "Accepted Virtual Assets" (AVA) framework. (adgm.com; coinbax)
- Updated guidance issued **June 2025** and further amendments **March 2026** — new licence categories, addresses tokenized securities, DeFi protocols. (adgm.com Guidance VER07.100625; kslaw; themiddleeastinsider)
- Positioned for **global institutional firms, custody providers, tokenization platforms, DeFi operators** seeking regulatory certainty. (getdefy.co)
- Source domain: **adgm.com** (FSRA).

### 4. DFSA — Dubai Financial Services Authority (DIFC free zone, Dubai)
- DFSA regulates financial services in the **DIFC** free zone. Introduced **Crypto Token** regulations in **2022**; firms can be licensed to offer financial services involving recognised Crypto Tokens within/from DIFC. (dfsa.ae)
- Updated Crypto Token rules **came into force 12 January 2026**. (dfsa.ae news)
- DFSA focus: investment tokens, securities, institutional. (coinbax; neoslegal)
- Source domain: **dfsa.ae**.

## GCC neighbours

### Bahrain — CBB
- **Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB)** — first GCC country with comprehensive crypto framework. Crypto-Asset (CRA) Module within **Volume 6 (Capital Markets)** of the CBB Rulebook, since **2019**, updated quarterly. (Al Tamimi; legalbison)
- **July 2025**: CBB introduced **Stablecoin Issuance and Offering (SIO) Module** permitting fiat-backed stablecoins (BHD, USD) under approval. (Al Tamimi)
- Source domain: **cbb.gov.bh**.

### Qatar — QFC
- Qatar Financial Centre (QFC) introduced a **Digital Assets Framework** (tokenization, smart-contract recognition), finalized ~Q2 2025; distinct regulatory zone for digital assets. Spot trading of cryptocurrencies still restricted onshore. (Carnegie; medium/danawayo)

### Saudi Arabia — SAMA
- Saudi Central Bank (SAMA) appointed a "Crypto Chief", runs a regulatory sandbox; no specific stablecoin regulation yet, draft guidelines under discussion. Conservative stance. (Carnegie; medium/danawayo)
