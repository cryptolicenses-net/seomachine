# Research Brief: What Is Crypto Regulation? (2026)

## Topic
Global overview of crypto regulation in 2026: definition, the regulatory spectrum from banned to embraced, four main regulatory categories (AML, securities, consumer protection, tax), key frameworks (MiCA, FATF, FCA, MAS, VARA, GENIUS Act), classification of crypto assets, regulatory timeline 2009–2026, and what's coming next.

## Key Facts Used
- 2026 is the "enforcement era" — regulators actively supervising licensed entities, not just approving them
- EU MiCA in full force December 2024: single CASP authorisation for all 27 member states
- US GENIUS Act (2025): first federal stablecoin framework requiring 1:1 reserves and monthly disclosure
- China maintains complete ban on crypto exchanges and trading (since 2021)
- El Salvador: Bitcoin as legal tender since 2021; CNAD licenses digital asset service providers
- FATF Recommendation 15 (VASP standard) covers 200+ jurisdictions
- FINMA Guidance 01/2026 (January 2026): new guidance on crypto custody risks
- Switzerland operates under FINMA supervision; AML obligations under AMLA
- SECO administers Swiss sanctions implementation aligned with international standards
