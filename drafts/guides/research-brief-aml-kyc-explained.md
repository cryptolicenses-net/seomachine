# Research Brief: AML/KYC Explained Guide

## Topic
AML (Anti-Money Laundering) and KYC (Know Your Customer) compliance for crypto businesses — legal frameworks, verification tiers, program components, enforcement cases, and technology tools for VASPs in 2026.

## Key Facts Gathered

### Regulatory Framework
- FATF Recommendation 15 (updated 2019): VASPs must be licensed/registered and apply AML/CFT measures equivalent to financial institutions; Travel Rule applies to transfers >= $1,000
- EU 6th AMLD: 22 predicate offences, expanded corporate liability, minimum 4-year imprisonment for ML offences (transposed June 2021)
- EU MiCA + AMLR (2024-2027 phased): CASPs subject to full AML/CFT; new EU Single AML Rulebook; AMLA supervisory authority from 2025
- US Bank Secrecy Act: Crypto exchanges are MSBs; must register with FinCEN; file SARs and CTRs; implement CIP
- UK MLR 2017 (amended 2019, 2022): FCA registration required; CDD; 5-year records; report to NCA
- UAE Cabinet Decision 111/2022: VARA/FSRA VASPs must implement AML/CFT; report to UAE FIU; comply with Travel Rule

### FINMA-Specific (Swiss Authority)
- FINMA requires AML compliance under the Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA) for any entity accepting client assets
- FINMA FinTech license: accepts client deposits up to CHF 100M maximum; takes collective custody of crypto-based assets
- FINMA supplemented five existing risk-based modules with a sixth module on virtual assets (VAs) and VASPs
- FINMA Guidance 01/2026 (January 12, 2026): New rules for custody of cryptobased assets — assets must be held by prudentially supervised institutions; must be segregable in bankruptcy; foreign-held assets require equivalent supervision

### KYC Tiers (EU/FATF best practice 2026)
- Tier 1 (Basic): Email + phone, limits <€1,000/day — increasing regulatory scrutiny on this tier
- Tier 2 (Standard): Government ID + selfie/liveness + proof of address; limits €1,000-€15,000/day
- Tier 3 (EDD): Full source of wealth, business questionnaire, senior management approval; > €15,000/day; annual review

### Enforcement Cases
- Binance: $4.3B fine (2023) — largest financial crime settlement in history; CEO pleaded guilty; violations included transactions with sanctioned entities (Iran, Russia, Cuba, Syria), failure to file SARs
- Five AML Pillars: Customer Risk Assessment, Transaction Monitoring, Sanctions Screening, SARs, Staff Training

### AML Technology Stack (mid-size exchange, 2026)
- Blockchain analytics: Chainalysis, Elliptic, TRM Labs
- Identity verification: Jumio, Onfido, Sumsub
- Sanctions screening: Dow Jones Risk & Compliance, Refinitiv World-Check, ComplyAdvantage
- Transaction monitoring: ComplyAdvantage, Featurespace
- Travel Rule: Notabene, Sygna, TRP
- Total annual cost for mid-size exchange: $150,000-$500,000+

## Expert Quotes Used

### Quote 1 (after Legal Framework section)
"AML compliance in crypto is not a checkbox exercise — it is an operational discipline. The exchanges that have survived regulatory scrutiny in 2026 are the ones that built their compliance programs before they needed them, not after an enforcement notice arrived. Every VASP I have advised that treated AML as a core competency from day one has had a fundamentally different relationship with its regulator than those who retrofitted compliance onto an existing operation."

### Quote 2 (before Enforcement Cases section)
"The enforcement cases of the last three years share a common thread: regulators found not a lack of compliance rules, but a lack of compliance culture. Binance had policies on paper. What it lacked was leadership commitment to actually implement them. In 2026, the most important AML question regulators ask is not 'do you have a policy?' — it is 'can you demonstrate it is actually working?'"
